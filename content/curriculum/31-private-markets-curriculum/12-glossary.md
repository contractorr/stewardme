# Module 12: Glossary & Quick Reference

A comprehensive glossary of private market fund terms, organized alphabetically with cross-references.

---

## A

**Accredited Investor**
Individual or entity meeting SEC criteria for investing in private funds (income, net worth, or professional requirements).

**Accrued Carried Interest**
Carried interest calculated but not yet distributed to GP. Shown as liability on fund balance sheet and reduces LP NAV. See: [Carried Interest](#c)

**Add-on Acquisition**
Purchase made by a portfolio company (not the fund) to expand the business. Common strategy in buyout funds.

**Advisory Committee**
See: [LPAC](#l)

**Alternative Investments**
Asset classes outside traditional stocks and bonds, including private equity, hedge funds, real estate, infrastructure.

**American Waterfall**
Distribution waterfall calculated deal-by-deal rather than on whole-fund basis. GP can receive carry before all capital returned. Requires clawback provision. See: [Waterfall](#w), [European Waterfall](#e)

**Anchor Investor**
Large LP committing early in fundraising, often receiving favorable terms in exchange for validation.

---

## B

**Blind Pool**
Fund where specific investments are not known at time of commitment. Most PE funds are blind pools.

**Bridge Financing**
Short-term debt used to bridge timing gaps, often repaid when longer-term financing is arranged.

**Buyout**
Acquisition of controlling stake (typically 51-100%) in an established company. May involve significant leverage.

---

## C

**Called Capital**
See: [Contributed Capital](#c)

**Cap Rate (Capitalization Rate)**
Real estate metric: NOI / Property Value. Lower cap rate = higher valuation.

**Capital Account**
LP's running balance with the fund, tracking contributions, distributions, income, expenses, gains, and carry.

**Capital Call (Drawdown)**
GP's formal request for LP to contribute committed capital. Legally binding with typical 10-day notice.

**Capital Commitment**
Total amount LP contractually agrees to invest over fund life. Legal obligation, not immediate payment.

**Carried Interest (Carry)**
GP's share of fund profits, typically 20%. Only earned after hurdle rate achieved. May be subject to clawback.

**Catch-Up**
Waterfall provision where GP receives 100% of distributions (after return of capital and hurdle) until GP reaches their target profit share.

**Clawback**
Provision requiring GP to return excess carried interest if fund ultimately underperforms hurdle. Critical in American waterfalls.

**Closed-End Fund**
Fund with fixed life and no redemptions. Investors commit at start and receive distributions as exits occur. All PE funds are closed-end.

**Closing**
Event where LPs formally join fund. First close starts investment period; final close ends fundraising.

**Co-Investment**
Opportunity for LP to invest directly alongside fund in specific deal, typically at reduced or no fees.

**Commitment Period**
See: [Investment Period](#i)

**Comparable Company Analysis (Comps)**
Valuation method using multiples from similar public companies applied to private company.

**Contributed Capital (Paid-In Capital)**
Cumulative capital actually paid by LP to fund. Differs from commitment (promised) amount.

**Control Investment**
Investment where fund has majority ownership (>50%) and governance control.

---

## D

**DCF (Discounted Cash Flow)**
Valuation method calculating present value of projected future cash flows using discount rate.

**Deal-by-Deal**
See: [American Waterfall](#a)

**Default**
LP failure to fund capital call. Triggers severe penalties including forfeiture, forced sale, legal action.

**Direct Alpha**
Performance metric measuring IRR spread versus public market equivalent.

**Distribution**
Cash returned from fund to LP. Includes return of capital, realized gains, income.

**Distribution Notice**
Formal notification from GP to LP of upcoming distribution, including amount and tax character.

**DPI (Distributions to Paid-In)**
Performance metric: Cumulative Distributions / Paid-In Capital. Measures realized return. See: [Module 07](07-performance-metrics.md)

**Drawdown**
See: [Capital Call](#c)

**Dry Powder**
Uncalled capital available for investment. At fund level = unfunded commitments across all LPs.

**Due Diligence**
Investigation process before investment. GP conducts on portfolio companies; LP conducts on GP.

---

## E

**EBITDA**
Earnings Before Interest, Taxes, Depreciation, and Amortization. Key profitability metric used in valuation.

**Enterprise Value (EV)**
Total company value: Equity Value + Net Debt. Used in EV/EBITDA multiples.

**Equalization**
Adjustment for LPs joining after first close to account for investments already made and fees paid.

**European Waterfall**
Distribution waterfall calculated on whole-fund basis. GP receives carry only after all LP capital returned plus hurdle. More LP-friendly than American. See: [Waterfall](#w)

**EV/EBITDA**
Valuation multiple: Enterprise Value / EBITDA. Primary metric for buyout valuations.

**Exit**
Sale or monetization of portfolio investment. Methods include trade sale, secondary sale, IPO, dividend recap.

---

## F

**Fair Value**
Estimated current market value of investment. For Level 3 assets, requires judgment and assumptions.

**Fair Value Hierarchy**
Accounting framework (ASC 820) classifying assets by reliability of valuation inputs:
- Level 1: Quoted market prices
- Level 2: Observable inputs
- Level 3: Unobservable inputs

**Fee Offset**
Reduction to management fee based on fees GP receives from portfolio companies.

**Final Close**
Last opportunity for LPs to join fund. Typically 12-18 months after first close.

**First Close**
Initial closing when fund reaches minimum size to begin investing.

**Follow-on Investment**
Additional investment in existing portfolio company. May occur after investment period.

**Fund Administrator**
Third party providing accounting, reporting, and operational services to fund.

**Fund of Funds (FoF)**
Fund that invests in other private equity funds rather than directly in companies.

**Fund Size**
Total commitments to fund from all partners (LPs + GP).

**Funded Commitment**
See: [Contributed Capital](#c)

---

## G

**General Partner (GP)**
Fund manager with authority over investments and operations. Has unlimited liability.

**GP Commit (GP Commitment)**
Capital committed by GP alongside LPs, typically 1-5% of fund. Demonstrates alignment.

**Gross Returns**
Performance before management fees and carried interest. Shows investment performance.

**Growth Equity**
Investment in established, profitable companies seeking expansion capital. Typically minority stake, low/no leverage.

---

## H

**Hard Cap**
Maximum fund size, cannot be exceeded.

**Harvest Period**
Later fund years focused on value creation and exiting investments. Typically years 6-10.

**Holding Period**
Duration investment is held from acquisition to exit.

**Hurdle Rate (Preferred Return)**
Minimum return LPs must receive before GP earns carried interest. Typically 8% IRR.

---

## I

**Illiquidity Premium**
Extra return investors expect for locking up capital in illiquid investments.

**In-Kind Distribution**
Distribution of securities rather than cash. Rare in PE.

**Infrastructure**
Asset class including essential physical systems: transportation, utilities, energy, communications.

**Investment Period (Commitment Period)**
Initial fund years when new investments are made. Typically 5 years from final close.

**IRR (Internal Rate of Return)**
Annualized return accounting for timing of cash flows. The discount rate making NPV of all cash flows equal zero. See: [Module 07](07-performance-metrics.md)

---

## J

**J-Curve**
Pattern of negative early returns followed by improving returns as fund matures. Caused by fees and investments held at cost. See: [Module 06](06-jcurve-vintage.md)

**Joint and Several Liability**
Each GP partner is individually liable for full clawback obligation.

---

## K

**K-1**
US tax document reporting LP's share of fund income, gains, losses for tax filing.

**Key Person Clause**
Provision suspending investment period if named senior professionals leave or reduce involvement.

**KPI (Key Performance Indicator)**
Metrics used to evaluate portfolio company performance (revenue, EBITDA, growth rates, etc.).

---

## L

**Late-Stage**
Mature companies closer to exit, typically revenue-generating with proven business model.

**Level 3 Assets**
Investments valued using unobservable inputs (models, estimates). Most PE investments are Level 3.

**Leverage**
Use of debt to amplify returns. Measured as Debt/EBITDA or LTV.

**Limited Partner (LP)**
Passive investor in fund. Provides capital but no control over investments. Limited liability.

**Limited Partnership Agreement (LPA)**
Legal document governing fund terms, including fees, waterfall, rights, and obligations.

**Liquidation**
Wind-down of fund at end of term. All assets sold and proceeds distributed.

**LPA**
See: [Limited Partnership Agreement](#l)

**LPAC (LP Advisory Committee)**
Committee of major LPs providing oversight on conflicts, valuations, and certain approvals.

**LTV (Loan-to-Value)**
Leverage ratio: Debt / Asset Value. Higher LTV = more leverage.

---

## M

**Management Company**
Separate entity employing investment professionals and receiving management fees. Manages multiple funds.

**Management Fee**
Annual fee to GP for managing fund. Typically 1.5-2% on committed capital, stepping down post-investment period.

**Mark-to-Market**
Updating investment value to reflect current fair value.

**Mezzanine**
Subordinated debt, typically with equity features (warrants). Higher yield than senior debt.

**MFN (Most Favored Nation)**
Side letter clause giving LP right to receive any better terms granted to other LPs.

**Milestone**
Significant achievement used to evaluate progress (product launch, revenue target, etc.).

**Minority Investment**
Investment where fund owns less than 50% and does not have control.

**MOIC (Multiple on Invested Capital)**
See: [TVPI](#t)

---

## N

**NAV (Net Asset Value)**
Fund's total assets minus liabilities. LP's NAV = their proportionate share.

**Net Returns**
Performance after management fees and carried interest. What LP actually receives.

**NOI (Net Operating Income)**
Real estate metric: Revenue minus operating expenses. Does not include debt service.

**No-Fault Divorce**
LPA provision allowing LP supermajority to remove GP without cause.

---

## O

**Open-End Fund**
Fund allowing ongoing subscriptions and redemptions. Common in real estate, rare in PE.

**Organizational Expenses**
One-time fund formation costs (legal, accounting, filing). Typically capped.

**Over-Commitment Strategy**
LP committing more than available capital, relying on distributions to fund calls.

---

## P

**Paid-In Capital**
See: [Contributed Capital](#c)

**Pari Passu**
"On equal footing" - distributions or rights shared equally among parties.

**Partnership Expenses**
Ongoing fund operating costs (legal, audit, admin) charged to fund, not management company.

**Placement Agent**
Intermediary helping GP raise capital from LPs. Paid commission on capital raised.

**Platform Company**
Initial investment that serves as base for add-on acquisitions (buy-and-build strategy).

**PME (Public Market Equivalent)**
Benchmarking methodology comparing PE returns to public market investment with same cash flows.

**Portfolio Company**
Company owned by the fund.

**Post-Money Valuation**
Company valuation immediately after new investment: Pre-Money + Investment Amount.

**PPM (Private Placement Memorandum)**
Marketing document provided to prospective LPs describing fund strategy, terms, risks.

**Pre-Money Valuation**
Company valuation before new investment.

**Preferred Return**
See: [Hurdle Rate](#h)

**Primary Investment**
LP investment in new fund during fundraising (vs. secondary).

**Private Credit**
Non-bank lending to companies, including direct lending, mezzanine, distressed.

**Private Equity**
Broad term for private ownership investments. Includes buyout, growth, venture capital.

**Pro Rata**
Proportional to ownership percentage.

---

## Q

**Quartile Ranking**
Performance ranking within vintage/strategy peer group:
- 1st Quartile: Top 25%
- 2nd Quartile: 50-75th percentile
- 3rd Quartile: 25-50th percentile
- 4th Quartile: Bottom 25%

---

## R

**Realized Gain/Loss**
Profit or loss from investment that has been sold. Crystallized, certain.

**Recallable Distribution**
Distribution that GP can call back for future investments. Typically return of capital only.

**Recapitalization (Recap)**
Restructuring company's capital, often replacing equity with debt to return capital to investors.

**Residual Value**
Current estimated value of remaining investments. See: [RVPI](#r)

**Return of Capital**
Distribution returning LP's original investment, not profit. First tier of waterfall.

**RVPI (Residual Value to Paid-In)**
Performance metric: Current NAV / Paid-In Capital. Measures unrealized return.

---

## S

**Secondary**
Purchase of existing fund interest (LP secondary) or portfolio company (GP secondary) from another party.

**Seed Stage**
Earliest venture investment stage, often pre-revenue with concept/prototype only.

**Senior Debt**
Debt with first priority claim in liquidation. Lower risk, lower return than subordinated debt.

**Series A/B/C**
Sequential venture capital funding rounds, each typically at higher valuation.

**Side Letter**
Agreement between GP and specific LP granting special terms (fee discounts, MFN, reporting).

**Sponsor**
Another term for GP or private equity firm.

**Step-Down**
Reduction in management fee rate, typically when investment period ends.

**Subscription Agreement**
Document signed by LP to join fund, confirming commitment and representations.

**Subscription Line (Sub Line)**
Credit facility allowing fund to borrow against LP commitments. Used to bridge capital calls.

---

## T

**Target Fund Size**
GP's goal for total commitments. May differ from hard cap.

**Term**
Fund duration, typically 10 years plus extensions.

**Top Quartile**
Funds ranking in top 25% of peer group by performance.

**Trade Sale**
Exit via sale to strategic buyer (operating company, not financial sponsor).

**Transaction Fee**
Fee paid by portfolio company to GP for acquisition services. Often offsets management fee.

**TVPI (Total Value to Paid-In)**
Performance metric: (Distributions + NAV) / Paid-In Capital. Total return multiple. Also called MOIC.

---

## U

**UBTI (Unrelated Business Taxable Income)**
Tax concept affecting tax-exempt LPs. Certain income (debt-financed) may be taxable.

**Unfunded Commitment**
Remaining capital LP is obligated to pay when called: Commitment - Called + Recallable.

**Unrealized Gain/Loss**
Change in value of investment still held. Paper gain/loss, subject to change.

---

## V

**Valuation**
Process of estimating fair value of investment. See: [Module 08](08-nav-valuation.md)

**Value-Add**
Investment strategy requiring active improvement (real estate renovation, operational turnaround).

**Venture Capital (VC)**
Investment in early-stage, high-growth-potential startups. Higher risk/return than buyout.

**Vintage Year**
Year fund begins investing. Primary categorization for benchmarking.

---

## W

**WALT (Weighted Average Lease Term)**
Real estate metric measuring average remaining lease duration, weighted by rent.

**Waterfall**
Order and allocation of distributions between LPs and GP. See: [Module 10](10-waterfall-carry.md)

**Whole Fund**
See: [European Waterfall](#e)

**Write-Down**
Reduction in investment's carrying value due to impairment.

**Write-Off**
Complete elimination of investment value (marked to zero).

---

## Regulatory & Compliance Terms

**AIFMD (Alternative Investment Fund Managers Directive)**
EU regulation governing managers of alternative investment funds. Requires registration, reporting (Annex IV), and investor disclosures.

**CRS (Common Reporting Standard)**
OECD framework for automatic exchange of financial account information between tax authorities.

**EDCI (ESG Data Convergence Initiative)**
Industry-led effort to standardize ESG metrics for private equity, enabling LP aggregation and benchmarking.

**ERISA (Employee Retirement Income Security Act)**
US law governing private pension plans. Establishes fiduciary duties, prohibited transactions, and reporting requirements.

**FATCA (Foreign Account Tax Compliance Act)**
US law requiring foreign financial institutions to report US account holders to IRS.

**Form PF**
SEC/CFTC reporting form for US-registered private fund advisers. Large PE advisers file quarterly.

**GIPS (Global Investment Performance Standards)**
Voluntary ethical standards for calculating and presenting investment performance. Created by CFA Institute. See: [Module 13](13-regulatory-compliance.md)

**PAI (Principal Adverse Impact)**
SFDR term for negative effects investments have on sustainability factors. 18 mandatory indicators for companies.

**PRI (Principles for Responsible Investment)**
UN-supported network promoting ESG incorporation. Signatories commit to annual transparency reporting.

**SFDR (Sustainable Finance Disclosure Regulation)**
EU regulation requiring sustainability disclosures. Classifies funds as Article 6, 8, or 9.

**Solvency II**
EU prudential regulation for insurance companies. Requires capital charges and look-through reporting for investments.

**TCFD (Task Force on Climate-related Financial Disclosures)**
Framework for climate risk and opportunity disclosure. Four pillars: Governance, Strategy, Risk Management, Metrics.

## Quick Reference Formulas

```
PERFORMANCE METRICS
───────────────────
TVPI = (Distributions + NAV) / Paid-In Capital
DPI  = Distributions / Paid-In Capital
RVPI = NAV / Paid-In Capital
TVPI = DPI + RVPI

IRR = Rate where NPV of all cash flows = 0

PME = Fund TVPI / Public Market Equivalent TVPI

CAPITAL METRICS
───────────────
Unfunded = Commitment - Called + Recallable
NAV = Assets - Liabilities
LP NAV = Fund NAV × LP Ownership %

VALUATION
─────────
Enterprise Value = Equity Value + Net Debt
EV/EBITDA = Enterprise Value / EBITDA
Cap Rate = NOI / Property Value

LEVERAGE
────────
Net Debt = Total Debt - Cash
Net Debt/EBITDA = Net Debt / EBITDA
LTV = Debt / Asset Value
```
