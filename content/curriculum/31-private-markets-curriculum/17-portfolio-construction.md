### Commitment vs Exposure vs NAV

```
KEY CONCEPTS
в”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җ

COMMITMENT:
в”ңв”Җв”Җ Total amount promised to fund
в”ңв”Җв”Җ Legal obligation
в””в”Җв”Җ Does NOT equal actual investment

FUNDED/CALLED:
в”ңв”Җв”Җ Capital actually invested
в”ңв”Җв”Җ Drawn from commitment
в””в”Җв”Җ Creates "exposure"

UNFUNDED:
в”ңв”Җв”Җ Remaining commitment not yet called
в”ңв”Җв”Җ Future obligation
в””в”Җв”Җ "Dry powder"

EXPOSURE (NAV):
в”ңв”Җв”Җ Current market value
в”ңв”Җв”Җ Funded minus distributions plus/minus gains
в””в”Җв”Җ What you actually "own"

EXAMPLE:
в”Ңв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”җ
в”Ӯ Fund Commitment:      $100M            в”Ӯ
в”Ӯ Called to date:       $70M             в”Ӯ
в”Ӯ Unfunded:            $30M              в”Ӯ
в”Ӯ Distributions:        $25M             в”Ӯ
в”Ӯ Current NAV:          $80M             в”Ӯ
в”Ӯ                                        в”Ӯ
в”Ӯ Exposure = $80M (NAV)                  в”Ӯ
в”Ӯ NOT $100M (commitment)                 в”Ӯ
в”Ӯ NOT $70M (called)                      в”Ӯ
в””в”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”ҳ
```

## 17.2 Pacing Models

### What is a Pacing Model?

A **pacing model** forecasts how much to commit each year to achieve and maintain a target allocation.

```
PACING MODEL COMPONENTS
в”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җ

INPUTS:                          OUTPUTS:
в”ңв”Җв”Җ Current PE NAV               в”ңв”Җв”Җ Annual commitment targets
в”ңв”Җв”Җ Unfunded commitments         в”ңв”Җв”Җ Expected NAV trajectory
в”ңв”Җв”Җ Total portfolio size         в”ңв”Җв”Җ Expected cash flows
в”ңв”Җв”Җ Target PE allocation         в”ңв”Җв”Җ Allocation forecast
в”ңв”Җв”Җ Expected returns             в””в”Җв”Җ Overcommitment ratio
в”ңв”Җв”Җ Expected cash flows
в””в”Җв”Җ Commitment period

BASIC FORMULA:
в”Ңв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”җ
в”Ӯ                                                         в”Ӯ
в”Ӯ New Commitments = f(Target NAV - Projected NAV)        в”Ӯ
в”Ӯ                                                         в”Ӯ
в”Ӯ Where Projected NAV =                                   в”Ӯ
в”Ӯ   Current NAV                                           в”Ӯ
в”Ӯ   + Expected calls (from unfunded)                     в”Ӯ
в”Ӯ   + Expected appreciation                               в”Ӯ
в”Ӯ   - Expected distributions                              в”Ӯ
в”Ӯ                                                         в”Ӯ
в””в”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”ҳ

```

```
Because PE money isn't called immediately:
в”ңв”Җв”Җ Must commit MORE than target allocation
в”ңв”Җв”Җ "Overcommit" to reach actual exposure
в””в”Җв”Җ Ratio depends on pacing assumptions

OVERCOMMITMENT RATIO:
                    Total Unfunded Commitments

                    Target NAV Allocation

в”ңв”Җв”Җ Mature program: 1.0-1.3x
в”ңв”Җв”Җ Aggressive growth: 2.0-2.5x
```

### Rebalancing Strategies

```
REBALANCING APPROACHES
в”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җв”Җ

1. COMMITMENT-BASED REBALANCING
   в””в”Җв”Җ Adjust new commitment pace
   в””в”Җв”Җ Slow and indirect
   в””в”Җв”Җ Lowest cost

   Example: Overweight by 3%
   в”ңв”Җв”Җ Reduce annual commitments by 30%
   в””в”Җв”Җ Takes 2-3 years to normalize

2. SECONDARY MARKET REBALANCING
   в””в”Җв”Җ Buy/sell existing positions
   в””в”Җв”Җ Faster but costly
   в””в”Җв”Җ Market timing risk

   Example: Sell $30M at 10% discount
   в”ңв”Җв”Җ Immediate exposure reduction
   в””в”Җв”Җ Cost: $3M in discount

3. PUBLIC MARKET OFFSET
   в””в”Җв”Җ Adjust public allocations
   в””в”Җв”Җ Use public equity to manage total equity beta
   в””в”Җв”Җ Indirect but practical

   Example: PE 3% overweight
   в”ңв”Җв”Җ Reduce public equity by 3%
   в””в”Җв”Җ Total equity exposure maintained

4. DERIVATIVE OVERLAY (Advanced)
   в””в”Җв”Җ Use futures/swaps on public equity
   в””в”Җв”Җ Hedge PE market exposure
   в””в”Җв”Җ Complex, requires expertise
```

## Knowledge Check

1. Why must investors "overcommit" to reach their target PE allocation?
2. What are the key inputs to a pacing model?
3. Why is vintage diversification important?
4. How does the denominator effect impact PE allocations?
5. What are the options for rebalancing an overweight PE position?

<details>
<summary>Answers</summary>

1. Because PE capital is called gradually over 3-5 years, committing only the target amount results in actual exposure well below target. Overcommitting (typically 1.5-2x target) accounts for the delay between commitment and deployment, allowing actual NAV to reach the target level.

2. Key inputs: current PE NAV, unfunded commitments, total portfolio size, target allocation, expected contribution rates, expected distribution rates, expected appreciation rates, and time horizon.

3. Vintage year returns vary dramatically based on market entry/exit timing. Diversifying across vintages reduces the impact of committing heavily in a poor vintage year and smooths overall program returns.

4. When public markets decline, PE NAV typically doesn't adjust immediately (lag effect), causing PE allocation to rise as a percentage of a smaller total portfolio. This can push allocations above targets without any action by the LP.

5. Options include: (1) reduce new commitments (slow, no cost), (2) sell positions in secondary market (faster, 10-20% discount), (3) wait for natural distributions (no cost, uncertain timing), (4) offset via public market allocations.

</details>
