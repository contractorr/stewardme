## 21.1 The Negotiation Landscape

### GP vs LP Dynamics

```
NEGOTIATION POWER DYNAMICS
──────────────────────────

GP LEVERAGE (Strong Position):
├── Hot fund / oversubscribed
├── Top-quartile track record
├── Limited capacity
├── Many LPs seeking access
├── First-time but hot team
└── Result: "Take it or leave it" terms

LP LEVERAGE (Strong Position):
├── Large commitment size
├── Early/anchor investor
├── Strategic value (brand, co-invest)
├── First-time fund needs credibility
├── Challenged fundraise
└── Result: Negotiable terms

TYPICAL NEGOTIATION SPECTRUM:
┌─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┐
│                                                                 │
│ ◄─── GP-Friendly ────────────────────── LP-Friendly ───►       │
│                                                                 │
│ Oversubscribed    Balanced         Challenged      First-time  │
│ top GP            fundraise        fundraise       GP needs    │
│ "No changes"      Some changes     More changes    anchor LP   │
│                                                                 │
└─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┘
```

### What's Negotiable?

```
NEGOTIATION AREAS
─────────────────

IN THE LPA (All LPs):
├── Management fee (rate, basis, step-down)
├── Carried interest (rate, hurdle, catch-up)
├── Expenses (cap, types, disclosure)
├── Fund term and extensions
├── Investment restrictions
├── Key person provisions
├── LPAC composition
└── Removal rights

IN SIDE LETTERS (Individual LPs):
├── Fee discounts
├── Co-investment rights
├── Reporting enhancements
├── Excuse rights
├── Most favored nation (MFN)
├── Capacity rights (future funds)
├── Regulatory accommodations
└── Transfer rights

GENERALLY NOT NEGOTIABLE:
├── Core economic waterfall structure
├── Fundamental investment strategy
├── GP's fiduciary duties
├── Basic LP rights
└── Regulatory requirements
```

## 21.2 Side Letters

### What Are Side Letters?

```
SIDE LETTER BASICS
──────────────────

DEFINITION:
├── Separate agreement between GP and individual LP
├── Modifies or supplements LPA terms
├── Binding only on signatory LP
├── Confidential between parties
└── Creates "two-tier" LP structure

WHY THEY EXIST:
├── LPA is one-size-fits-all
├── Large LPs have leverage
├── Regulatory requirements differ
├── Strategic LPs get benefits
└── Custom accommodations needed

TYPICAL PROCESS:
┌─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┐
│                                                                 │
│ 1. GP circulates draft LPA to all prospective LPs              │
│                                                                 │
│ 2. Large LPs request side letter negotiations                  │
│                                                                 │
│ 3. GP and LP negotiate specific provisions                     │
│                                                                 │
│ 4. Side letter executed alongside LP subscription              │
│                                                                 │
│ 5. Other LPs may request MFN (most favored nation) rights     │
│                                                                 │
│ 6. MFN LPs can elect to receive certain side letter benefits   │
│                                                                 │
└─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┘
```

### Common Side Letter Provisions

```
SIDE LETTER PROVISION CATEGORIES
────────────────────────────────

1. ECONOMIC TERMS
   ├── Management fee discount
   ├── Carry reduction
   ├── Fee offset arrangements
   └── Expense caps

2. CO-INVESTMENT
   ├── Pro-rata co-investment rights
   ├── Priority co-investment
   ├── Minimum allocation
   └── Exclusivity on certain deals

3. REPORTING
   ├── Enhanced quarterly reporting
   ├── Portfolio company access
   ├── Annual meeting attendance
   └── Custom data formats

4. GOVERNANCE
   ├── LPAC seat
   ├── Consent rights
   ├── Advisory board membership
   └── Information rights

5. REGULATORY
   ├── ERISA compliance
   ├── UBTI limitations
   ├── VCOC/REOC qualification
   └── FOIA considerations

6. TRANSFER & LIQUIDITY
   ├── Enhanced transfer rights
   ├── Secondary sale provisions
   └── Early redemption rights

7. MFN
   ├── Right to receive other LP benefits
   └── Notification of side letters
```

### Side Letter Example

```
SAMPLE SIDE LETTER PROVISIONS
─────────────────────────────

ABC Partners Fund VIII, L.P.
Side Letter with Midwest Pension Fund ($75M commitment)

1. MANAGEMENT FEE REDUCTION
   "Notwithstanding Section 6.1 of the LPA, the Limited
   Partner shall pay a management fee equal to 1.75%
   (rather than 2.0%) of Committed Capital during the
   Investment Period and 1.25% (rather than 1.5%) of
   Invested Capital thereafter."

   VALUE: ~$1.9M savings over fund life

2. CO-INVESTMENT RIGHTS
   "The General Partner shall offer the Limited Partner
   the opportunity to participate in co-investment
   opportunities on a pro-rata basis (based on the LP's
   percentage commitment) before offering such
   opportunities to non-LP co-investors."

3. LPAC SEAT
   "The Limited Partner shall be entitled to designate
   one representative to serve on the Limited Partner
   Advisory Committee."

4. REPORTING
   "The General Partner shall provide the Limited Partner
   with quarterly portfolio company operating metrics
   in Excel format within 60 days of quarter end."

5. MFN
   "The Limited Partner shall be entitled to receive the
   benefit of any more favorable economic or governance
   terms granted to any other Limited Partner, subject
   to certain exceptions..."
```

## 21.3 Most Favored Nation (MFN) Clauses

### How MFN Works

```
MFN MECHANICS
─────────────

CONCEPT:
├── LP gets right to receive benefits given to other LPs
├── Levels playing field
├── Prevents hidden deals
└── Creates transparency

TYPICAL MFN PROVISION:
┌─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┐
│                                                                 │
│ "If the General Partner grants to any Limited Partner any      │
│ rights, benefits, or privileges more favorable than those      │
│ contained in this Agreement or side letter, the General        │
│ Partner shall notify the Limited Partner of such terms and     │
│ the Limited Partner shall have the right to elect to receive   │
│ the benefit of any or all such more favorable terms."          │
│                                                                 │
└─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┘

PROCESS:
1. GP grants side letter to LP-A with 25bp fee discount
2. GP must notify MFN-holders of this term
3. MFN-holders elect whether to receive same discount
4. Those electing receive amended side letter
```

### MFN Limitations

```
MFN CARVE-OUTS (Common Exceptions)
──────────────────────────────────

TYPICALLY EXCLUDED FROM MFN:

1. SIZE-BASED BENEFITS
   └── Fee discounts tied to commitment size
   └── LP-A gets 25bp discount for $100M+
   └── LP-B with $25M can't claim via MFN

2. REGULATORY ACCOMMODATIONS
   └── ERISA-specific provisions
   └── Tax-exempt UBTI protections
   └── Foreign investor accommodations
   └── Not relevant to other LP types

3. AFFILIATE ARRANGEMENTS
   └── GP affiliate/employee terms
   └── Strategic partner arrangements
   └── Founders/anchor economics

4. OPERATIONAL PROVISIONS
   └── Wire instructions
   └── Notice addresses
   └── Administrative items

5. CONFIDENTIALITY
   └── Some terms remain undisclosed
   └── GP may aggregate/summarize

MFN EXERCISE:
┌─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┐
│                                                                 │
│ GP notifies MFN-holders: "The following terms have been        │
│ granted to other LPs:                                          │
│                                                                 │
│ A. Management fee discount of 25 bps (commitment >$100M)       │
│ B. LPAC seat (commitment >$50M)                                │
│ C. Enhanced quarterly reporting                                 │
│ D. Priority co-investment rights (commitment >$75M)            │
│                                                                 │
│ Please elect which terms you wish to receive (subject to       │
│ applicable commitment thresholds) within 30 days."             │
│                                                                 │
└─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┘
```

## 21.4 Co-Investment Rights

### Types of Co-Investment Rights

```
CO-INVESTMENT RIGHT STRUCTURES
──────────────────────────────

1. PRO-RATA RIGHTS
   └── LP can invest alongside fund
   └── Amount proportional to fund commitment
   └── Example: 5% of fund = 5% of co-invest

2. PRIORITY RIGHTS
   └── First right to co-invest before others
   └── Not guaranteed allocation
   └── Position in queue matters

3. GUARANTEED ALLOCATION
   └── Specific $ or % guaranteed
   └── Strongest form of co-invest right
   └── Rare, requires significant leverage

4. BEST EFFORTS
   └── GP will try to offer opportunities
   └── No guarantee
   └── Most common provision

ALLOCATION MECHANICS:
┌─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┐
│                                                                 │
│ Deal: $500M equity check                                        │
│ Fund investment: $100M                                          │
│ Co-investment available: $400M                                  │
│                                                                 │
│ ALLOCATION WATERFALL:                                           │
│ 1. Guaranteed allocations: $100M to Anchor LP                  │
│ 2. Priority rights (pro-rata): $150M to priority LPs           │
│ 3. Standard pro-rata: $100M to other LPs                       │
│ 4. Third parties: $50M to non-LP co-investors                  │
│                                                                 │
│ Note: Many LPs decline co-invest (capacity, timing)            │
│       GP often has more demand than supply                     │
│                                                                 │
└─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┘
```

### Co-Investment Considerations

```
CO-INVESTMENT PROS AND CONS
───────────────────────────

FOR LPs:

PROS:
├── No management fee
├── No (or reduced) carried interest
├── More exposure to best deals
├── Relationship building with GP
├── Direct investment experience
└── Lower blended fee on PE program

CONS:
├── Concentration risk (single asset)
├── Selection bias (GP keeps best?)
├── Adverse selection risk
├── Operational burden (diligence)
├── Staffing requirements
├── Fast timeline pressure
└── Winner's curse

FOR GPs:

PROS:
├── Access larger deals
├── LP relationship deepening
├── Reduce fund concentration
├── Retain dry powder
└── Competitive advantage in deals

CONS:
├── Administrative burden
├── Allocation fairness questions
├── LP relations if deal fails
├── Disclosure complexity
└── LPAC oversight

CO-INVEST ECONOMICS:
┌─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┐
│ Fund Investment: $50M (2% mgmt fee, 20% carry)                 │
│ Co-Investment: $50M (0% fee, 0% carry)                         │
│                                                                 │
│ Exit at 2.0x:                                                   │
│ Fund return: $100M - fees/carry = ~$85M net to LP              │
│ Co-invest return: $100M = $100M net to LP                      │
│                                                                 │
│ Blended: $185M on $100M = 1.85x (vs 1.70x fund-only)          │
└─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┘
```

## 21.5 LPAC Governance

### What is the LPAC?

```
LPAC BASICS
───────────

LIMITED PARTNER ADVISORY COMMITTEE

PURPOSE:
├── Represent LP interests
├── Approve conflicts of interest
├── Consent to certain GP actions
├── Provide feedback to GP
└── NOT a fiduciary body (usually)

COMPOSITION:
├── Typically 5-10 members
├── Largest LPs usually represented
├── Sometimes includes independent member
├── Rotating terms possible
└── GP as non-voting observer

FORMATION:
├── Established in LPA
├── Members designated in side letters
├── Or elected by LP vote
└── Constituted at final close
```

### LPAC Responsibilities

```
LPAC APPROVAL MATTERS
─────────────────────

COMMON LPAC CONSENT ITEMS:

1. CONFLICTS OF INTEREST
   ├── GP affiliate transactions
   ├── Cross-fund investments
   ├── Principal transactions
   └── Related party dealings

2. VALUATION
   ├── Material valuation disputes
   ├── Write-downs/write-ups review
   └── Valuation methodology changes

3. FUND TERMS
   ├── Term extensions
   ├── Investment period extensions
   └── Fee modifications

4. OPERATIONAL
   ├── Key person departure response
   ├── GP removal procedures
   └── Litigation matters

5. INVESTMENT RESTRICTIONS
   ├── Exceeding concentration limits
   ├── Non-conforming investments
   └── Strategy deviations

LPAC PROCESS:
┌─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┐
│                                                                 │
│ 1. GP identifies matter requiring LPAC consent                 │
│                                                                 │
│ 2. GP prepares materials describing matter                     │
│                                                                 │
│ 3. GP distributes to LPAC members (advance notice)            │
│                                                                 │
│ 4. LPAC meets (in person, phone, or written consent)          │
│                                                                 │
│ 5. LPAC votes (typically majority required)                    │
│                                                                 │
│ 6. GP proceeds if approved, modifies if rejected               │
│                                                                 │
│ Typical advance notice: 5-10 business days                     │
│ Quorum: Usually majority of members                            │
│ Approval: Usually majority of quorum                           │
│                                                                 │
└─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┘
```

### LPAC Member Considerations

```
SERVING ON LPAC
───────────────

BENEFITS:
├── Early information access
├── Influence on fund matters
├── Deeper GP relationship
├── Visibility into conflicts
└── Prestige/status

RESPONSIBILITIES:
├── Attend meetings (2-4/year typical)
├── Review materials
├── Vote on matters
├── Maintain confidentiality
└── Act in interest of all LPs (not just own)

CHALLENGES:
├── Time commitment
├── Potential liability concerns
├── Information asymmetry
├── Difficult decisions (GP removal)
└── Pressure from GP

BEST PRACTICES:
┌─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┐
│                                                                 │
│ □ Review all materials before meetings                         │
│ □ Ask questions if unclear                                     │
│ □ Consider all LPs, not just own position                      │
│ □ Document rationale for votes                                 │
│ □ Consult legal counsel if uncertain                          │
│ □ Request indemnification from fund                           │
│ □ Maintain confidentiality                                     │
│                                                                 │
└─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┘
```

## 21.6 Key Person Provisions

### What Are Key Person Provisions?

```
KEY PERSON BASICS
─────────────────

PURPOSE:
├── Protect LPs from team departures
├── Ensure continuity of management
├── Trigger for investment pause
└── Ultimate protection: GP removal

TYPICAL KEY PERSONS:
├── Founding partners
├── Senior investment professionals
├── Those with significant track record
└── Usually 2-5 individuals named

TRIGGER EVENTS:
┌─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┐
│                                                                 │
│ KEY PERSON EVENT occurs when:                                   │
│                                                                 │
│ □ Named key person dies or is disabled                         │
│ □ Named key person leaves the firm                             │
│ □ Named key person reduces time commitment below threshold     │
│   (e.g., <50% of business time)                                │
│ □ Combination of key persons no longer at firm                 │
│   (e.g., 2 of 4 must remain)                                   │
│                                                                 │
└─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┘
```

### Key Person Event Consequences

```
KEY PERSON EVENT CONSEQUENCES
─────────────────────────────

AUTOMATIC SUSPENSION:
├── Investment period suspended
├── No new investments
├── Follow-on investments may continue
├── Existing commitments honored
└── Suspension typically 90-180 days

CURE PERIOD:
├── GP has time to address
├── Hire replacement
├── Obtain LP/LPAC consent to continue
└── Propose modified terms

LP VOTE:
├── If not cured, LPs vote
├── To reinstate investment period
├── Or to terminate fund
├── Typically requires majority/supermajority

EXAMPLE TIMELINE:
┌─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┐
│                                                                 │
│ Day 0:    Key person departs (trigger event)                   │
│           Investment period automatically suspended             │
│                                                                 │
│ Day 1-90: GP attempts cure (hire replacement, etc.)            │
│           LPAC consulted on proposed solution                  │
│                                                                 │
│ Day 90:   Cure period ends                                      │
│           If not cured: LP vote called                         │
│                                                                 │
│ Day 120:  LP vote deadline                                      │
│           Option A: >50% vote to reinstate → continue          │
│           Option B: <50% to reinstate → fund winds down        │
│                                                                 │
└─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┘
```

### Negotiating Key Person Terms

```
KEY PERSON NEGOTIATION POINTS
─────────────────────────────

WHO IS NAMED:
├── More individuals = more protection
├── But too many = frequent triggers
├── Balance: Core team members only
└── Should require minimum # to remain

TIME COMMITMENT:
├── "Substantially all" (vague)
├── Specific % (e.g., >50%)
├── Business time vs total time
└── Clarity reduces disputes

TRIGGER THRESHOLD:
├── Any one departure vs combination
├── "2 of 4 must remain" common
├── Consider succession planning
└── Named replacements acceptable?

CURE PERIOD:
├── Shorter = more LP protection
├── Longer = more GP flexibility
├── 90-180 days typical
└── Extension provisions?

REINSTATEMENT VOTE:
├── Majority vs supermajority
├── Of commitments or LPs?
├── Quorum requirements
└── Voting procedures

LP-FRIENDLY ENHANCEMENTS:
├── Shorter cure period (60 days)
├── Supermajority to reinstate (66%)
├── Individual LP termination right
├── Fee reduction during suspension
└── No new fees until reinstated
```

## 21.7 Other Protective Provisions

### Excuse Rights

```
EXCUSE RIGHTS
─────────────

ALLOW LP TO OPT OUT OF SPECIFIC INVESTMENTS

COMMON TRIGGERS:
├── Legal/regulatory restrictions
├── ERISA concerns
├── UBTI concerns
├── Reputational issues
├── Conflicts of interest
└── Policy restrictions (ESG, geography)

EXAMPLE:
┌─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┐
│                                                                 │
│ Fund invests in tobacco company                                 │
│                                                                 │
│ University endowment LP has policy prohibiting tobacco         │
│                                                                 │
│ LP exercises excuse right for this investment                   │
│                                                                 │
│ Result:                                                         │
│ ├── LP doesn't fund this capital call                          │
│ ├── LP's commitment reduced proportionally                     │
│ ├── Other LPs fund the gap (or not)                            │
│ └── LP doesn't share in returns from this investment           │
│                                                                 │
└─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┘

LIMITATIONS:
├── Can't excuse for economic reasons
├── Must have legitimate basis
├── GP may require documentation
├── Excessive use may affect relationship
```

### No-Fault Removal Rights

```
GP REMOVAL PROVISIONS
─────────────────────

"NO-FAULT" REMOVAL:
├── LPs can remove GP without cause
├── Typically requires supermajority (66-80%)
├── Significant protection for LPs
└── Rarely exercised

"FOR CAUSE" REMOVAL:
├── Requires GP misconduct
├── Fraud, gross negligence, etc.
├── Lower vote threshold (majority)
├── More common but still rare

CONSEQUENCES OF REMOVAL:
├── New GP must be identified
├── Existing investments continue
├── Carried interest treatment varies
├── Fund may wind down
└── Complex and disruptive

NO-FAULT REMOVAL MECHANICS:
┌─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┐
│                                                                 │
│ Trigger: 75% of commitments vote to remove GP                  │
│                                                                 │
│ Process:                                                        │
│ 1. LPs organize and document concerns                          │
│ 2. Written notice to GP of vote                                │
│ 3. LP meeting or written consent                               │
│ 4. If threshold met: GP removed                                │
│ 5. Successor GP identified (or wind down)                      │
│                                                                 │
│ GP Economics on Removal:                                        │
│ ├── Vested carry retained (for existing investments)           │
│ ├── Unvested carry typically forfeited                         │
│ ├── Management fee terminates                                  │
│ └── Transition period may apply                                │
│                                                                 │
└─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┘
```

### Concentration and Diversification Limits

```
INVESTMENT RESTRICTIONS
───────────────────────

CONCENTRATION LIMITS:
├── Maximum % of fund in single investment
├── Typically 15-25% of commitments
├── Prevents over-concentration
└── May require LPAC approval to exceed

DIVERSIFICATION REQUIREMENTS:
├── Minimum # of investments
├── Sector diversification
├── Geographic diversification
└── Less common, more flexible

EXAMPLE RESTRICTIONS:
┌─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┐
│                                                                 │
│ "No single investment shall exceed 20% of aggregate            │
│ Capital Commitments without LPAC approval."                    │
│                                                                 │
│ "The Fund shall not invest more than 30% of aggregate         │
│ Capital Commitments in any single industry sector."            │
│                                                                 │
│ "Investments outside North America shall not exceed            │
│ 25% of aggregate Capital Commitments."                         │
│                                                                 │
└─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┘
```

## Knowledge Check

1. What is a side letter and why do LPs negotiate them?
2. How does MFN (Most Favored Nation) work?
3. What are the pros and cons of co-investment rights?
4. What is the LPAC and what does it approve?
5. What happens during a key person event?

<details>
<summary>Answers</summary>

1. A side letter is a separate agreement between GP and individual LP that modifies or supplements the LPA. LPs negotiate them because the LPA is one-size-fits-all, but large LPs have leverage to obtain better economics, enhanced governance rights, regulatory accommodations, and co-investment access.

2. MFN gives an LP the right to receive benefits granted to other LPs. When the GP gives any LP more favorable terms, the GP must notify MFN holders, who can elect to receive those same terms (subject to certain carve-outs like size-based thresholds or regulatory provisions).

3. Pros: No management fee, no/reduced carry, exposure to best deals, lower blended fees. Cons: Concentration risk, potential adverse selection, operational burden, fast decision timelines, staffing requirements to evaluate opportunities.

4. LPAC (Limited Partner Advisory Committee) represents LP interests and typically approves: conflicts of interest, GP affiliate transactions, valuation matters, fund term extensions, and investment restriction exceptions. It usually has 5-10 members from the largest LPs.

5. When a key person event occurs (departure, death, reduced commitment), the investment period is automatically suspended. The GP has a cure period (typically 90-180 days) to address it. If not cured, LPs vote to either reinstate the investment period or wind down the fund.

</details>

```

<summary>Answers</summary>

```
1. PRIORITY TERMS TO NEGOTIATE:
   HIGH PRIORITY:
   ├── Management fee reduction (25bp discount)
   ├── Co-investment priority/guarantee
   ├── MFN rights (full scope)
   └── Enhanced reporting

   MEDIUM PRIORITY:
   ├── LPAC seat (below $150M threshold)
   ├── Carry reduction on co-invest
   └── Capacity rights for Fund VII

   LOWER PRIORITY:
   ├── Transfer rights enhancement
   └── Additional excuse rights

2. YOUR LEVERAGE:
   STRENGTHS:
   ├── 5% of fund is meaningful commitment
   ├── Existing relationship (valued, less risky)
   ├── Pension fund brand/reference value
   ├── Co-investment capability (GP values this)
