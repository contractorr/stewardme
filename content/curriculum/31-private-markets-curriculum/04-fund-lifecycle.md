# Module 04: The Fund Lifecycle

## Learning Objectives

By the end of this module, you will understand:
- Each phase of a fund's life from formation to liquidation
- Key milestones and events at each stage
- How capital flows throughout the lifecycle
- Typical timelines and what drives them
- Extension provisions and wind-down procedures

## 4.2 Phase 1: Fundraising

### Timeline: 12-18 months before first investment

### What Happens

The GP raises capital from LPs before the fund can begin investing.

```
FUNDRAISING PROCESS
───────────────────

Month 1-3: PREPARATION
├── • Finalize fund strategy and thesis
├── • Draft PPM (Private Placement Memorandum)
├── • Prepare LPA (Limited Partnership Agreement)
├── • Create marketing materials
├── • Engage placement agent (optional)
└── • Target list of potential LPs

Month 4-9: MARKETING
├── • Road shows and LP meetings
├── • One-on-one presentations
├── • Due diligence responses
├── • Track record presentations
├── • Operational due diligence (ODDs)
└── • Side letter negotiations

Month 10-14: CLOSINGS
├── • First Close (minimum capital raised)
│   └── Fund can begin investing
├── • Second Close
├── • Third Close
└── • Final Close (no new LPs accepted)
```

### Closings Explained

| Close | Timing | Purpose |
|-------|--------|---------|
| **First Close** | When minimum capital reached | Fund officially launches; investing can begin |
| **Interim Closes** | Periodic (monthly/quarterly) | Additional LPs join |
| **Final Close** | 12-18 months after first | No new LPs; fund size finalized |

**Equalization**: LPs joining after first close must pay:
- Their share of investments made
- Catch-up management fees
- Interest on late contributions

### Fund Size Targets

```
EXAMPLE: ABC PARTNERS FUND VIII
───────────────────────────────

Target Fund Size:    $3.0 billion
Hard Cap:            $3.5 billion
First Close Target:  $1.5 billion

ACTUAL CLOSINGS:
┌─────────────────────────────────────────────────┐
│ First Close (March 2024)                        │
│ • 15 LPs committed $1.8B                        │
│ • Fund begins investing                         │
├─────────────────────────────────────────────────┤
│ Second Close (June 2024)                        │
│ • 8 new LPs add $600M                           │
│ • Cumulative: $2.4B                             │
├─────────────────────────────────────────────────┤
│ Final Close (September 2024)                    │
│ • 5 new LPs add $500M                           │
│ • Over-subscribed: $2.9B                        │
│ • Some LPs scaled back to meet hard cap         │
│ • Final fund size: $3.2B                        │
└─────────────────────────────────────────────────┘
```

## 4.3 Phase 2: Investment Period

### Timeline: Years 1-5 (typically)

### What Happens

The GP deploys committed capital into investments.

```
INVESTMENT PERIOD ACTIVITIES
────────────────────────────

YEAR 1
├── • Deploy 15-25% of capital
├── • Establish early portfolio
├── • Build deal pipeline
└── • First capital calls

YEAR 2-3
├── • Peak deployment activity
├── • Deploy 40-60% of capital (cumulative)
├── • Regular capital calls
└── • Initial portfolio management

YEAR 4-5
├── • Complete primary investments
├── • Deploy remaining 80-100%
├── • Follow-on investments begin
├── • Some early exits possible
└── • Investment period ends
```

### Capital Deployment Pattern

```
Cumulative Capital Called (% of Commitment)
    │
100%┤                                    ────────────
    │                               ────
 80%┤                          ────
    │                     ────
 60%┤                ────
    │           ────
 40%┤      ────
    │  ────
 20%┤ ──
    │
  0%┼──────────────────────────────────────────────► Year
    0     1     2     3     4     5     6     7
```

### Investment Period Rules

| Rule | Typical Term |
|------|-------------|
| **Duration** | 5 years from final close |
| **Purpose** | New investments only during this period |
| **Extensions** | 1-2 year extensions with LP consent |
| **Exceptions** | Follow-ons may continue post-period |

### What Ends When Investment Period Ends

After the investment period:
- ❌ No new platform investments
- ❌ Unfunded commitment for new deals may expire
- ✅ Follow-on investments in existing companies allowed
- ✅ Management fee typically steps down
- ✅ Portfolio management continues

## 4.4 Phase 3: Harvest Period

### Timeline: Years 5-10

### What Happens

Focus shifts from buying to value creation and selling.

```
HARVEST PERIOD ACTIVITIES
─────────────────────────

VALUE CREATION (Years 5-7)
├── • Operational improvements mature
├── • Add-on acquisitions complete
├── • Management teams developed
├── • Exit preparation begins
└── • Some distributions start

REALIZATION (Years 7-10)
├── • Active exit processes
├── • Trade sales
├── • Secondary sales
├── • IPOs (rare)
├── • Dividend recapitalizations
├── • Heavy distribution activity
└── • Final exits

WIND-DOWN (Years 10+)
├── • Remaining assets sold
├── • Final distributions
├── • Fund closure
└── • Carried interest finalized
```

### Exit Methods

| Exit Type | Description | Frequency |
|-----------|-------------|-----------|
| **Trade Sale** | Sell to strategic buyer (corporation) | ~50% of exits |
| **Sponsor-to-Sponsor** | Sell to another PE fund | ~30% of exits |
| **IPO** | Public listing | ~5-10% |
| **Dividend Recap** | Refinance, take out proceeds | Partial exit |
| **Management Buyout** | Sell to management team | Rare |

### Exit Timing Considerations

```
FACTORS AFFECTING EXIT TIMING
─────────────────────────────

COMPANY-SPECIFIC
├── • Business performance / growth trajectory
├── • Value creation plan completion
├── • Management team readiness
└── • Competitive position

MARKET CONDITIONS
├── • M&A market activity
├── • Credit availability for buyers
├── • Industry multiples
├── • IPO window

FUND CONSIDERATIONS
├── • Fund age / remaining life
├── • LP return expectations
├── • Carried interest crystallization
└── • Follow-on fund timing
```

## 4.5 Phase 4: Extension & Wind-Down

### Timeline: Years 10-12+

### Extension Provisions

Most funds allow 2-3 one-year extensions beyond the 10-year term:

| Extension | Approval Required | Purpose |
|-----------|-------------------|---------|
| **First (Year 11)** | GP discretion or LPAC | Complete remaining exits |
| **Second (Year 12)** | LP supermajority (66-75%) | Difficult-to-sell assets |
| **Third (Year 13+)** | LP supermajority | Exceptional circumstances |

### Why Extensions Happen

- Market conditions unfavorable for exits
- Portfolio company needs more time
- Restructuring in progress
- COVID-like disruptions

### Wind-Down Process

```
FUND WIND-DOWN
──────────────

FINAL EXITS
├── • Sell remaining assets (may accept lower prices)
├── • Wind down portfolio companies
└── • Liquidate any remaining positions

FINAL DISTRIBUTIONS
├── • Distribute remaining cash
├── • In-kind distributions (rare)
├── • Carried interest settlement
└── • Clawback calculations (if applicable)

ADMINISTRATIVE CLOSURE
├── • Final audit
├── • Final tax returns / K-1s
├── • Dissolution filings
├── • Record retention
└── • Fund termination
```

## 4.7 Reporting Throughout the Lifecycle

### Report Frequency by Phase

| Phase | Report Types | Frequency |
|-------|-------------|-----------|
| **Fundraising** | PPM updates, term negotiations | As needed |
| **Investment Period** | Quarterly reports, capital calls | Quarterly + as needed |
| **Harvest Period** | Quarterly reports, distributions | Quarterly + as needed |
| **Wind-Down** | Final reports, K-1s | Quarterly → Annual |

### What Changes in Reports Over Time

```
REPORT CONTENT BY FUND AGE
──────────────────────────

EARLY FUND (Years 1-3)
├── Heavy focus on new investments
├── Pipeline and deal activity
├── IRR often negative or low (J-curve)
├── NAV building up
└── High unfunded commitment

MID-LIFE FUND (Years 4-7)
├── Portfolio company performance
├── Value creation progress
├── Exit preparations
├── IRR improving
├── Mix of calls and distributions
└── More mature portfolio

LATE FUND (Years 8-10+)
├── Exit activity summary
├── Remaining portfolio details
├── Final IRR taking shape
├── Heavy distributions
├── Low/zero unfunded
└── Wind-down plans
```

## 4.8 Special Situations

### Early Exit (Fund Restructuring)

Sometimes funds restructure before natural end:

| Scenario | What Happens |
|----------|-------------|
| **GP-led Secondary** | New vehicle buys remaining portfolio; LPs can roll or cash out |
| **Tender Offer** | Third party offers to buy LP interests |
| **Fund Continuation** | New terms, extended life, fresh capital |

### Fund Termination Events

Rare but possible:

- **GP removal**: LPs vote out GP for cause
- **Key person event**: Senior team departure triggers suspension
- **Regulatory issues**: Compliance failures
- **GP bankruptcy**: Management company fails

## 4.10 Practical Example: Full Fund Lifecycle

```
CASE STUDY: TECH PARTNERS FUND VI
─────────────────────────────────

FUND DETAILS
• Vintage: 2018
• Size: $800M
• Strategy: Growth equity / Tech buyout
• Term: 10 years + 2 x 1-year extensions

TIMELINE:

2017 (Year -1): FUNDRAISING
├── Q2: First close at $400M
├── Q3: Second close at $600M
├── Q4: Final close at $800M
└── Key LPs: 2 pensions, 3 endowments, 5 family offices

2018-2022 (Years 1-5): INVESTMENT PERIOD
├── 2018: Invested $120M in 3 companies
├── 2019: Invested $180M in 4 companies
├── 2020: COVID slowdown - only $80M in 2 companies
├── 2021: Catch-up - $200M in 5 companies
├── 2022: Final $150M in 3 add-ons
└── Total: $730M invested in 14 companies

2023-2026 (Years 6-9): HARVEST
├── 2023: 2 exits generating $280M distributions
├── 2024: 3 exits generating $450M distributions
├── 2025: 4 exits generating $580M distributions
├── 2026: 2 exits generating $320M distributions
└── Remaining: 3 companies valued at $400M

2027-2028 (Years 10-11): WIND-DOWN
├── 2027: Extension approved; 2 exits for $350M
├── 2028: Final exit for $180M
└── Fund closed

FINAL RESULTS:
• Total called: $760M
• Total distributed: $2,160M
• Final TVPI: 2.84x
• Net IRR: 22.3%
```

## Knowledge Check

1. What's the difference between first close and final close?
2. What typically happens when the investment period ends?
3. Why might a fund need extensions beyond year 10?
4. When do distributions typically peak during a fund's life?
5. What is a GP-led secondary?

<details>
<summary>Answers</summary>

1. First close = minimum capital raised, fund starts investing. Final close = no new LPs, fund size finalized.
2. No new platform investments allowed; unfunded commitment may expire; management fee often steps down; but follow-on investments and portfolio management continue.
3. Market conditions unfavorable for exits, portfolio companies need more time, restructuring in progress, or unexpected disruptions.
4. Usually years 7-9, when most mature investments are being exited.
5. A transaction where the GP creates a new vehicle to acquire remaining portfolio companies, giving existing LPs the option to cash out or roll into the new vehicle.

</details>
