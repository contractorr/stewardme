### ESG vs Impact

```
ESG vs IMPACT DISTINCTION
─────────────────────────

ESG INTEGRATION:
├── Considers ESG factors in investment decisions
├── Risk management focus
├── Applied to ALL investments
├── Avoid harm, manage risks
└── No specific outcome targets

Example: PE fund screens for governance risks,
         monitors environmental compliance,
         improves labor practices at portcos

IMPACT INVESTING:
├── Intentionally generates positive outcomes
├── Financial returns + measurable impact
├── Specific impact thesis
├── Targets certain sectors/themes
└── Requires impact measurement

Example: Fund invests in renewable energy
         companies with goal of X tons CO2
         avoided and Y% financial return

SPECTRUM:
┌─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┐
│                                                                 │
│ Traditional ──► ESG Risk ──► ESG Value ──► Impact ──► Impact  │
│ Investing       Screening    Creation      First      Only     │
│                                                                 │
│ No ESG         Avoid bad    ESG drives    Impact =   Impact   │
│ consideration  investments  returns       priority   paramount │
│                                                                 │
└─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┘
```

## 19.2 Regulatory Framework

### SFDR (EU Sustainable Finance Disclosure Regulation)

```
SFDR OVERVIEW
─────────────

APPLIES TO:
├── EU-based asset managers
├── Funds marketed in EU
├── Managers with EU investors
└── Effectively global reach for PE

FUND CLASSIFICATIONS:
┌─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┐
│                                                                 │
│  ARTICLE 6                                                      │
│  "Mainstream"                                                   │
│  ├── No sustainability objective                               │
│  ├── Must disclose integration of sustainability risks         │
│  └── Minimum requirement                                        │
│                                                                 │
│  ARTICLE 8                                                      │
│  "Light Green"                                                  │
│  ├── Promotes environmental or social characteristics          │
│  ├── Does not have sustainability as primary objective         │
│  ├── Must disclose how characteristics are met                 │
│  └── Most PE funds landing here                                │
│                                                                 │
│  ARTICLE 9                                                      │
│  "Dark Green"                                                   │
│  ├── Has sustainable investment as objective                   │
│  ├── Impact funds typically                                    │
│  ├── Highest disclosure requirements                           │
│  └── "Do no significant harm" test                             │
│                                                                 │
└─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┘

PRINCIPAL ADVERSE IMPACTS (PAIs):
├── GHG emissions (Scope 1, 2, 3)
├── Carbon footprint
├── Energy consumption
├── Biodiversity impact
├── Water usage
├── Waste production
├── Social/governance indicators
└── Must report on mandatory indicators
```

### SFDR Reporting Requirements

```
SFDR DISCLOSURE REQUIREMENTS
────────────────────────────

PRE-CONTRACTUAL (PPM/LPA):
├── How sustainability risks integrated
├── PAI consideration (or explain why not)
├── Article 8: How characteristics promoted
├── Article 9: Sustainable objective details
└── Taxonomy alignment disclosure

PERIODIC REPORTS:
├── Annual sustainability reporting
├── PAI indicators (if committed)
├── Progress on Article 8/9 objectives
├── Comparison to benchmark (if applicable)
└── Due within 6 months of year-end

WEBSITE DISCLOSURE:
├── Summary of ESG policies
├── Article 8/9 fund information
├── PAI statement
├── Remuneration policy (ESG integration)
└── Must be kept up to date

EU TAXONOMY:
┌─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┐
│ Classification system for "environmentally sustainable"        │
│                                                                 │
│ Activities must:                                                │
│ ├── Contribute to 1+ environmental objectives                 │
│ ├── Do no significant harm to other objectives                │
│ ├── Meet minimum social safeguards                            │
│ └── Comply with technical screening criteria                  │
│                                                                 │
│ Disclosure: % of investments Taxonomy-aligned                  │
└─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┘
```

### Impact Measurement Challenges

```
IMPACT MEASUREMENT CHALLENGES
─────────────────────────────

1. ATTRIBUTION
   └── Did the investment CAUSE the impact?
   └── Or would it have happened anyway?

   Example: Invested in solar company
   ├── Company installed 100 MW
   ├── But would have grown without your capital
   └── How much impact is attributable to you?

2. ADDITIONALITY
   └── Would impact occur without this investment?
   └── Key question for impact claims

   Framework:
   ├── HIGH additionality: First capital in, enables existence
   ├── MODERATE: Accelerates growth, expands reach
   ├── LOW: Would happen anyway, just different investor

3. DATA AVAILABILITY
   └── Portfolio companies may not track impact data
   └── Measurement can be costly
   └── Quality varies significantly

4. AGGREGATION
   └── How to aggregate different types of impact?
   └── Tons CO2 + jobs created + loans provided = ?
   └── No single impact "return"

5. GREENWASHING RISK
   └── Overstating impact claims
   └── Selective reporting
   └── Regulatory scrutiny increasing
```

```
THEORY OF CHANGE FRAMEWORK
──────────────────────────

CONCEPT:
A logic model connecting investment activities to impact

STRUCTURE:
┌─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┐
│                                                                 │
│ INPUTS ──► ACTIVITIES ──► OUTPUTS ──► OUTCOMES ──► IMPACT      │
│                                                                 │
│ What we    What we      What we      Changes      Long-term    │
│ invest    do           produce      that result  effects       │
│                                                                 │
└─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┘

EXAMPLE: Affordable Housing Fund
┌─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┐
│                                                                 │
│ INPUTS:    $100M fund capital                                   │
│            ↓                                                    │
│ ACTIVITIES: Acquire/renovate affordable apartments              │
│            ↓                                                    │
│ OUTPUTS:   500 units renovated, 200 new units built            │
│            ↓                                                    │
│ OUTCOMES:  700 families housed at below-market rents           │
│            $2M annual rent savings for residents               │
│            ↓                                                    │
│ IMPACT:    Improved housing stability, health outcomes,        │
│            educational attainment for children                  │
│                                                                 │
│ MEASUREMENT:                                                    │
│ ├── Outputs: Units (easy to measure)                           │
│ ├── Outcomes: Rent savings (moderate)                          │
│ └── Impact: Long-term life outcomes (hard to measure)          │
│                                                                 │
└─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┘
```

## Knowledge Check

1. What is the difference between ESG integration and impact investing?
2. What are the three SFDR fund classifications?
3. What organization maintains the IRIS+ impact metrics?
4. Name three sections of the ILPA ESG DDQ.
5. What are the four pillars of TCFD reporting?

<details>
<summary>Answers</summary>

1. ESG integration considers environmental, social, and governance factors in investment decisions primarily for risk management, applied to all investments. Impact investing intentionally generates measurable positive social/environmental outcomes alongside financial returns, with specific impact targets.

2. Article 6 (no sustainability objective, minimum disclosure), Article 8 ("light green" - promotes E/S characteristics), Article 9 ("dark green" - sustainable investment as objective).

3. The Global Impact Investing Network (GIIN) maintains IRIS+.

4. Any three of: Policy & Governance, Investment Process, Ownership & Stewardship, Reporting & Disclosure, Climate, Diversity/Equity/Inclusion.

5. The four TCFD pillars are: (1) Governance, (2) Strategy, (3) Risk Management, (4) Metrics & Targets.

</details>
