# Personal Finance Glossary

## Quick Reference of Key Terms

This glossary provides concise definitions of essential personal finance terms covered throughout the study guide. Terms are organized alphabetically for easy reference.

---

## A

**401(k)**: Employer-sponsored retirement account allowing pre-tax (traditional) or after-tax (Roth) contributions; 2025 limit $23,500 ($31,000 age 50+)

**529 Plan**: Tax-advantaged college savings account; contributions grow tax-free; withdrawals tax-free for qualified education expenses

**Adjusted Gross Income (AGI)**: Total income minus above-the-line deductions; determines eligibility for tax credits and deductions

**Amortization**: Gradual repayment of loan through scheduled payments covering principal and interest

**Annual Percentage Rate (APR)**: Total cost of borrowing including interest and fees, expressed as yearly percentage

**Annuity**: Financial product providing guaranteed income stream for specified period or lifetime

**Appreciation**: Increase in asset value over time

**Asset**: Anything of value owned that can be converted to cash (house, investments, cash)

**Asset Allocation**: Distribution of investment portfolio across different asset classes (stocks, bonds, real estate)

**Asset Class**: Category of investments with similar characteristics (equities, fixed income, real estate, cash)

---

## B

**Balance Transfer**: Moving credit card debt from high-interest card to lower-interest card (often 0% introductory rate)

**Bankruptcy**: Legal process to eliminate or restructure debt when unable to repay; severely damages credit (7-10 years on report)

**Basis Point**: One-hundredth of one percent (0.01%); 100 basis points = 1%

**Bear Market**: Market decline of 20% or more from recent highs

**Beneficiary**: Person or entity designated to receive assets from account, trust, or estate upon owner's death

**Bond**: Debt security representing loan to issuer (government or corporation) with fixed interest payments

**Budget**: Financial plan allocating income across expenses, savings, and investments

**Bull Market**: Market rise of 20% or more from recent lows

---

## C

**Capital Gains**: Profit from selling asset for more than purchase price; taxed at preferential rates if held 1+ year

**Cash Flow**: Net amount of cash moving in and out; income minus expenses

**Certificate of Deposit (CD)**: Bank deposit account with fixed term and interest rate; FDIC insured; early withdrawal penalty

**Compound Interest**: Earning returns on both principal and accumulated interest; exponential growth over time

**Contribution Limit**: Maximum annual amount allowed to contribute to retirement account (varies by account type)

**Correlation**: Statistical measure of how two investments move relative to each other (-1 to +1)

**Credit Report**: Detailed record of credit history maintained by Equifax, Experian, and TransUnion

**Credit Score**: Three-digit number (300-850) representing creditworthiness based on credit history

**Credit Utilization**: Credit card balances divided by total credit limits; major factor in credit scores (keep under 30%)

---

## D

**Debt-to-Income Ratio (DTI)**: Total monthly debt payments divided by gross monthly income; key mortgage qualification metric

**Deduction**: Expense that reduces taxable income; value equals deduction amount times marginal tax rate

**Depreciation**: Decrease in asset value over time

**Diversification**: Spreading investments across multiple assets to reduce risk without sacrificing expected returns

**Dividend**: Distribution of company profits to shareholders; typically paid quarterly

**Dollar-Cost Averaging**: Investing fixed amounts regularly regardless of market conditions; reduces timing risk

**Down Payment**: Upfront payment toward home purchase; typically 3.5-20% of price

**Duration**: Measure of bond price sensitivity to interest rate changes; higher duration = more volatility

---

## E

**Effective Tax Rate**: Total taxes paid divided by total income; actual average tax rate (always lower than marginal)

**Emergency Fund**: Savings reserved for unexpected expenses; recommended 3-12 months of expenses

**Equity**: 1) Stocks/shares representing ownership in companies; 2) Value of home beyond mortgage debt

**Estate**: Total assets and liabilities owned at death

**Estate Tax**: Federal tax on transfer of estate at death; 2025 exemption $13.61M individual/$27.22M married; 40% rate above exemption

**ETF (Exchange-Traded Fund)**: Investment fund tracking index, trading like stock on exchange; typically low fees

**Executor**: Person named in will to manage estate settlement and distribute assets

---

## F

**FDIC Insurance**: Federal Deposit Insurance Corporation protection of bank deposits up to $250,000 per depositor per institution

**Fiduciary**: Person or entity legally obligated to act in your best financial interest

**FICO Score**: Most common credit scoring model (300-850); weighs payment history, utilization, length, new credit, mix

**Financial Independence**: Having sufficient passive income to cover living expenses without employment

**Fixed Income**: Investments providing regular, predictable income (bonds, CDs)

**Fixed-Rate Mortgage**: Mortgage with interest rate locked for entire term

**Foreclosure**: Legal process where lender repossesses property due to missed mortgage payments

**Full Retirement Age (FRA)**: Age for 100% Social Security benefit; 66-67 depending on birth year

---

## G

**Good Debt**: Borrowing for appreciating assets or income-producing investments (mortgages, strategic student loans)

**Bad Debt**: High-interest borrowing for depreciating assets or consumption (credit cards, payday loans)

**Gross Income**: Total income before taxes and deductions

**Growth Stock**: Company reinvesting profits for expansion; typically high P/E ratio, no dividends

---

## H

**Healthcare Directive**: Document specifying medical treatment preferences if unable to communicate; includes living will and medical power of attorney

**High-Yield Savings Account**: Savings account with interest rate significantly above national average (4-5% in 2024-25); FDIC insured

**Home Equity**: Market value of home minus outstanding mortgage balance

**HSA (Health Savings Account)**: Triple tax-advantaged account for medical expenses; requires high-deductible health plan; 2025 limit $4,300 individual/$8,550 family

---

## I

**Index Fund**: Investment fund tracking market index (S&P 500, Total Market); low fees, passive management

**Inflation**: General increase in prices over time; reduces purchasing power; typically 2-3% annually

**Intestate**: Dying without valid will; state law determines asset distribution

**Investment-Grade Bond**: Corporate bond rated BBB- or higher by S&P; low default risk; lower yields than high-yield bonds

**IRA (Individual Retirement Account)**: Personal retirement account; traditional (pre-tax) or Roth (after-tax); 2025 limit $7,000 ($8,000 age 50+)

**Itemized Deductions**: Specific deductible expenses (mortgage interest, state taxes, charitable donations); alternative to standard deduction

---

## L

**Liability**: Financial obligation or debt owed to others

**Liquidity**: How quickly asset can be converted to cash without losing value

**Living Will**: Part of healthcare directive; specifies end-of-life treatment preferences

**Long-Term Capital Gains**: Profit from selling asset held over 1 year; taxed at preferential rates (0%, 15%, or 20%)

---

## M

**Marginal Tax Rate**: Tax rate on last dollar of income; determines value of deductions

**Market Capitalization**: Total value of company's outstanding shares (price × shares outstanding)

**Maturity Date**: Date when bond principal is repaid

**Medicare**: Federal health insurance for 65+ and disabled; Parts A (hospital), B (medical), C (advantage plans), D (prescription drugs)

**Modified Adjusted Gross Income (MAGI)**: AGI with certain deductions added back; used for phase-out calculations

**Mortgage**: Loan for purchasing real estate; property serves as collateral

**Municipal Bond**: Bond issued by state/local government; interest typically exempt from federal tax

**Mutual Fund**: Pooled investment fund actively managed by professionals; typically higher fees than index funds

---

## N

**Net Investment Income Tax**: 3.8% surtax on investment income when MAGI exceeds $200k (single)/$250k (married)

**Net Worth**: Total assets minus total liabilities; measure of overall financial health

**Nominal Return**: Investment return before adjusting for inflation

**Non-Deductible IRA**: Traditional IRA contribution without tax deduction; basis tracked for tax-free withdrawal later

---

## P

**P/E Ratio (Price-to-Earnings)**: Stock valuation metric; stock price divided by earnings per share; S&P 500 average ~20-25

**Pension**: Employer-funded retirement plan promising specific monthly benefit (defined benefit); rare in private sector

**PMI (Private Mortgage Insurance)**: Insurance required on mortgages with <20% down; protects lender; typically 0.5-1.5% of loan annually

**Portfolio**: Collection of investments held by individual or institution

**Power of Attorney (POA)**: Document authorizing someone to make financial decisions if incapacitated

**Pre-Tax Contribution**: Retirement contribution reducing current taxable income (traditional 401k, traditional IRA)

**Primary Residence**: Main home where you live; qualifies for capital gains exclusion ($250k single/$500k married)

**Principal**: 1) Original loan amount borrowed; 2) Initial investment amount before gains

**Probate**: Court-supervised process of validating will and distributing assets; public, time-consuming (6-24 months)

---

## Q

**Qualified Dividend**: Dividend meeting IRS criteria for lower tax rate (0%, 15%, or 20% vs. ordinary income)

**Qualified Distribution**: Tax-free withdrawal from Roth account after age 59½ and 5-year holding period

---

## R

**Real Estate Investment Trust (REIT)**: Company owning income-producing real estate; trades like stock; must distribute 90% of income as dividends

**Real Return**: Investment return after adjusting for inflation

**Rebalancing**: Adjusting portfolio back to target asset allocation by selling outperformers and buying underperformers

**Refinancing**: Replacing existing loan with new loan, typically at better terms or lower rate

**Required Minimum Distribution (RMD)**: Mandatory annual withdrawal from tax-deferred accounts starting age 73; penalty 25% of amount not withdrawn

**Residual Clause**: Will provision distributing all remaining assets not specifically bequeathed

**Revocable Living Trust**: Legal entity holding assets during lifetime; avoids probate; can modify or cancel anytime

**Risk Tolerance**: Ability and willingness to endure investment volatility

**Roth Conversion**: Moving money from traditional IRA to Roth IRA; pay taxes now for tax-free future growth

**Roth IRA**: Retirement account with after-tax contributions; tax-free growth and withdrawals; no RMDs; 2025 income limits $150-165k single/$236-246k married

---

## S

**Sequence of Returns Risk**: Danger of poor market returns early in retirement depleting portfolio faster

**Short-Term Capital Gains**: Profit from selling asset held less than 1 year; taxed as ordinary income

**Social Security**: Federal retirement and disability insurance program funded by payroll taxes; provides monthly income in retirement

**Standard Deduction**: Fixed amount reducing taxable income; 2025 amounts $15k single/$30k married; simpler alternative to itemizing

**Stock**: Security representing ownership share in corporation; growth through appreciation and dividends

**Stop-Loss Order**: Instruction to sell security if price falls to specified level; limits losses

---

## T

**Tax Credit**: Dollar-for-dollar reduction in taxes owed; more valuable than deductions

**Tax-Deferred**: Growth not taxed until withdrawal (traditional 401k, traditional IRA)

**Tax-Free**: Growth and withdrawals never taxed (Roth accounts, HSA for medical expenses)

**Taxable Account**: Regular brokerage account; no contribution limits; no tax advantages; capital gains and dividends taxed annually

**Term Life Insurance**: Life insurance covering specific period (10, 20, 30 years); pure death benefit; lower cost than permanent

**Testamentary Trust**: Trust created by will upon death; goes through probate

**Total Return**: Capital appreciation plus dividends/interest received

**Treasury Bond**: Debt security issued by U.S. government; virtually risk-free; 20-30 year maturity; interest exempt from state/local tax

**Trustee**: Person or institution managing trust assets according to trust terms

---

## U

**Underwriting**: Process of evaluating loan application and determining approval, terms, and rate

---

## V

**Value Stock**: Undervalued company trading below intrinsic value; typically low P/E ratio, pays dividends

**Vesting**: Process of earning ownership of employer contributions to retirement accounts over time

**Volatility**: Degree of price fluctuation in investment values; standard deviation of returns

---

## W

**W-2**: Tax form showing annual wages and taxes withheld; issued by employers

**W-4**: Tax form employees complete indicating withholding allowances

**Wash Sale**: Selling security at loss and repurchasing substantially identical security within 30 days; loss disallowed for tax purposes

**Will (Last Will and Testament)**: Legal document specifying asset distribution, executor, and guardians upon death

**Withholding**: Taxes automatically deducted from paycheck and sent to IRS

---

## Y

**Yield**: Income return on investment; dividends for stocks, interest for bonds; expressed as annual percentage

**Yield to Maturity (YTM)**: Total return on bond if held to maturity; accounts for interest payments and capital gain/loss

---

## Financial Calculations Quick Reference

### Compound Interest Formula
**A = P(1 + r/n)^(nt)**
- A = Final amount
- P = Principal (initial amount)
- r = Annual interest rate (decimal)
- n = Compounding frequency per year
- t = Time in years

### Rule of 72
**Years to Double = 72 ÷ Annual Return (%)**
- 6% return: 72 ÷ 6 = 12 years to double
- 9% return: 72 ÷ 9 = 8 years to double

### Debt-to-Income Ratio (DTI)
**DTI = Total Monthly Debt Payments ÷ Gross Monthly Income × 100**
- Lenders prefer under 43-50%

### Credit Utilization
**Utilization = Total Credit Card Balances ÷ Total Credit Limits × 100**
- Keep under 30%, ideally under 10%

### 4% Rule (Retirement)
**Required Nest Egg = Annual Expenses × 25**
- $60,000 expenses: $1.5M nest egg needed
- Withdraw 4% annually adjusted for inflation

### 50/30/20 Budget Rule
- 50% Needs (essentials)
- 30% Wants (discretionary)
- 20% Savings (future-oriented)

### Tax-Equivalent Yield (Municipal Bonds)
**Tax-Equiv Yield = Muni Yield ÷ (1 - Tax Rate)**
- 3% muni in 32% bracket: 3% ÷ 0.68 = 4.41% equivalent

### Home Affordability (28/36 Rule)
- Housing costs: Max 28% of gross income
- Total debt: Max 36% of gross income

### Emergency Fund Size
- Single income: 6-12 months expenses
- Dual income: 3-6 months expenses
- Self-employed: 12+ months expenses

## Investment Return Benchmarks

**Historical Annual Returns (1926-2024)**:
- Large-cap stocks (S&P 500): ~10%
- Small-cap stocks: ~12%
- International stocks: ~8%
- Bonds (10-year Treasury): ~5%
- Real estate (REITs): ~9%
- Cash (T-bills): ~3%
- Inflation: ~3%

**Real returns** (after inflation): Stocks ~7%, Bonds ~2%
