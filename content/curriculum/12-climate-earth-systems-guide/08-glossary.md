# Glossary: Key Terms in Climate and Earth Systems

## A

**Adaptation**: Adjustments in natural or human systems in response to actual or expected climate change to minimize harm or exploit beneficial opportunities.

**Aerosol**: Tiny solid or liquid particles suspended in atmosphere; can cool climate by reflecting sunlight (sulfates) or warm it (black carbon).

**Albedo**: Measure of reflectivity; proportion of solar radiation reflected by a surface. Ice/snow have high albedo (~0.8), dark ocean has low albedo (~0.06).

**Anthropogenic**: Originating from human activities (e.g., anthropogenic climate change from greenhouse gas emissions).

**Atmosphere**: Gaseous envelope surrounding Earth, extending from surface to ~10,000 km altitude; regulates temperature and hosts weather.

**Attribution**: Scientific determination of relative contributions of natural vs human factors to observed climate changes.

## B

**Biodiversity**: Variety of life at genetic, species, and ecosystem levels; underpins ecosystem resilience and services.

**Biogeochemical Cycle**: Movement of chemical elements/compounds through living and non-living components of Earth system (e.g., carbon cycle, nitrogen cycle).

**Biome**: Large-scale ecosystem type characterized by climate and dominant vegetation (e.g., tropical rainforest, tundra, desert).

**Biosphere**: All living organisms on Earth and their interactions with other Earth system components.

## C

**Carbon Budget**: Cumulative amount of CO₂ emissions compatible with limiting warming to specific temperature target (e.g., 400 Gt CO₂ for 67% chance of limiting warming to 1.5°C).

**Carbon Capture and Storage (CCS)**: Capturing CO₂ from emission sources or atmosphere and storing it permanently, typically in geological formations.

**Carbon Dioxide (CO₂)**: Greenhouse gas produced by burning fossil fuels, deforestation, and industrial processes; accounts for ~75% of anthropogenic warming.

**Carbon Dioxide Removal (CDR)**: Extracting CO₂ from atmosphere and storing it durably (e.g., reforestation, direct air capture, enhanced weathering).

**Carbon Footprint**: Total greenhouse gas emissions caused by individual, organization, event, or product over its lifecycle.

**Carbon Neutral**: Achieving net-zero carbon emissions by balancing emissions with removal or offsets.

**Carbon Sequestration**: Long-term storage of carbon, removing it from active atmospheric cycle (e.g., in forests, soils, geological storage).

**Carbon Sink**: Reservoir absorbing more carbon than it releases (e.g., forests, oceans). Opposite of carbon source.

**Climate**: Long-term average weather patterns in a region, typically calculated over 30+ years.

**Climate Feedback**: Process where initial climate change amplifies (positive feedback) or dampens (negative feedback) itself. Example: Ice-albedo feedback.

**Climate Justice**: Concept that climate change impacts and responsibilities are distributed unequally; vulnerable populations often least responsible bear greatest harm.

**Climate Model**: Mathematical representation of Earth system components and interactions; used to project future climate under different scenarios.

**Climate Sensitivity**: Global temperature increase from doubling atmospheric CO₂; estimated at ~3°C (range 2.5-4°C).

**CO₂-equivalent (CO₂e)**: Metric combining all greenhouse gases by their global warming potential relative to CO₂ (e.g., 1 ton methane = 28-34 tons CO₂e over 100 years).

**Coriolis Effect**: Deflection of moving objects (including air and water) due to Earth's rotation; rightward in Northern Hemisphere, leftward in Southern Hemisphere.

**Cryosphere**: Frozen water components of Earth system - ice sheets, glaciers, sea ice, permafrost, snow cover.

## D

**Deforestation**: Permanent removal of forest cover, typically for agriculture, logging, or development; contributes ~11% of global emissions.

## E

**Earth System**: Interconnected physical, chemical, and biological components of planet - atmosphere, hydrosphere, lithosphere, cryosphere, biosphere.

**Ecosystem**: Community of interacting organisms and their physical environment.

**Ecosystem Services**: Benefits humans obtain from ecosystems - provisioning (food, water), regulating (climate, water purification), supporting (nutrient cycling), cultural (recreation, spiritual).

**El Niño-Southern Oscillation (ENSO)**: Irregular oscillation (2-7 years) between warm (El Niño) and cool (La Niña) phases in tropical Pacific, affecting global weather.

**Emissions**: Release of greenhouse gases into atmosphere from human activities or natural processes.

**Enhanced Greenhouse Effect**: Strengthening of natural greenhouse effect from increased atmospheric greenhouse gas concentrations due to human activities.

**Extinction**: Complete loss of a species from Earth; current rates 100-1000x natural background rates.

## F

**Feedback Loop**: Process where initial change causes effects that amplify (positive) or dampen (negative) the original change.

**Forcing (Radiative Forcing)**: Change in energy balance at top of atmosphere measured in W/m²; positive forcing = warming, negative = cooling.

**Fossil Fuels**: Carbon-rich fuels formed from ancient organic matter - coal, oil, natural gas; burning releases CO₂ stored for millions of years.

## G

**Glacial/Interglacial Cycles**: Alternating cold (glacial) and warm (interglacial) periods over tens of thousands of years driven by Milankovitch cycles.

**Global Warming Potential (GWP)**: Measure of how much heat a greenhouse gas traps relative to CO₂ over specified timeframe (typically 100 years).

**Greenhouse Effect**: Trapping of outgoing infrared radiation by greenhouse gases, warming Earth's surface; natural effect makes Earth habitable; human activities enhance it.

**Greenhouse Gas**: Gas that absorbs and emits infrared radiation, warming Earth's surface. Major ones: water vapor, CO₂, methane, nitrous oxide, fluorinated gases.

## H

**Hydrosphere**: All water on Earth - oceans (97%), freshwater lakes/rivers, groundwater, water vapor.

## I

**Ice-Albedo Feedback**: Positive feedback where ice/snow melt reduces reflectivity, causing more solar absorption and further warming and melting.

**Insolation**: Incoming solar radiation; varies with latitude, season, and orbital parameters.

**IPCC (Intergovernmental Panel on Climate Change)**: UN body assessing climate science; produces comprehensive assessment reports every 6-7 years.

## J

**Jet Stream**: Fast-flowing air current at tropopause (~10 km altitude, 200-400 km/h); steers weather systems.

## K

**Keeling Curve**: Graph showing continuous rise in atmospheric CO₂ measured at Mauna Loa, Hawaii since 1958; iconic evidence of anthropogenic CO₂ increase.

**Kyoto Protocol**: 1997 international treaty committing industrialized countries to emission reductions; first phase 2008-2012.

## L

**La Niña**: Cool phase of ENSO; cooler-than-normal sea surface temperatures in eastern tropical Pacific; typically brings wet conditions to Australia/SE Asia, dry to Americas.

**Lithosphere**: Earth's solid outer layer - rocks, soil, minerals, tectonic plates.

## M

**Maladaptation**: Adaptation action that inadvertently increases vulnerability or has adverse outcomes (e.g., seawall preventing natural coastal ecosystem migration).

**Methane (CH₄)**: Potent greenhouse gas (28-34x CO₂ over 100 years); sources include livestock, natural gas leaks, rice cultivation, wetlands; accounts for ~18% of warming.

**Milankovitch Cycles**: Long-term variations in Earth's orbit affecting climate over tens of thousands of years - eccentricity (100k years), obliquity (41k years), precession (26k years).

**Mitigation**: Actions to reduce greenhouse gas emissions or enhance carbon sinks to limit extent of climate change.

## N

**Net Primary Productivity (NPP)**: Rate of carbon uptake by photosynthesis minus plant respiration; measure of ecosystem productivity.

**Net-Zero**: Achieving balance between greenhouse gas emissions and removals from atmosphere; required by ~2050 to limit warming to 1.5°C.

**Nitrous Oxide (N₂O)**: Greenhouse gas (265-298x CO₂ over 100 years); primarily from agricultural fertilizers; accounts for ~4% of warming.

## O

**Ocean Acidification**: Decrease in ocean pH from absorbing atmospheric CO₂; pH dropped 0.1 units (30% more acidic) since pre-industrial; threatens shell-forming organisms.

**Ocean Circulation**: Movement of ocean water driven by temperature, salinity, and wind; redistributes heat globally; includes surface currents and deep thermohaline circulation.

**Ozone (O₃)**: In stratosphere, absorbs harmful UV radiation (ozone layer); at surface, harmful air pollutant.

**Ozone Layer**: Region of stratosphere (15-35 km altitude) with high O₃ concentration; protects life from UV radiation; damaged by CFCs; recovering due to Montreal Protocol.

## P

**Paris Agreement**: 2015 international climate treaty; goal to limit warming to well below 2°C, ideally 1.5°C; countries submit Nationally Determined Contributions (NDCs).

**Parts Per Million (ppm)**: Unit of concentration; atmospheric CO₂ currently ~420 ppm (0.042%) vs 280 ppm pre-industrial.

**Permafrost**: Permanently frozen ground; contains ~1,700 Gt carbon; thawing releases greenhouse gases, creating positive feedback.

**Phenology**: Timing of seasonal biological events (flowering, migration, breeding); shifting earlier with warming, causing potential mismatches.

**Photosynthesis**: Process by which plants/algae use sunlight to convert CO₂ and water into organic matter and oxygen; removes ~120 Gt C/year from atmosphere.

## R

**Radiative Forcing**: See Forcing.

**Range Shift**: Geographic movement of species' distribution in response to climate change; generally poleward and upward in elevation.

**Renewable Energy**: Energy from sources that naturally replenish - solar, wind, hydropower, geothermal, sustainable bioenergy.

**Resilience**: Capacity of system to absorb disturbance and reorganize while maintaining essential function and identity.

**Respiration**: Process by which organisms break down organic matter, releasing CO₂; plant/soil respiration returns ~120 Gt C/year to atmosphere.

## S

**Sea Level Rise**: Increase in ocean surface height from thermal expansion and ice melt; risen 21-24 cm since 1880; currently rising 3.7 mm/year.

**Sequestration**: See Carbon Sequestration.

**Stratosphere**: Atmospheric layer 12-50 km altitude; contains ozone layer; temperature increases with altitude.

## T

**Thermohaline Circulation**: Large-scale ocean circulation driven by density differences from temperature and salinity; transports heat globally; includes Atlantic Meridional Overturning Circulation (AMOC).

**Tipping Point**: Critical threshold beyond which system undergoes rapid, potentially irreversible change (e.g., Amazon rainforest to savanna, ice sheet collapse).

**Troposphere**: Lowest atmospheric layer (0-12 km); contains 80% of atmospheric mass; all weather occurs here; temperature decreases with altitude.

**Tundra**: Biome characterized by cold temperatures, short growing seasons, permafrost, low-growing vegetation; found at high latitudes and high elevations.

## V

**Volcanic Forcing**: Climate effect from volcanic eruptions; sulfur dioxide forms stratospheric aerosol layer reflecting sunlight, causing temporary cooling (1-3 years).

## W

**Water Vapor**: Most important greenhouse gas; concentration controlled by temperature (Clausius-Clapeyron relation); acts as amplifying feedback - warmer air holds more water vapor, enhancing warming.

**Weather**: Atmospheric conditions (temperature, precipitation, wind) at specific time and place; short-term (hours to weeks) vs climate (long-term average).

**Wetlands**: Ecosystems where water covers soil or is present at/near surface; include marshes, swamps, bogs; provide water filtration, flood control, carbon storage.

## Climate Targets Quick Reference

| Target | Global Emissions Trajectory | Impacts |
|--------|---------------------------|---------|
| **1.5°C** | Net-zero CO₂ by ~2050; -45% by 2030 vs 2010 | More manageable impacts; some irreversible changes |
| **2.0°C** | Net-zero CO₂ by ~2070; -25% by 2030 vs 2010 | Significantly more severe impacts; higher tipping point risks |
| **3.0°C** | Current policy trajectory | Severe widespread impacts; multiple tipping points likely |
