# Water & Food Systems

## Global Water

### Distribution
- Total water on Earth: ~1.4 billion km³
- Saltwater (oceans): 97.5%
- Freshwater: 2.5% (~35 million km³)
  - Glaciers/ice caps: 69% of freshwater (locked)
  - Groundwater: 30%
  - Surface (rivers/lakes): ~0.3%
  - **Available freshwater:** <1% of total water

### Water Use

| Sector | % of Global Freshwater Withdrawal |
|---|---|
| Agriculture (irrigation) | ~70% |
| Industry | ~19% |
| Municipal/domestic | ~11% |

Global withdrawal: ~4,000 km³/year; about 20% of accessible renewable freshwater.

### Water Stress
- **Water stress:** >25% of renewable freshwater withdrawn
- **Water scarcity:** >40% withdrawn
- **Absolute scarcity:** <500 m³/person/year

| Region | Status |
|---|---|
| MENA (Middle East/North Africa) | Most water-stressed region globally; 12 of 17 most water-stressed countries |
| South Asia (India, Pakistan) | Groundwater depletion crisis; Indus and Ganges basins |
| Western US | Colorado River over-allocated; aquifer depletion |
| Northern China | North China Plain aquifer declining 1-3m/year |
| Sub-Saharan Africa | Infrastructure scarcity more than physical scarcity |
| Australia | Drought-prone; Murray-Darling Basin conflicts |

### Water Conflicts and Crises

**Transboundary Water Disputes**
- ~263 transboundary river basins (shared by 2+ countries); ~60% have no cooperative management framework
- Nile: Ethiopia's Grand Ethiopian Renaissance Dam (GERD) threatens Egypt's water supply (90% of Egypt's water from Nile); Egypt considers dam existential threat; no agreement despite decade of negotiations
- Indus: Treaty between India/Pakistan (1960, World Bank-brokered) under strain; climate change reducing Himalayan glaciers that feed both countries; nuclear-armed neighbors with water dependency
- Tigris/Euphrates: Turkey's GAP dam project reduced downstream flow to Syria/Iraq by ~40%; contributed to Syrian agricultural collapse pre-civil war (2006-2010 drought displaced 1.5M farmers)
- Mekong: China's 11 dams on upper Mekong reduce flow/sediment to downstream Vietnam, Cambodia, Laos, Thailand; threatens fisheries that feed 60M people
- Colorado River: Over-allocated since 1922 compact (based on abnormally wet years); Lake Mead at historic lows; mandatory cuts to Arizona, Nevada; Mexico gets dregs

**Cape Town Day Zero (2018)**
- 3-year drought → reservoir levels dropped to ~13%; city of 4M nearly ran out of water
- Emergency measures: 50L/person/day limit (normal: 200L); agricultural cuts; emergency desalination
- Avoided "Day Zero" (complete shutoff) through extreme conservation; water demand dropped 50%
- Lesson: Major modern city can approach water failure; climate change makes this more common

**Chennai, India (2019)**
- Reservoirs dried completely; water trucked in from 200+ km away; 9M people affected
- Cause: Drought + groundwater overextraction + urbanization of lake catchment areas + poor management
- Pattern: Indian cities increasingly hitting water crises (Bangalore, Delhi, Hyderabad all at risk)

## Water Infrastructure

### Treatment and Distribution
- **Source:** Surface water (rivers, lakes, reservoirs) or groundwater (wells, aquifers)
- **Treatment plant:** Coagulation → flocculation → sedimentation → filtration → disinfection (chlorine, UV, ozone)
- **Distribution:** Pressurized pipe network; storage tanks/towers; fire hydrants
- **Monitoring:** Continuous water quality testing; residual chlorine, turbidity, contaminants

### Sanitation
- **Sewage collection:** Gravity-fed pipe network → treatment plant
- **Wastewater treatment:**
  - Primary: Physical removal of solids (screening, settling)
  - Secondary: Biological treatment (activated sludge, trickling filters) — removes ~85% of organic matter
  - Tertiary: Advanced treatment (nutrients removal, disinfection) — produces water safe for reuse or environmental discharge
- **Septic systems:** Decentralized; common in rural areas; require maintenance
- ~3.6 billion people lack safely managed sanitation (2022)

### Desalination
- Converts seawater → freshwater; removes ~99% of salt
- **Reverse osmosis (RO):** Dominant technology (~70% of global capacity); pressure forces water through semi-permeable membrane
- Global capacity: ~100 million m³/day (2023); ~1% of global freshwater use
- Leaders: Saudi Arabia, UAE, Israel, Spain, US (California, Florida)
- **Israel:** ~80% of domestic water from desalination; most water-efficient country
- Cost: ~$0.50-1.50/m³ (declining but still 2-5x conventional water treatment)
- Energy-intensive: 3-6 kWh/m³; coupling with renewables reduces carbon footprint
- Brine disposal: Concentrated salt waste → environmental concern for marine ecosystems

## Agriculture as System

### The Green Revolution (1960s-1980s)
- Norman Borlaug: Developed high-yield dwarf wheat varieties; "saved a billion lives"
- Package: Improved seeds + synthetic fertilizers + irrigation + pesticides
- Results: Cereal yields doubled in developing world (1960-2000); India went from famine threat to grain exporter
- **Critique:** Monoculture dependence, chemical runoff, water depletion, reduced biodiversity, favored large farms

### Modern Agricultural System

**Inputs:**
- **Seeds:** Increasingly hybrid/GMO; major companies: Bayer/Monsanto, Corteva, Syngenta
- **Fertilizer:** Nitrogen (Haber-Bosch process: converts atmospheric N₂ → ammonia; uses ~1-2% of global energy; feeds ~50% of world population indirectly), Phosphorus (mined; finite reserves; Morocco has ~70% of reserves), Potassium
- **Water:** Irrigation = 70% of freshwater use; center-pivot, drip (most efficient), flood (least efficient)
- **Pesticides:** Herbicides, insecticides, fungicides; ~$65B global market
- **Mechanization:** Tractors, harvesters, drones; US farm labor is <2% of workforce (was >50% in 1870)

**Outputs:**
- ~10 billion tonnes of food produced annually
- ~8 billion tonnes for human consumption (directly or via livestock)
- Livestock: ~77% of agricultural land but only ~18% of calories and ~37% of protein

### Food Supply Chain
- Production → Storage → Processing → Transport → Retail → Consumer
- Cold chain: Continuous temperature control from production to consumption; critical for perishables; ~40% of food in developing countries lost due to inadequate cold chain
- Food waste: ~1.3 billion tonnes/year (~1/3 of production); split between field losses (developing world) and consumer waste (developed world)
- Processing: ~$4.2T global food processing industry; transforms raw commodities into consumer products; consolidation intense (top 10 food companies control ~10% of global food revenue)
- Retail: Supermarket revolution spreading globally; displaces traditional wet markets/small farmers; Walmart, Carrefour, Tesco in developing world

### Food Security

| Metric | Status |
|---|---|
| Global calories produced | Enough for ~10 billion (surplus exists) |
| Hungry/undernourished | ~735 million (2022); increasing since 2015 |
| Overweight/obese | ~2.5 billion adults |
| Problem | Distribution, poverty, conflict — not total supply |

### Food System Economics

**Global Agricultural Trade**
- ~$2.1T/year in agricultural trade (2023); grown 3-4x in 20 years
- Top exporters: US, EU, Brazil, China, Canada, Indonesia, Australia
- Top importers: EU, China, US, Japan, UK
- 3 crops dominate global trade: Wheat, rice, corn (maize) — together provide ~50% of global calories

**Commodity Market Dynamics**

| Commodity | Top Exporter | % of Global Exports | Key Risk |
|---|---|---|---|
| Wheat | Russia (~20%), then EU, Australia, Canada, US | Russia/Ukraine together ~28% | 2022 Ukraine war → wheat price spike 50%+; export blockade threatened famine in Africa/MENA |
| Rice | India (~40%), Thailand, Vietnam | India export ban (2023) caused price spike | India feeds ~1.8B globally via exports; one policy decision affects food security worldwide |
| Corn | US (~30%), Brazil, Argentina | Concentration in Americas | Ethanol mandate diverts ~40% of US corn crop to fuel; food vs fuel debate |
| Soybeans | Brazil (~50%), US, Argentina | Brazil deforestation link | ~80% of global soy → animal feed; meat industry drives soy demand |
| Palm oil | Indonesia (~55%), Malaysia (~30%) | Duopoly | Deforestation, biodiversity loss; in ~50% of supermarket products |

**Farm Economics**
- Small farms (<2 hectares): 84% of world's farms but only ~35% of food production; declining everywhere
- Farm consolidation: US average farm size tripled since 1950; ~4% of US farms produce >66% of output
- Farm subsidies: US ~$20B/year, EU ~€55B/year (CAP); distort global markets; benefit large farms disproportionately; suppress developing country farmers' competitiveness
- Farmer indebtedness: US farm debt ~$520B; Indian farmer suicides (~12,000/year) linked to debt, crop failure, input costs
- Seed market concentration: Top 4 companies (Bayer/Monsanto, Corteva, ChemChina/Syngenta, BASF) control ~60% of global seed market and ~75% of agrochemicals

**Food Price Volatility**
- 2007-08 food price crisis: Wheat +130%, rice +74%; triggered riots in 30+ countries; multiple causes (biofuel mandates, export bans, speculation, drought, oil prices)
- 2022 food price crisis: Ukraine war + fertilizer price spike + drought → FAO food price index hit all-time high; disproportionately affected import-dependent countries (Egypt, Lebanon, Somalia)
- Export ban cascade: When prices spike, exporting countries ban exports to protect domestic supply → worsens global shortage → more bans → vicious cycle

**Livestock Economy**
- Global meat production: ~350M tonnes/year; growing ~2%/year; driven by rising incomes in Asia
- Feed conversion ratios: Chicken 2:1 (kg feed per kg meat), pork 4:1, beef 8:1 → beef least efficient protein by far
- Land use: Livestock + feed crops = ~77% of agricultural land but only 18% of calories, 37% of protein
- Water footprint: 1 kg beef = 15,400 liters; 1 kg chicken = 4,300 liters; 1 kg lentils = 50 liters
- Environmental impact: Livestock ~14.5% of global GHG emissions (methane from ruminants + feed production + land use change)
- Alternative proteins: Plant-based meat ($8B market, 2023; slowing growth after initial hype); cultivated/lab-grown meat (first regulatory approvals 2023; far from cost-competitive); insects (2B people already eat them; growing Western interest); precision fermentation

**The Fertilizer Dependency**
- Haber-Bosch (synthetic nitrogen): Most important invention of 20th century; enables ~50% of world food production; without it, ~3-4 billion people couldn't be fed
- Natural gas → ammonia: ~60% of fertilizer production cost is gas; Russia/Ukraine war → gas price spike → fertilizer price doubled → reduced application in developing world → lower yields → food insecurity cascade
- Phosphorus: Mined from phosphate rock; Morocco+Western Sahara have ~70% of reserves; finite and no substitute; "peak phosphorus" debated (2030-2070)
- Potassium: Russia + Belarus = ~40% of global supply; sanctions disrupted trade (2022)

## The Water-Energy-Food Nexus

These three systems are deeply interdependent:

- **Water for energy:** Cooling power plants (~40% of US freshwater withdrawal); hydroelectric; fracking
- **Energy for water:** Pumping, treatment, desalination (~4% of global electricity)
- **Water for food:** Irrigation (70% of freshwater)
- **Energy for food:** Fertilizer production, mechanization, processing, transport, refrigeration (~30% of global energy)
- **Food for energy:** Biofuels (corn ethanol, palm oil biodiesel)

**Stress in one → cascading effects on others.** Example: Drought → reduces hydroelectric output → raises electricity prices → increases irrigation pumping costs → reduces food production.

## Key Challenges

1. **Groundwater depletion:** Ogallala Aquifer (US Great Plains) declining ~1m/year in places; Punjab (India/Pakistan) water table dropping; once depleted, recovery takes centuries-millennia
2. **Soil degradation:** ~33% of global soils degraded; erosion, salinization, compaction, loss of organic matter; topsoil forms at 1cm per 200-1000 years; lost far faster
3. **Phosphorus peak:** Finite resource; estimates of peak production range from 2030 to 2070; no substitute in agriculture
4. **Pollinator decline:** ~75% of crop species benefit from animal pollination; bee colonies declining from pesticides, habitat loss, disease
5. **Climate impact on agriculture:** +2°C → significant yield declines in tropics; shifting growing zones; more extreme weather → crop failures
6. **Antibiotic use in livestock:** ~73% of antibiotics sold are for farm animals; drives resistance that affects human medicine

## Agriculture: Advanced Topics

### Precision Agriculture
- GPS-guided tractors: Sub-centimeter accuracy; auto-steer reduces overlap/waste
- Variable-rate application: Sensors map soil nutrient levels → fertilizer/pesticide applied only where needed → 15-30% input reduction
- Drones: Crop monitoring (NDVI imaging), targeted spraying; $5-15/hectare vs $50+ manual scouting
- IoT soil sensors: Real-time moisture, temperature, nutrient monitoring; automated irrigation scheduling
- Satellite monitoring: Sentinel-2, Planet Labs; field-level yield prediction; increasingly accessible to large commercial farms but cost-prohibitive for smallholders

### Irrigation Systems Compared

| System | Water Efficiency | Cost | Best For |
|---|---|---|---|
| Flood/furrow | 30-50% | Low | Rice paddies, low-value crops; developing world |
| Sprinkler (center-pivot) | 70-85% | Medium | Large flat fields; Great Plains, Saudi Arabia |
| Drip | 90-95% | High | High-value crops (fruits, vegetables); water-scarce regions |
| Subsurface drip | 95%+ | Very high | Premium applications; reduces evaporation to near-zero |

- Israel pioneered drip irrigation (Netafim, 1965); now used globally; most water-efficient but requires clean water, maintenance, capital
- Ogallala Aquifer under US Great Plains: Supports $20B/year in agriculture; declining 1m/year in Kansas; some areas already exhausted; no policy to manage depletion rate

### Aquaculture
- Fastest-growing food sector: ~50% of fish consumed now farmed (vs wild-caught)
- Global production: ~90M tonnes/year (2023); vs ~90M wild catch (stagnant since 1990s)
- Top producers: China (~60% of global aquaculture), India, Indonesia, Vietnam, Bangladesh
- Species: Shrimp, salmon, tilapia, catfish, carp; increasingly diversified
- Concerns: Disease outbreaks (sea lice in salmon), antibiotic use, mangrove destruction (shrimp farms), feed from wild fish (improving with plant-based feeds), nutrient pollution
- Recirculating aquaculture systems (RAS): Indoor, controlled environment; near-zero water discharge; salmon farms in landlocked countries; high capital cost

### Future of Agriculture

| Technology | Potential | Status |
|---|---|---|
| Gene editing (CRISPR) | Disease-resistant, climate-adapted, higher-yield crops | Regulatory barriers vary; less contentious than GMO; accelerating |
| Vertical farming | 95% less water, year-round, pesticide-free; leafy greens | Growing fast but only economical for high-value crops; energy-intensive |
| Cellular agriculture | Meat without animals; 99% less land/water | First approvals 2023; decades from price parity |
| Microbiome engineering | Soil health, nutrient fixation without synthetic fertilizer | Early research; Pivot Bio nitrogen-fixing microbes |
| Robotics | Automated weeding, harvesting (strawberries, apples); addresses labor shortages | Deploying at scale for simple tasks; complex harvesting harder |
| Carbon farming | Soil carbon sequestration; regenerative agriculture | Carbon credit markets growing; measurement/verification challenges |
