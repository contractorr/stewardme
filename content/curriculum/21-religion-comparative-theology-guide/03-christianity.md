# Abrahamic Religions II: Christianity

## Overview

Christianity: World's largest religion (2.4 billion, 31% of global population). Founded 1st century CE on teachings of Jesus of Nazareth. Core: Jesus is Son of God, died for humanity's sins, resurrected. Spread from Jewish sect → Roman state religion (313 CE) → global faith. Shaped Western civilization: law, art, politics, ethics, education. Major divisions: Catholic (1.3B), Protestant (900M), Orthodox (260M).

---

## Jesus of Nazareth (c. 4 BCE - 30 CE)

### Historical Context

**Roman Judea:** Occupied; resentment toward Rome; messianic expectations; sectarian divisions (Pharisees, Sadducees, Essenes, Zealots)

**Sources:**
- **Gospels (Matthew, Mark, Luke, John):** Written 70-110 CE; theological, not biographical; synoptic problem (Matthew, Mark, Luke share material)
- **Paul's Letters:** Earliest Christian writings (50s CE); theology, not biography
- **Non-Christian:** Josephus (Jewish historian), Tacitus (Roman historian) — brief mentions; confirm crucifixion

**Historical Consensus:**
- Jewish preacher in Galilee
- Baptized by John the Baptist
- Taught about Kingdom of God
- Attracted followers (12 apostles)
- Crucified by Romans (Pontius Pilate) c. 30 CE
- Followers claimed resurrection

**Disputed:**
- Virgin birth, miracles, resurrection (faith claims, not historical verification)
- Exact teachings (oral tradition → written decades later; theological editing)

---

### Life & Ministry (per Gospels)

**Birth:** Bethlehem; virgin birth (Mary); visited by shepherds, magi

**Early Life:** Raised in Nazareth; carpenter's son

**Baptism:** By John the Baptist in Jordan River; Holy Spirit descends

**Temptation:** 40 days in wilderness; tempted by Satan

**Ministry (~3 years):**
- **Teaching:** Parables (Good Samaritan, Prodigal Son, Sower); Sermon on Mount (Beatitudes, Golden Rule)
- **Miracles:** Healing, exorcisms, walking on water, feeding 5,000, raising Lazarus
- **Disciples:** 12 apostles (Peter, James, John, Judas, etc.); inner circle of Peter/James/John

**Core Message:**
- **Kingdom of God:** Imminent; repent and believe
- **Love:** Love God; love neighbor as self (summarizes law)
- **Forgiveness:** Forgive others as God forgives you
- **Humility:** Blessed are meek, poor in spirit; first shall be last
- **Radical Ethics:** Turn other cheek; love enemies; give to poor

---

### Passion & Resurrection

**Last Supper:** Passover meal; institutes Eucharist (bread = body, wine = blood)

**Gethsemane:** Prays; betrayed by Judas; arrested

**Trial:** Jewish Sanhedrin (blasphemy for claiming to be Son of God) → Pontius Pilate (political threat: "King of Jews")

**Crucifixion:** Roman execution method; died on cross; buried by Joseph of Arimathea

**Resurrection:** Tomb empty on third day; appears to disciples; ascends to heaven

**Theological Significance:**
- Death = atonement for sin (substitutionary sacrifice)
- Resurrection = defeat of death; promise of eternal life
- Salvation via faith in Jesus, not just observance of law

## Paul & Early Church (30-100 CE)

### Apostle Paul (c. 5-67 CE)

**Background:** Saul of Tarsus; Pharisee; persecuted Christians

**Conversion:** Damascus Road experience (c. 33 CE); Jesus appears in vision; becomes missionary

**Mission:** Three missionary journeys (Asia Minor, Greece); founded churches; wrote letters (epistles)

**Theology:**
- **Justification by Faith:** Salvation through faith in Jesus, not obedience to Jewish law (Torah)
- **Grace:** Unmerited favor; God's gift, not earned
- **Gentile Inclusion:** Non-Jews can be Christians without becoming Jews (no circumcision, no kosher laws)
- **Body of Christ:** Church as unified body; members interdependent
- **Christ as Second Adam:** Undoes Adam's sin

**Council of Jerusalem (c. 50 CE):** Paul vs Peter/James; decided Gentiles don't need to follow Jewish law (except basic ethics)

**Death:** Likely executed in Rome under Nero (64-67 CE)

**Legacy:** Shaped Christian theology; 13 New Testament letters attributed (7 undisputed, 6 disputed authorship)

---

### Spread & Persecution

**Geographic:** Jerusalem → Antioch → Asia Minor → Greece → Rome → North Africa → beyond

**Factors:**
- **Universal Message:** Open to all (not ethnic)
- **Urban Networks:** Spread via trade routes, cities
- **Social Appeal:** Radical equality ("no Jew nor Greek, slave nor free, male nor female"); care for poor/sick
- **Organization:** Bishops, deacons; mutual aid
- **Roman Roads:** Infrastructure enabled travel

**Persecutions:**
- **Nero (64 CE):** Blamed Christians for fire; torture, executions
- **Domitian (90s CE):** Enforced emperor worship; Christians refused
- **Decius (250 CE):** Empire-wide persecution; required sacrifice to emperor
- **Diocletian (303-311 CE):** "Great Persecution"; destroyed churches, scriptures; thousands killed
- **Martyrdom:** Seen as imitating Christ; inspired converts ("blood of martyrs is seed of the Church" — Tertullian)

---

## Constantine & Christendom (313-600 CE)

### Edict of Milan (313 CE)

- Constantine I (+ Licinius) legalized Christianity
- End of persecution; property restored
- Christianity = tolerated religion

**Constantine's Conversion:**
- Battle of Milvian Bridge (312): Vision of cross; "In this sign, conquer"
- Political calculation + genuine belief (debated)
- Baptized on deathbed (337)

---

### Council of Nicaea (325 CE)

**Issue:** Arian Controversy
- **Arius:** Jesus created by God; subordinate
- **Athanasius:** Jesus co-eternal with God; same substance (homoousios)

**Outcome:** Nicene Creed; affirmed Trinity (Father, Son, Holy Spirit co-equal, co-eternal); Arianism condemned

**Significance:** Imperial church councils; orthodoxy defined; state enforces

---

### Theodosius I (380-395 CE)

- Christianity = state religion (Edict of Thessalonica, 380)
- Pagan worship banned
- Christianity = official; paganism = persecuted

---

### Early Controversies & Councils

| Council | Year | Issue | Outcome |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Nicaea I** | 325 | Nature of Christ (Arianism) | Nicene Creed; Jesus = divine |
| **Constantinople I** | 381 | Holy Spirit divinity; Trinity | Nicene Creed expanded; Trinity affirmed |
| **Ephesus** | 431 | Mary as "Mother of God" (Theotokos) vs "Mother of Christ" | Theotokos affirmed; Nestorius condemned |
| **Chalcedon** | 451 | Christ's nature: one or two? | Two natures (human + divine) in one person; Monophysites split |

**Result:** Orthodoxy = Nicene Christianity; alternative views = heresies

---

### Church Fathers

| Figure | Dates | Contribution |
|---|---|---|
| **Augustine of Hippo** | 354-430 | *Confessions*, *City of God*; original sin; predestination; just war |
| **Jerome** | 347-420 | Translated Bible into Latin (Vulgate); standard for 1,000+ years |
| **Ambrose** | 340-397 | Bishop; defied emperor; church-state relations |
| **John Chrysostom** | 347-407 | "Golden-mouthed"; preacher; liturgy; monastic reform |
| **Gregory the Great** | 540-604 | Pope; Gregorian chant; missions; administrative reforms |

## Medieval Christianity (600-1500)

### East-West Schism (1054)

**Causes:**
- **Theological:** Filioque (Holy Spirit proceeds from Father "and Son" — Western addition to Creed)
- **Papal Authority:** Pope claims universal jurisdiction; East rejects
- **Cultural:** Latin West vs Greek East; different liturgies, practices
- **Political:** Rivalry between Rome and Constantinople

**Split:**
- **Catholic Church:** Rome; Pope; Latin; Western Europe
- **Orthodox Church:** Constantinople; Patriarch; Greek; Eastern Europe, Russia, Middle East

**Not Formally Healed:** 1965 mutual excommunications lifted but still separate

---

### Crusades (1095-1291)

**First Crusade (1095-1099):** Pope Urban II; recapture Jerusalem from Muslims; succeeded; brutal sack of Jerusalem

**Later Crusades:** Most failed; Fourth Crusade (1204) sacked Constantinople (Christian city); deepened East-West rift

**Effects:**
- **Religious Conflict:** Christians vs Muslims; also vs Jews (massacres en route)
- **Trade:** European contact with East; goods, ideas
- **Military Orders:** Knights Templar, Knights Hospitaller

---

### Scholasticism (1100-1500)

**Goal:** Reconcile faith and reason; use philosophy (Aristotle) to understand theology

**Thomas Aquinas (1225-1274):**
- *Summa Theologica*: Comprehensive theology
- **Five Ways:** Proofs of God's existence (cosmological, teleological, etc.)
- **Natural Law:** Moral law accessible via reason, independent of revelation
- **Just War Theory:** Developed from Augustine

**Universities:** Paris, Oxford, Bologna; Church-founded; theology "queen of sciences"

---

### Monasticism

**Benedict of Nursia (480-547):** Benedictine Rule; ora et labora (pray and work); monasteries preserved learning

**Franciscans (1209):** Francis of Assisi; poverty, service to poor, nature

**Dominicans (1216):** Preaching, scholarship; Aquinas was Dominican

**Role:** Preserved texts, copied manuscripts, schools, hospitals, agriculture

---

### Inquisition (1184 onward)

**Purpose:** Root out heresy

**Methods:** Interrogation, torture, execution (burning)

**Targets:** Cathars, Waldensians, later Jews/Muslims (Spanish Inquisition, 1478)

**Legacy:** Symbol of religious oppression; historical abuses acknowledged by Church

---

## Protestant Reformation (1517-1648)

### Martin Luther (1483-1546)

**Background:** German monk; Augustinian; troubled by sin, salvation

**95 Theses (1517):** Posted in Wittenberg; attacked indulgences (paying for reduced time in purgatory)

**Theology:**
- **Sola Fide (Faith Alone):** Justification by faith, not works
- **Sola Scriptura (Scripture Alone):** Bible is sole authority, not Church tradition
- **Priesthood of All Believers:** Direct access to God; no need for priestly mediation
- **Two Sacraments:** Baptism, Eucharist only (vs Catholic seven)

**Excommunication (1521):** Refused to recant; declared heretic; protected by German princes

**Translation:** Bible into German; made accessible to laity

**Legacy:** Founded Lutheranism; split Western Christianity

---

### John Calvin (1509-1564)

**Location:** Geneva; created theocracy

**Theology:**
- **Predestination:** God predetermined who is saved/damned; humans can't change it
- **Total Depravity:** Humans completely sinful; incapable of good without grace
- **Irresistible Grace:** Elect cannot resist God's call
- **TULIP:** Five points of Calvinism (Total depravity, Unconditional election, Limited atonement, Irresistible grace, Perseverance of saints)

**Work Ethic:** Success = sign of election (Weber's Protestant ethic thesis)

**Legacy:** Reformed tradition; Presbyterianism; Puritans

---

### Other Reformers

**Huldrych Zwingli (1484-1531):** Swiss; reformed Zürich; symbolic Eucharist (vs Luther's "real presence")

**Anabaptists:** Radical; adult baptism only; separation of church-state; pacifism; persecuted by both Catholics and Protestants

---

### English Reformation

**Henry VIII (r. 1509-1547):** Wanted annulment from Catherine of Aragon; Pope refused; broke with Rome (1534)

**Act of Supremacy (1534):** King = head of Church of England (Anglican Church)

**Elizabeth I (r. 1558-1603):** "Elizabethan Settlement"; via media (middle way) between Catholic and Protestant

---

### Catholic Counter-Reformation

**Council of Trent (1545-1563):** Responded to Protestantism
- Reaffirmed traditional doctrines (seven sacraments, transubstantiation, purgatory)
- Banned indulgence abuses
- Improved clergy education

**Jesuits (Society of Jesus, 1540):** Ignatius of Loyola; education, missions, loyalty to Pope; Counter-Reformation shock troops

**Inquisition Intensified:** Combated Protestantism; Index of Forbidden Books

---

### Wars of Religion (1524-1648)

**German Peasants' War (1524-1525):** Inspired by Reformation; crushed; 100,000 dead

**Thirty Years' War (1618-1648):** Catholic vs Protestant; devastated Germany; 8 million dead

**Peace of Westphalia (1648):** Ended wars; established cuius regio, eius religio (ruler's religion = state religion); birth of modern state system

---

## Global Spread (1500-1900)

### Colonial Missions

**Spanish/Portuguese:** Americas, Philippines; forced conversions; syncretism (Catholicism + indigenous beliefs)

**Jesuits:** China (Matteo Ricci), Japan, India, Latin America; "accommodation" (adapt Christianity to local culture)

**Protestant Missions (1800s):** Africa, Asia; often linked to colonialism but also education, healthcare

**Effects:**
- **Cultural Destruction:** Indigenous religions suppressed
- **Syncretism:** Blended Christianity (e.g., Vodou, Santería)
- **Education:** Mission schools; literacy
- **Global Christianity:** Now majority non-Western

---

## Major Branches

### Roman Catholicism (1.3 billion)

**Authority:** Pope (successor to Peter); bishops; magisterium (teaching authority)

**Sacraments (Seven):** Baptism, Eucharist, Confirmation, Reconciliation (Confession), Marriage, Holy Orders (ordination), Anointing of Sick

**Distinctive Beliefs:**
- **Transubstantiation:** Bread/wine become actual body/blood of Christ
- **Purgatory:** Intermediate state; purification before heaven
- **Mary:** Immaculate Conception (sinless), Assumption (taken to heaven bodily), prayers to Mary
- **Saints:** Intercession; veneration (not worship)
- **Papal Infallibility:** Pope cannot err when teaching ex cathedra on faith/morals (Vatican I, 1870)

**Vatican II (1962-1965):** Modernized Church; Mass in vernacular (not Latin); ecumenism; religious freedom

---

### Eastern Orthodoxy (260 million)

**Structure:** Autocephalous (self-governing) churches; Patriarchs; Constantinople = "first among equals"

**Distinctive Beliefs:**
- **Theosis:** Salvation = becoming like God (divinization)
- **Filioque:** Rejected; Holy Spirit proceeds from Father only
- **Icons:** Venerated; windows to heaven
- **Mary (Theotokos):** Mother of God; important but no Immaculate Conception dogma

**Liturgy:** Divine Liturgy; highly ritualized; unchanged for centuries; incense, chanting

**Schism:** 1054 from Catholic; never reunited

---

### Protestantism (900 million)

**Core Principles:**
- **Sola Scriptura:** Scripture alone authoritative
- **Sola Fide:** Justification by faith alone
- **Priesthood of All Believers:** No mediator needed
- **Two Sacraments:** Baptism, Eucharist (most; some add foot-washing)

**Major Denominations:**

| Denomination | Origin | Key Beliefs | Members |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Lutheran** | Germany, 1517 | Luther's theology; liturgical | 70M |
| **Reformed/Presbyterian** | Switzerland, 1536 | Calvinist; predestination; elder-led | 75M |
| **Anglican/Episcopal** | England, 1534 | Via media; liturgical; bishops | 85M |
| **Baptist** | England, 1600s | Believer's baptism (adult); congregational | 100M |
| **Methodist** | England, 1738 (Wesley) | Arminian (free will); holiness; missions | 80M |
| **Pentecostal** | US, 1906 | Baptism of Holy Spirit; speaking in tongues; healing | 280M |
| **Adventist** | US, 1860s | Saturday Sabbath; Second Coming imminent | 22M |
| **Evangelical** | (diverse) | Born-again experience; Bible inerrant; missions | 300M+ |

**Non-Denominational:** Growing trend; independent churches; often megachurches

## Contemporary Issues

### Secularization

- Europe: Rapid decline; "empty churches"
- US: "Nones" (unaffiliated) growing (26% in 2020, up from 16% in 2007)
- Latin America: Evangelicals growing at Catholic expense

### Sexual Abuse Crisis (Catholic)

- Systemic abuse by clergy; cover-ups by hierarchy
- Thousands of victims; billions in settlements
- Trust collapse; attendance drop

### Women's Ordination

- Catholic/Orthodox: No women priests (theological: Jesus male, apostles male)
- Protestant: Many ordain women (Lutherans, Methodists, Presbyterians, Anglicans except conservative branches)
- Debate: Tradition vs equality

### LGBTQ+ Inclusion

- Catholic/Orthodox: Homosexual acts sinful; no same-sex marriage
- Mainline Protestant: Increasingly affirming (Episcopalians, ELCA Lutherans, Presbyterians USA)
- Evangelical: Mostly opposed; some "affirming" churches emerging
- Schisms: Anglican Communion splitting over LGBTQ+ issues

### Christian Nationalism

- US: Fusion of Christian identity + American nationalism; January 6 Capitol riot had Christian symbols
- Russia: Orthodox Church aligned with Putin; "traditional values" vs "decadent West"
- Debate: Theocracy vs pluralism

### Global South Shift

- Africa: 650M Christians (2020); fastest growth
- Latin America: 600M (but declining share)
- Asia: 380M (growing)
- Christianity now majority non-Western; more conservative than Western churches

## Key Terms

**Trinity:** One God in three persons (Father, Son, Holy Spirit); co-equal, co-eternal

**Incarnation:** God became human in Jesus

**Atonement:** Jesus' death reconciles humanity to God; pays for sin

**Resurrection:** Jesus rose from dead; defeated death

**Salvation:** Deliverance from sin; eternal life; via faith (Protestant) or faith + works (Catholic)

**Eucharist/Communion:** Ritual meal; bread and wine; commemorates Last Supper

**Baptism:** Initiation rite; water; symbol of death/rebirth

**Sacrament:** Outward sign of inward grace; Catholics = 7, Protestants = 2

**Pope:** Bishop of Rome; Catholic leader; claims universal authority

**Ecumenism:** Movement for Christian unity; dialogue between denominations

---

## Summary

Christianity: 2.4 billion adherents; largest religion globally. Founded on Jesus of Nazareth (crucified c. 30 CE), proclaimed resurrected. Paul spread to Gentiles; faith, not law. Became Roman state religion (313 CE); defined orthodoxy via councils (Nicaea, Chalcedon). Split East-West (1054): Catholic (Rome, Pope) vs Orthodox (Constantinople, Patriarchs). Protestant Reformation (1517): Luther, Calvin challenged Catholic authority; sola fide, sola scriptura. Colonial missions spread globally (1500-1900); now majority non-Western. Three major branches: Catholic (1.3B, Pope, seven sacraments, tradition + scripture), Orthodox (260M, theosis, icons, liturgy), Protestant (900M, diverse, scripture alone, two sacraments). Contemporary: Secularization in West, growth in Global South, debates over women/LGBTQ+, sexual abuse crisis, Christian nationalism. Shaped Western civilization: law, art, universities, ethics, politics.
