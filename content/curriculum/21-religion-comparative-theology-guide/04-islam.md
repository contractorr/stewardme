# Abrahamic Religions III: Islam

## Overview

Islam: 1.9 billion adherents (24% global); second-largest, fastest-growing religion. Founded 7th century CE Arabia by Prophet Muhammad. Core: One God (Allah), Muhammad final prophet, Quran divine word. Five Pillars structure practice. Spread from Arabia → Middle East, North Africa, Central/South Asia, Southeast Asia in centuries. Major split: Sunni (85-90%) vs Shia (10-15%). Shaped mathematics, science, philosophy, architecture, law (Sharia). Contemporary: Political Islam, terrorism debates, secularism tensions.

---

## Muhammad & Origins (570-632 CE)

### Pre-Islamic Arabia

**Geography:** Arabian Peninsula; desert; trade routes; Mecca = commercial hub

**Religion:** Polytheism; Kaaba (Mecca) housed 360+ idols; also Jews, Christians, Zoroastrians

**Tribal Society:** Clans; blood feuds; oral poetry; no unified state

**Hanifs:** Monotheists rejecting idolatry; precursors to Islam

---

### Muhammad's Life

**Birth (c. 570):** Mecca; orphaned young; raised by grandfather, then uncle Abu Talib

**Marriage (595):** Khadijah (wealthy widow, 15 years older); monogamous until her death (619)

**First Revelation (610, age 40):** Cave Hira; Angel Gabriel (Jibril): "Recite!" (Iqra); Quran begins

**Early Preaching (610-622):** Monotheism, social justice, end of idolatry; small following; Meccan elite hostile (threatened trade, power)

**Hijra (622):** Emigration to Medina (Yathrib); invited to mediate tribal disputes; Year 1 of Islamic calendar

**Medina Phase (622-632):**
- Built community (ummah); mosque; Constitution of Medina (multi-religious compact)
- Military conflicts: Badr (624, Muslim victory), Uhud (625, setback), Trench (627, victory)
- Quraysh tribe (Mecca) opposed; eventually submitted
- Conquest of Mecca (630): Peaceful; destroyed idols in Kaaba; made Islamic center

**Farewell Pilgrimage (632):** Established hajj rituals; final sermon ("all Muslims equal")

**Death (632, age ~62):** Fever; died in Medina; buried under mosque

**Legacy:** Prophet, statesman, military leader; Seal of Prophets (final messenger); moral exemplar

## Quran & Hadith

### Quran

**Meaning:** "Recitation"

**Revelation:** 610-632 CE (22 years); Arabic; Angel Gabriel → Muhammad

**Structure:** 114 suras (chapters); ~6,236 ayat (verses); longest to shortest (except Fatiha)

**Content:**
- God's oneness (tawhid), prophets, judgment, heaven/hell
- Stories: Adam, Noah, Abraham, Moses, Jesus (all prophets)
- Law: Prayer, fasting, charity, marriage, inheritance, crime
- Ethics: Justice, compassion, humility

**Compilation:** Oral + written fragments → standardized under Caliph Uthman (650s)

**Inimitability (I'jaz):** Quran = miracle; linguistically/theologically perfect; humans cannot replicate

**Recitation:** Memorized by millions (hafiz); melodic recitation (tajweed); ritual importance

**Status:** Eternal, uncreated word of God (Sunni mainstream); created (Mu'tazilites, some Shia)

---

### Hadith

**Definition:** Reports of Muhammad's sayings, actions, approvals; "Sunnah" = Muhammad's example

**Structure:** Isnad (chain of transmission) + matn (content)

**Collections (Sunni):**
- **Six Books (Kutub al-Sittah):** Bukhari, Muslim (most authentic), Abu Dawood, Tirmidhi, Nasa'i, Ibn Majah
- **Sahih Bukhari, Sahih Muslim:** "Authentic" collections; ~7,000 hadith each

**Grading:** Sahih (authentic), Hasan (good), Da'if (weak), Mawdu (fabricated)

**Role:** Supplements Quran; explains rituals; legal source; biography (sira)

**Criticism:** Fabrications common; political motivations; historical reliability debated

---

## Core Beliefs (Aqidah)

### Six Articles of Faith

1. **God (Allah):** One, indivisible, omnipotent, omniscient, merciful; 99 names
2. **Angels:** Created from light; Gabriel, Michael, Israfil, Azrael; obey God perfectly
3. **Revealed Books:** Torah, Psalms, Gospel, Quran (final, uncorrupted)
4. **Prophets:** 124,000 sent; 25 named in Quran; Muhammad final; Adam first
5. **Day of Judgment:** Resurrection, accounting, heaven (Jannah) or hell (Jahannam)
6. **Divine Decree (Qadar):** God's will; predestination vs free will debated

---

### Five Pillars (Arkan al-Islam)

**Practices defining Muslim identity:**

1. **Shahada (Testimony):** "No god but Allah, Muhammad is His messenger"; declaration makes one Muslim
2. **Salat (Prayer):** Five daily prayers (Fajr, Dhuhr, Asr, Maghrib, Isha); facing Mecca; ritual ablution (wudu); prostrations
3. **Zakat (Charity):** 2.5% of wealth annually to poor; obligatory almsgiving; purifies wealth
4. **Sawm (Fasting):** Ramadan (9th month); no food/drink/sex dawn-sunset; spiritual discipline
5. **Hajj (Pilgrimage):** Mecca; once in lifetime if able; Kaaba circumambulation; 2-3 million annually

## Early Islamic History

### Rashidun Caliphs (632-661)

**"Rightly Guided":** First four caliphs; companions of Muhammad

| Caliph | Reign | Achievements | Death |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Abu Bakr** | 632-634 | Consolidated Arabia; Ridda Wars (apostasy) | Natural |
| **Umar** | 634-644 | Conquered Levant, Egypt, Persia; organized state | Assassinated |
| **Uthman** | 644-656 | Standardized Quran; expanded empire | Assassinated (civil war begins) |
| **Ali** | 656-661 | Muhammad's cousin/son-in-law; civil war; opposed by Muawiya | Assassinated |

**Expansion:** Egypt, Levant, Persia, North Africa conquered; Byzantine/Sassanid empires defeated

**Fitna (Civil War):** Uthman's assassination → Ali vs Muawiya → Battle of Siffin (657) → stalemate → arbitration → schisms

---

### Sunni-Shia Split

**Origins:** Succession dispute after Muhammad's death (632)

**Sunni Position:**
- Caliph = elected/chosen by community
- Abu Bakr rightly chosen (Muhammad's companion, close friend)
- Ali legitimate but 4th caliph
- 85-90% of Muslims

**Shia Position:**
- Leadership = divinely ordained; must be from Muhammad's family (Ahl al-Bayt)
- Ali rightful first Imam (leader); passed over unjustly
- 12 Imams (Twelver Shia, largest Shia branch); 12th in occultation (hidden), will return as Mahdi
- 10-15% of Muslims

**Battle of Karbala (680):** Husayn (Ali's son, Muhammad's grandson) killed by Umayyad forces; martyrdom; central to Shia identity; Ashura commemoration (self-flagellation, mourning)

**Theological Differences:**
- **Authority:** Sunni = Quran + Hadith + Consensus (ijma) + Analogy (qiyas); Shia = Quran + Hadith + Imams' teachings
- **Imams:** Shia = infallible, divinely guided; Sunni = Imams = prayer leaders (not infallible)
- **Martyrdom:** Central to Shia identity (Husayn); less emphasized in Sunni

---

### Umayyad Dynasty (661-750)

**Capital:** Damascus

**Expansion:** Spain, Central Asia, Indus Valley; stopped at Tours (732, France) by Charles Martel

**Arabization:** Arabic official language; administration

**Criticism:** Hereditary rule (not elected); perceived as worldly, not pious

**Overthrow (750):** Abbasids; nearly all Umayyads killed; one escaped to Spain (Cordoba Caliphate)

---

### Abbasid Dynasty (750-1258)

**Capital:** Baghdad (founded 762); cosmopolitan; multi-ethnic

**Golden Age (800-1100):**
- **Mathematics:** Algebra (al-Khwarizmi); algorithms; Arabic numerals (from India)
- **Astronomy:** Observatories; star catalogs; improved Ptolemy
- **Medicine:** Hospitals; Ibn Sina (*Canon of Medicine*); surgery
- **Philosophy:** Translated Greek texts (Aristotle, Plato); Al-Farabi, Ibn Rushd (Averroes)
- **Chemistry:** Jabir ibn Hayyan; distillation, acids
- **Literature:** *1001 Nights*; poetry

**House of Wisdom (Bayt al-Hikma):** Baghdad library; translation movement; preserved/advanced knowledge

**Decline:** Fragmentation; Mongol invasion; sack of Baghdad (1258); Black Death

---

### Ottoman Empire (1299-1922)

**Peak (1500s-1600s):** Controlled Middle East, North Africa, Southeast Europe; Istanbul capital

**Suleiman the Magnificent (r. 1520-1566):** Expanded empire; legal reforms; patron of arts

**Millet System:** Religious communities (Muslims, Christians, Jews) self-governed

**Decline (1700s-1900s):** "Sick Man of Europe"; lost territory; modernization attempts; WWI defeat; abolished 1922 (Turkey becomes republic)

**Caliphate Abolished (1924):** Ataturk; secular Turkey

## Islamic Law (Sharia)

### Sources

1. **Quran:** Divine revelation; primary
2. **Sunnah/Hadith:** Muhammad's example
3. **Ijma:** Consensus of scholars
4. **Qiyas:** Analogical reasoning

---

### Schools of Law (Madhabs — Sunni)

| School | Founder | Region | Characteristics |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Hanafi** | Abu Hanifa (d. 767) | Turkey, Central Asia, South Asia | Most liberal; reason emphasized |
| **Maliki** | Malik ibn Anas (d. 795) | North/West Africa | Medina practice important |
| **Shafi'i** | Al-Shafi'i (d. 820) | Southeast Asia, East Africa | Systematized legal theory |
| **Hanbali** | Ahmad ibn Hanbal (d. 855) | Saudi Arabia, Gulf | Most conservative; literal Quran/Hadith |

**Shia:** Ja'fari school (Twelver Shia)

---

### Scope

**Five Categories:** Obligatory (fard), recommended (mustahabb), neutral (mubah), discouraged (makruh), forbidden (haram)

**Areas:** Worship (ritual), transactions (contracts, marriage), punishments (hudud), governance, warfare

**Hudud (Fixed Punishments):** Theft (amputation), adultery (stoning/lashing), apostasy (death — debated), drinking alcohol (lashing)
- **Application:** Varies; Saudi Arabia, Iran strict; most Muslim-majority countries don't apply; debate over compatibility with human rights

---

## Sufism (Islamic Mysticism)

**Goal:** Direct experience of God; love, spiritual purification

**Practices:**
- **Dhikr:** Remembrance of God; repetitive chanting
- **Sama:** Whirling (Rumi/Mevlevi order); music, dance
- **Asceticism:** Renunciation of material; focus on spiritual

**Orders (Tariqas):** Qadiriyya, Naqshbandiyya, Chishtiyya; led by shaykh (spiritual master)

**Figures:** Rumi (poet), Al-Ghazali (theologian-mystic), Ibn Arabi (metaphysician)

**Controversy:** Wahhabi/Salafi Islam rejects Sufism as bid'ah (innovation); shrine visitation = idolatry

---

## Modern Islam

### Reform Movements

**Wahhabism (1700s, Saudi Arabia):** Muhammad ibn Abd al-Wahhab; purify Islam; reject Sufism, shrines; strict Quran/Hadith; allied with Saud family; exports globally

**Salafism:** "Pious predecessors" (salaf); return to early Islam; rejects innovation; overlaps with Wahhabism

**Modernism:** Harmonize Islam with modernity; Muhammad Abduh, Jamal al-Din al-Afghani; reason, science compatible with Islam

---

### Political Islam (Islamism)

**Muslim Brotherhood (1928, Egypt):** Hassan al-Banna; Islamic state; sharia law; social services; political participation vs violence debated

**Jamaat-e-Islami (1941, Pakistan):** Abul Ala Maududi; Islamic state; influenced global Islamism

**Iranian Revolution (1979):** Ayatollah Khomeini; overthrew Shah; established theocracy; Shia Islamism

**Taliban (1990s, Afghanistan):** Deobandi; strict sharia; harbored Al-Qaeda

**Hamas (1987, Palestine):** Offshoot of Muslim Brotherhood; resistance + social services; designated terrorist by many

**Hezbollah (1985, Lebanon):** Shia; Iranian-backed; militia + political party + social services

---

### Terrorism & Extremism

**Al-Qaeda (1988):** Osama bin Laden; global jihad; 9/11 attacks (2001); destroyed Afghan/Pakistan leadership but franchises remain

**ISIS/ISIL/Daesh (2014-2019):** Abu Bakr al-Baghdadi; declared caliphate (Iraq/Syria); genocide (Yazidis), beheadings, terror; defeated militarily but ideology persists

**Theological Justification:** Takfir (declaring Muslims apostates); offensive jihad; end-times eschatology; ignored by vast majority of Muslims

**Mainstream Rejection:** Scholars issue fatwas against; terrorism un-Islamic; hijacking of religion

---

### Women in Islam

**Quran:** Affirms spiritual equality; men/women equal before God

**Traditional Roles:** Modesty (hijab); gender segregation; men = breadwinners/protectors; women = mothers/homemakers

**Debates:**
- **Hijab:** Obligation vs choice vs oppression; varies by culture
- **Leadership:** Can women lead prayer? (Mostly no); be judges? (Debated)
- **Marriage:** Polygyny allowed (up to 4 wives, equal treatment); divorce easier for men; inheritance (daughters get half sons')
- **Testimony:** Some interpret Quran as women's testimony = half men's (legal contexts); disputed

**Reforms:** Female scholars, imams (rare); feminist interpretations of Quran; legal equality in some Muslim-majority countries (Turkey, Tunisia)

---

## Contemporary Issues

### Secularism vs Islamism

- Turkey: Ataturk's secularism vs Erdoğan's Islamism
- France: Laïcité (secularism) vs Muslim religious expression (hijab bans)
- Arab Spring (2011): Democracy hopes; mostly failed; Islamists gained (Egypt's Morsi, then military coup)

### Islamophobia

- Post-9/11: Muslims profiled, surveilled; hate crimes; mosque attacks
- Far-right: Anti-Muslim rhetoric; "Muslim ban" (Trump); "Great Replacement" conspiracy
- Debates: Legitimate criticism of extremism vs bigotry against Muslims

### Sectarianism

- Saudi Arabia (Sunni) vs Iran (Shia): Proxy wars (Yemen, Syria, Iraq, Lebanon)
- ISIS targeted Shia; Shia militias targeted Sunnis
- Sunni-Shia intermarriage declining; segregation increasing

### Apostasy & Blasphemy

- 13 countries: Apostasy (leaving Islam) = death penalty (rarely enforced)
- Blasphemy laws: Pakistan, Egypt, others; used to persecute minorities, silence dissent
- Charlie Hebdo massacre (2015): Cartoons of Muhammad → terror attack; free speech vs religious sensitivity

### Integration

- European Muslims: 2nd/3rd generation; identity tensions; discrimination; radicalization small minority
- US Muslims: More integrated; higher education/income than average; but post-9/11 backlash

## Key Terms

**Allah:** God (Arabic); same God as Judaism/Christianity

**Quran:** Holy book; revealed to Muhammad; divine word

**Hadith:** Sayings/actions of Muhammad; legal source

**Sharia:** Islamic law; based on Quran, Hadith, consensus, analogy

**Jihad:** "Struggle"; greater jihad = internal spiritual; lesser jihad = physical defense; hijacked by extremists

**Ummah:** Global Muslim community

**Caliph:** Successor to Muhammad; political-religious leader

**Imam:** Sunni = prayer leader; Shia = divinely guided leader (descendant of Muhammad)

**Hijab:** Headscarf; modesty; varies culturally

**Halal:** Permissible; halal food (no pork, alcohol; ritual slaughter)

**Haram:** Forbidden

## Summary

Islam: 1.9 billion adherents; second-largest, fastest-growing. Founded by Muhammad (570-632 CE) in Arabia; received Quran from Angel Gabriel. Core: One God, Muhammad final prophet, Five Pillars (shahada, prayer, charity, fasting, pilgrimage). Split: Sunni (85-90%, elected leaders) vs Shia (10-15%, hereditary Imams from Muhammad's family); Battle of Karbala (680) central to Shia identity. Golden Age (800-1100): Mathematics, astronomy, medicine, philosophy under Abbasids (Baghdad). Sharia (Islamic law) based on Quran, Hadith, consensus, analogy; scope covers worship, transactions, punishments; hudud (fixed punishments) debated. Sufism (mysticism): Direct experience via dhikr, asceticism; Rumi prominent. Modern: Wahhabism/Salafism (purist), Islamism (political Islam), terrorism (Al-Qaeda, ISIS) rejected by mainstream. Contemporary issues: Secularism tensions, Islamophobia, Sunni-Shia sectarianism, women's roles, blasphemy laws, integration. Shaped Middle East, North Africa, Central/South Asia, Southeast Asia culturally, legally, politically.
