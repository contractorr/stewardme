# Dharmic Religions: Hinduism, Buddhism, Jainism, Sikhism

## Overview

Dharmic religions: Originated Indian subcontinent; share concepts of dharma (duty/righteousness), karma (action/consequence), samsara (rebirth cycle), moksha/nirvana (liberation). No single founder (except Buddhism, Jainism, Sikhism); orthopraxy over orthodoxy; diverse, non-dogmatic. Hinduism: 1.2B (15% global); Buddhism: 520M (7%); Sikhism: 30M (0.4%); Jainism: 5M (0.06%). Contrast with Abrahamic: No single prophet, no mandatory conversion, cyclical time (not linear), multiple paths to truth.

---

## Hinduism (1.2 billion)

### Origins & Development

**Indus Valley Civilization (3300-1300 BCE):** Seals suggest proto-Shiva; ritual bathing; unclear continuity

**Vedic Period (1500-500 BCE):**
- **Aryans:** Migrated to India (debated); Sanskrit; pastoral
- **Vedas:** Four collections (Rigveda oldest, ~1500 BCE); hymns to gods; ritual
- **Polytheism:** Indra (war), Agni (fire), Varuna (cosmic order); Sacrifice (yajna) central
- **Caste:** Four varnas (Brahmin priests, Kshatriya warriors, Vaishya merchants, Shudra laborers); outside = Dalits ("untouchables")

**Upanishadic Period (800-200 BCE):**
- **Upanishads:** Philosophical texts; shift from ritual to knowledge
- **Brahman:** Ultimate reality; impersonal, infinite
- **Atman:** Individual soul; identical with Brahman ("Thou art That")
- **Maya:** Illusion; world as veil over Brahman
- **Karma:** Actions determine rebirth
- **Samsara:** Cycle of birth-death-rebirth
- **Moksha:** Liberation; escape samsara; merge with Brahman

---

### Core Concepts

**Brahman:** Ultimate reality; unchanging, infinite, impersonal; beyond description (neti neti — "not this, not this")

**Atman:** True self; eternal; reincarnates; not body/mind

**Maya:** Cosmic illusion; mistake Brahman for separate material world

**Karma:** Law of cause-effect; ethical causation; good deeds → good rebirth; bad deeds → bad rebirth (or animal/insect)

**Dharma:** Duty/righteousness; varies by caste, life stage; uphold cosmic order

**Samsara:** Rebirth cycle; suffering; goal = escape

**Moksha:** Liberation; union with Brahman; end of rebirth

**Four Aims (Purusharthas):** Dharma (duty), Artha (prosperity), Kama (pleasure), Moksha (liberation)

**Four Stages of Life (Ashramas):** Student (brahmacharya), Householder (grihastha), Forest dweller (vanaprastha), Renunciant (sannyasa)

---

### Major Traditions

**Vaishnavism (Vishnu):** Largest; Vishnu = preserver; avatars (Krishna, Rama); devotion (bhakti); Hare Krishna movement

**Shaivism (Shiva):** Shiva = destroyer/transformer; ascetic; meditation; Lingam worship

**Shaktism (Devi/Goddess):** Divine feminine; Durga, Kali, Lakshmi; power (shakti)

**Smartism:** Eclectic; worship five deities (Vishnu, Shiva, Devi, Surya, Ganesha); Advaita Vedanta philosophy

---

### Sacred Texts

**Shruti ("Heard"):** Divinely revealed; authoritative
- **Vedas:** Rigveda, Samaveda, Yajurveda, Atharvaveda; hymns, rituals
- **Upanishads:** Philosophical; Brahman-Atman identity

**Smriti ("Remembered"):** Human-authored; authoritative but secondary
- **Mahabharata:** Epic; 100,000 verses; Pandavas vs Kauravas; contains Bhagavad Gita
- **Bhagavad Gita:** Krishna-Arjuna dialogue; battlefield; dharma, yoga, devotion; most popular Hindu text
- **Ramayana:** Epic; Rama vs Ravana; ideal king, wife (Sita)
- **Puranas:** Myths, cosmology, temple practices; 18 major
- **Manusmriti:** Law code; caste rules; controversial (treatment of women, lower castes)

---

### Practices

**Puja:** Worship; offerings (flowers, food, incense) to deity images; home/temple

**Darshan:** Seeing/being seen by deity; eye contact with murti (statue)

**Yoga:** Union with divine; multiple paths:
- **Karma Yoga:** Action without attachment
- **Bhakti Yoga:** Devotion, love
- **Jnana Yoga:** Knowledge, study
- **Raja Yoga:** Meditation (Patanjali's Yoga Sutras; eight limbs; asanas, pranayama)

**Festivals:** Diwali (lights, Lakshmi), Holi (colors, Krishna), Navaratri (goddess), Durga Puja

**Pilgrimage:** Ganges (sacred river), Varanasi (holiest city), Char Dham (four sites)

**Sacraments (Samskaras):** 16 life-cycle rituals; birth, initiation, marriage, death

**Cremation:** Body burned; ashes scattered in river; soul reincarnates

---

### Caste System

**Varna:** Four classes (Brahmin, Kshatriya, Vaishya, Shudra) + Dalits (outside)

**Jati:** Endogamous subcastes; 3,000+; hereditary occupation

**Untouchability:** Dalits = "polluting"; banned 1950 (India constitution) but persists

**Reform:** Gandhi ("Harijans" — children of God); Ambedkar (Dalit leader, converted to Buddhism); affirmative action (reservations)

**Controversy:** Caste hierarchy justified by karma (past-life sins) → criticized as unjust; reform vs tradition

---

### Contemporary Issues

**Hindu Nationalism (Hindutva):** BJP party; India as Hindu nation; anti-Muslim; 2002 Gujarat riots; 2019 Citizenship Amendment Act

**Caste Discrimination:** Persists; violence against Dalits; inter-caste marriage opposed

**Women:** Widow remarriage stigmatized; dowry deaths; goddesses worshiped but women subordinated; reforms ongoing

**Diaspora:** 30M outside India; identity tensions; ISKCON (Hare Krishna), Transcendental Meditation, yoga popular in West

## Buddhism (520 million)

### The Buddha (c. 563-483 BCE)

**Birth:** Siddhartha Gautama; Lumbini (Nepal); royal family (Shakya clan)

**Prophecy:** Become world-ruler or spiritual teacher; father chose former; sheltered in palace

**Four Sights (age 29):** Old age, sickness, death, ascetic → realization of suffering

**Renunciation:** Left palace, wife, son; became wandering ascetic

**Asceticism (6 years):** Extreme fasting, meditation; near death; rejected as ineffective

**Middle Way:** Balance between indulgence and asceticism

**Enlightenment (age 35):** Bodh Gaya; meditated under Bodhi tree; defeated Mara (temptation); achieved nirvana; understood Four Noble Truths

**Teaching (45 years):** Deer Park (first sermon); wandered North India; established Sangha (monastic community)

**Death (age 80):** Kushinagar; parinirvana (final nirvana); cremated; relics distributed

---

### Four Noble Truths

1. **Dukkha (Suffering):** Life is suffering (birth, aging, sickness, death, separation, not getting what you want)
2. **Samudaya (Origin):** Suffering caused by craving/attachment (tanha)
3. **Nirodha (Cessation):** Suffering can end by eliminating craving
4. **Magga (Path):** Eightfold Path leads to end of suffering

---

### Noble Eightfold Path

**Wisdom (Prajna):**
1. Right View (understand Four Noble Truths)
2. Right Intention (renunciation, goodwill, harmlessness)

**Ethics (Sila):**
3. Right Speech (no lying, harsh words, gossip)
4. Right Action (no killing, stealing, sexual misconduct)
5. Right Livelihood (ethical occupation)

**Concentration (Samadhi):**
6. Right Effort (cultivate wholesome states)
7. Right Mindfulness (aware of body, feelings, mind, phenomena)
8. Right Concentration (meditative absorption)

---

### Key Concepts

**Anatta (No-Self):** No permanent, unchanging soul (contrast with Hindu atman); self = illusion; five aggregates (skandhas) = body, feeling, perception, mental formations, consciousness

**Anicca (Impermanence):** All conditioned things change; attachment to impermanent → suffering

**Nirvana:** "Blowing out"; extinction of craving; liberation from rebirth; not annihilation but indescribable state

**Dependent Origination (Pratityasamutpada):** All phenomena arise dependent on conditions; no independent existence; twelve links (ignorance → formations → consciousness → ... → birth → aging/death)

**Karma:** Actions (physical, verbal, mental) have consequences; no divine judge; natural law

**Rebirth (not Reincarnation):** No soul transmigrates; mental continuum continues; like flame passed candle to candle

**Middle Way:** Between extremes (asceticism/indulgence, eternalism/nihilism)

---

### Major Branches

#### Theravada ("Way of Elders") — 150M

**Geography:** Sri Lanka, Myanmar, Thailand, Cambodia, Laos

**Canon:** Pali Canon (Tripitaka); earliest texts

**Goal:** Arhat (enlightened individual); personal liberation

**Monasticism:** Central; monks = ideal; laypeople support monks, gain merit

**Practice:** Vipassana (insight meditation), Metta (loving-kindness)

**Conservative:** Adheres closely to early teachings

#### Mahayana ("Great Vehicle") — 360M

**Geography:** China, Japan, Korea, Vietnam, Taiwan

**Canon:** Sanskrit texts + new sutras (Lotus, Heart, Diamond)

**Goal:** Bodhisattva (one who delays nirvana to save all beings); compassion over personal liberation

**Bodhisattvas:** Avalokiteshvara (compassion), Manjushri (wisdom), Tara (female); worshiped like deities

**Innovations:** Buddha-nature (all have potential for Buddhahood); multiple Buddhas/bodhisattvas; lay path viable

**Schools:**
- **Pure Land:** Devotion to Amitabha Buddha; rebirth in Pure Land (easier to reach nirvana)
- **Zen (Chan):** Meditation; koans (paradoxical riddles); sudden enlightenment; "direct pointing" beyond scripture
- **Tiantai, Huayan:** Complex philosophy; interpenetration of all phenomena

#### Vajrayana ("Diamond Vehicle") — 20M

**Geography:** Tibet, Bhutan, Mongolia, parts of India/Nepal

**Canon:** Mahayana texts + tantras

**Goal:** Buddhahood in one lifetime (vs many lifetimes)

**Methods:** Mantras, mudras, mandalas, visualizations; esoteric; guru essential

**Deities:** Wrathful/peaceful forms; symbolic; aid practice

**Dalai Lama:** Tibetan Buddhism leader; lineage of reincarnations (Avalokiteshvara); 14th Dalai Lama (1935-) in exile (India) since Chinese invasion (1959)

**Controversy:** Feudal theocracy pre-1959; CIA support; Western romanticization vs historical reality

---

### Practices

**Meditation:** Central; vipassana (insight), samatha (calm), loving-kindness, Zen zazen

**Monastic Life:** Celibacy, poverty, non-violence; shaved heads; robes (orange/maroon)

**Five Precepts (Laypeople):** No killing, stealing, sexual misconduct, lying, intoxicants

**Merit (Punya):** Good deeds → better rebirth; donate to monks, temple upkeep

**Pilgrimage:** Bodh Gaya, Sarnath, Kushinagar, Lumbini

**Festivals:** Vesak (Buddha's birth, enlightenment, death), Magha Puja, Asalha Puja

---

### Contemporary Issues

**Engaged Buddhism:** Apply teachings to social/political issues; Thich Nhat Hanh (Vietnam War), Dalai Lama (Tibet)

**Buddhist Nationalism:** Myanmar (Rohingya genocide), Sri Lanka (anti-Tamil/Muslim); "969 Movement"; contradicts non-violence

**Secularization:** Mindfulness divorced from Buddhism; Western appropriation vs preservation

**Tibetan Buddhism:** China vs exile community; Dalai Lama succession disputed (China wants control)

## Jainism (5 million)

**Founder:** Mahavira (599-527 BCE), contemporary of Buddha; 24th Tirthankara (ford-maker)

**Core:** Ahimsa (non-violence) absolute; all life sacred; karma = physical substance coating soul; extreme asceticism purifies

**Beliefs:**
- **Anekantavada:** Multiple viewpoints valid; no absolute truth
- **Aparigraha:** Non-attachment; minimize possessions
- **No Creator God:** Universe eternal; no divine intervention

**Practices:**
- **Ahimsa:** Vegetarianism; strain water (not kill microbes); sweep path; monks wear masks (not inhale insects)
- **Fasting:** Frequent; ultimate = sallekhana (ritual starvation unto death)
- **Five Vows (monks):** Non-violence, truth, non-stealing, celibacy, non-attachment; laypeople take less strict versions

**Sects:**
- **Digambara ("Sky-clad"):** Monks naked (renounced clothing); women cannot achieve liberation
- **Svetambara ("White-clad"):** Monks wear white; women can achieve liberation

**Influence:** Disproportionate (0.4% India pop but wealthy, influential); Gandhi's ahimsa inspired by Jainism

## Sikhism (30 million)

**Founder:** Guru Nanak (1469-1539); Punjab; Hindu-Muslim syncretism + distinct revelation

**Core:** One God (Waheguru); equality (reject caste); service; balance worldly/spiritual

**Ten Gurus:** Nanak through Gobind Singh (1666-1708); Guru Granth Sahib (scripture) = eternal guru

**Beliefs:**
- **One God:** Formless, timeless; accessible to all
- **Karma & Reincarnation:** But liberation (mukti) via devotion, not asceticism
- **Equality:** All humans equal; reject caste, gender hierarchy
- **Sewa:** Selfless service

**Five Ks (Khalsa identity):**
1. Kesh (uncut hair)
2. Kangha (wooden comb)
3. Kara (steel bracelet)
4. Kirpan (ceremonial sword)
5. Kachera (undergarment)

**Gurdwara:** Temple; Guru Granth Sahib enthroned; langar (free communal meal; everyone sits on floor, equal)

**Practices:** Meditation on God's name (Naam Japna); honest living (Kirat Karo); sharing (Vand Chakko)

**Festivals:** Vaisakhi (Khalsa founding), Gurpurabs (Guru birthdays), Diwali (Sikh version)

**Contemporary:**
- **Punjab:** Homeland; demand for autonomy/independence (Khalistan movement); 1984 Golden Temple storming (Indian army), Indira Gandhi assassination; Sikh diaspora (Canada, UK, US)
- **Turban:** Religious requirement; discrimination/hate crimes (mistaken for Muslims post-9/11)

## Comparative Summary

| Concept | Hinduism | Buddhism | Jainism | Sikhism |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Ultimate Reality** | Brahman (impersonal) | No creator god; Nirvana | No creator god; Universe eternal | One God (Waheguru) |
| **Self** | Atman (eternal soul) | Anatta (no-self) | Jiva (soul, eternal) | Soul exists; merges with God |
| **Goal** | Moksha (union with Brahman) | Nirvana (end craving) | Moksha (purify soul) | Mukti (union with God) |
| **Path** | Yoga, devotion, knowledge | Eightfold Path, meditation | Ahimsa, asceticism, vows | Devotion, service, honest living |
| **Reincarnation** | Yes; based on karma | Rebirth (not reincarnation) | Yes; based on karma | Yes; based on karma + grace |
| **Social Structure** | Caste system | Reject caste | Reject caste | Reject caste |
| **Violence** | Context-dependent (warrior caste) | Non-violence ideal | Absolute non-violence | Self-defense permitted (Kirpan) |
| **Gender** | Patriarchal but goddesses worshiped | Ordination of women debated | Digambara: women inferior | Equality affirmed |

---

## Summary

Dharmic religions share karma, rebirth, liberation but differ in methods/goals. **Hinduism (1.2B):** Diverse; Brahman-Atman unity; dharma, yoga, devotion; caste system; Vedas, Upanishads, Gita; nationalism rising. **Buddhism (520M):** Buddha (563-483 BCE); Four Noble Truths, Eightfold Path; no-self, impermanence, nirvana; Theravada (arhat ideal), Mahayana (bodhisattva ideal), Vajrayana (tantric); meditation central; engaged Buddhism vs nationalism. **Jainism (5M):** Mahavira; absolute non-violence; asceticism; no creator; karma = physical; strain water, wear masks. **Sikhism (30M):** Guru Nanak; one God, equality, service; Five Ks; Punjab; Khalistan tensions. Contrast Abrahamic: cyclical time, multiple paths, orthopraxy, no mandatory conversion, diverse/non-dogmatic.
