# Abrahamic Religions I: Judaism

## Overview

Judaism: Oldest monotheistic religion (~3,500 years); foundation for Christianity and Islam. 15 million adherents (0.2% of world but disproportionate influence). Ethnic religion (hereditary via maternal line) + religious identity. Covenantal: God chose Jewish people; they must follow Torah. Orthopraxy (correct practice) over orthodoxy (correct belief). Survived diaspora (70 CE-1948); shaped Western law, ethics, philosophy.

---

## Historical Development

### Origins & Patriarchs (c. 2000-1500 BCE)

**Abraham:** Patriarch; covenant with God (circumcision; promised land of Canaan; descendants as numerous as stars)

**Isaac:** Abraham's son; near-sacrifice (binding of Isaac) — faith test

**Jacob/Israel:** Isaac's son; 12 sons → 12 tribes of Israel

**Joseph:** Sold into slavery in Egypt; rises to power; family joins him

**Historical Note:** Limited archaeological evidence for patriarchs; likely composite/legendary figures; core narrative establishes covenantal relationship

---

### Exodus & Torah (c. 1300-1200 BCE disputed)

**Moses:** Leads Israelites out of Egyptian slavery
- **Ten Plagues:** Culminates in Passover (angel of death "passes over" marked Jewish homes)
- **Parting of Red Sea:** Miraculous escape
- **Mount Sinai:** Receives Ten Commandments + rest of Torah
- **40 years wandering:** Generation dies before entering Promised Land

**Torah (Pentateuch/Five Books of Moses):**
1. Genesis: Creation, patriarchs
2. Exodus: Slavery, liberation, Sinai covenant
3. Leviticus: Priestly laws, ritual purity
4. Numbers: Wilderness wanderings
5. Deuteronomy: Moses' final speeches, restatement of law

**613 Commandments (Mitzvot):** 248 positive ("do"), 365 negative ("don't")
- Covers: Ritual, dietary (kashrut), civil, criminal, moral
- Foundation of Jewish law (Halakha)

**Historical Note:** Exodus historicity debated; no Egyptian records; possibly exodus of smaller group + legendary growth

---

### Kingdom & Temple (c. 1000-586 BCE)

**United Monarchy:**
- **Saul:** First king; anointed by Samuel
- **David:** Defeats Goliath; establishes Jerusalem as capital; unites tribes; Davidic covenant (eternal dynasty)
- **Solomon:** Builds First Temple (~950 BCE); Israel's golden age; splits after his death

**Divided Kingdom (922 BCE):**
- **Northern Kingdom (Israel):** 10 tribes; capital Samaria; conquered by Assyria 722 BCE
- **Southern Kingdom (Judah):** 2 tribes (Judah, Benjamin); capital Jerusalem; survives until 586 BCE

**Prophets:**
- **Major:** Isaiah, Jeremiah, Ezekiel
- **Minor:** Hosea, Amos, Micah, etc.
- Message: Social justice, monotheism, return to covenant; warned of destruction if unfaithful

**First Temple Destroyed (586 BCE):** Babylonians under Nebuchadnezzar II; Babylonian Exile begins

---

### Second Temple Period (516 BCE - 70 CE)

**Return from Exile (538 BCE):** Cyrus the Great (Persia) allows Jews to return; Second Temple built (516 BCE)

**Hellenization (332 BCE onward):** Alexander the Great conquers; Greek culture spreads
- Maccabean Revolt (167-160 BCE): Jews rebel against Seleucids forcing Hellenization; Hanukkah commemorates
- Hasmonean Dynasty: Brief independence (140-63 BCE)

**Roman Rule (63 BCE onward):** Pompey conquers; Judea becomes client state, then province

**Sectarian Divisions:**
| Group | Beliefs | Status |
|---|---|---|
| **Pharisees** | Oral Torah authoritative; resurrection; angels; free will | Survived as Rabbinic Judaism |
| **Sadducees** | Only written Torah; no afterlife; hereditary priests | Extinct after Temple destruction |
| **Essenes** | Ascetic; apocalyptic; ritual purity | Likely produced Dead Sea Scrolls; extinct |
| **Zealots** | Armed resistance to Rome | Led revolt; destroyed by Rome |

**Jesus:** Jewish preacher (4 BCE - 30 CE); crucified by Romans; followers become Christians (see Chapter 03)

**Jewish-Roman Wars:**
- First Revolt (66-73 CE): Crushed by Rome; Second Temple destroyed (70 CE); Masada falls (73 CE)
- Bar Kokhba Revolt (132-136 CE): Final Jewish rebellion; Jerusalem razed, renamed; Jews banned from city

---

### Rabbinic Judaism (70 CE - 600 CE)

**Response to Temple Destruction:** Shift from Temple cult to Torah study + prayer
- **Yavneh Academy:** Rabbinic leaders reconstitute Judaism without Temple
- **Rabbis replace priests:** Authority via scholarship, not heredity
- **Synagogue replaces Temple:** Decentralized worship; study, prayer, community

**Mishnah (c. 200 CE):** Compilation of Oral Torah (legal discussions, interpretations); edited by Rabbi Judah HaNasi

**Talmuds:** Commentary on Mishnah
- **Jerusalem Talmud (c. 400 CE):** Compiled in Israel; shorter, less authoritative
- **Babylonian Talmud (c. 500 CE):** Compiled in Babylon; longer, became standard; basis of Jewish law

**Structure of Talmud:** Mishnah (concise law) + Gemara (rabbinic debates, stories, tangents); non-linear, digressive, multi-vocal

---

### Medieval Period (600-1800)

**Under Islam:** Generally tolerated as "People of the Book" (dhimmi status)
- Golden Age in Spain (900-1200): Philosophy, science, poetry flourish
- **Maimonides (1138-1204):** *Guide for the Perplexed*; rationalist theology; "Thirteen Principles of Faith"

**Under Christianity:** Persecution intensified
- Crusades (1096 onward): Massacres of Rhineland Jews
- Blood libels: False accusations of ritual murder
- Expulsions: England (1290), France (multiple), Spain (1492), Portugal (1497)
- Ghettos: Segregated urban quarters (Venice Ghetto, 1516)
- Pogroms: Organized massacres (Eastern Europe, especially during Black Death blamed on Jews)

**Mysticism — Kabbalah:**
- **Zohar (c. 1280):** Mystical commentary on Torah; central Kabbalistic text
- **Isaac Luria (1534-1572):** Lurianic Kabbalah; creation via divine contraction (tzimtzuk); tikkun olam (repair of world)

---

### Modern Era (1800-present)

**Emancipation:** Jews gain citizenship, civil rights in Europe/Americas (1700s-1800s)

**Haskalah (Jewish Enlightenment, 1700s-1800s):** Embrace secular education, integration; Moses Mendelssohn leader

**Denominational Splits:**

| Denomination | Origin | Beliefs | Practice |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Orthodox** | Traditional continuation | Torah divine, unchanging; Talmud authoritative | Strict observance of Halakha; separate gender roles; kosher; Sabbath |
| **Reform** | Germany, 1800s | Torah human-authored; adapt to modernity | Egalitarian; less ritual; English/vernacular prayers; no kosher/Sabbath requirement |
| **Conservative** | US, late 1800s | Middle path; Torah divine but historical context matters | Moderately traditional; egalitarian (debated); balance tradition/modernity |
| **Reconstructionist** | US, 1920s (Mordecai Kaplan) | Judaism = evolving civilization, not just religion | Egalitarian; God as impersonal force; democratic decision-making |
| **Hasidic** | Eastern Europe, 1700s | Mystical; charismatic leaders (rebbes); joy, devotion over scholarship | Ultra-Orthodox; distinct dress; insular communities; Yiddish |

**Zionism (late 1800s):**
- **Theodor Herzl:** *The Jewish State* (1896); political Zionism; response to persistent antisemitism
- Goal: Jewish homeland in Palestine (historical land of Israel)
- 1948: State of Israel established after Holocaust, WWII

**Holocaust (Shoah, 1941-1945):** Nazi genocide; 6 million Jews killed (2/3 of European Jews; 1/3 of all Jews)
- Theological crisis: Where was God? (theodicy)
- Accelerated Zionism; Israel's founding 3 years later

**Israel-Diaspora Dynamics:**
- Israel: 7 million Jews (47% of world total)
- Diaspora: 8 million (US 6M, France 450K, Canada 390K, UK 290K)
- Tension: Israel as nation-state vs diaspora identity; settlements; Law of Return (automatic citizenship for Jews)

## Core Beliefs & Practices

### Theology

**Monotheism:** One God (YHWH/Adonai/Hashem); indivisible, incorporeal, eternal

**Covenant:** Mutual obligations; God protects/blesses Israel; Israel obeys Torah

**Chosen People:** Not superiority but responsibility; "light unto nations"

**Free Will:** Humans can choose good/evil; responsible for actions

**Afterlife:** Vague in Hebrew Bible; developed later; Olam Ha-Ba (World to Come); resurrection debated (Pharisees yes, Sadducees no)

**Messiah:** Future anointed leader; will gather exiles, rebuild Temple, usher in age of peace
- Christians believe Jesus = Messiah; Jews reject (didn't fulfill prophecies)
- Chabad (Hasidic group): Some believed Rabbi Schneerson was Messiah

**No Original Sin:** Each person responsible for own actions; children born pure

**Maimonides' 13 Principles (1160s):**
1. God exists
2. God is one
3. God is incorporeal
4. God is eternal
5. Worship God alone
6. Prophets are true
7. Moses greatest prophet
8. Torah divine
9. Torah unchanging
10. God knows human deeds
11. God rewards/punishes
12. Messiah will come
13. Resurrection of dead

### Practices (Mitzvot)

**Shabbat (Sabbath):** Friday sunset to Saturday sunset; day of rest; no work (39 categories prohibited); family meals, prayer, study

**Kashrut (Dietary Laws):**
- Kosher animals: Cloven hoof + chew cud (cows, sheep, goats); scales + fins (fish); no shellfish, pork
- Ritual slaughter (shechita): Minimize suffering
- Separation: No milk + meat together (based on "don't boil kid in mother's milk")

**Prayer:** 3 daily services (Shacharit, Mincha, Maariv); minyan (10 adults) for communal prayer

**Tallit (Prayer Shawl):** Fringes (tzitzit) remind of commandments

**Tefillin (Phylacteries):** Leather boxes with Torah verses; worn on arm, forehead during morning prayer

**Mezuzah:** Parchment with Shema prayer; affixed to doorposts

**Lifecycle Events:**
- **Brit milah (Circumcision):** 8 days after birth (boys); covenant sign
- **Bar/Bat Mitzvah:** Age 13 (boys), 12 (girls); adult responsibilities
- **Marriage:** Under chuppah (canopy); groom breaks glass (remember Temple destruction)
- **Death:** Burial within 24 hours; shiva (7-day mourning); kaddish (mourner's prayer)

**High Holy Days:**
- **Rosh Hashanah (New Year):** Judgment day; shofar (ram's horn) blown; reflection
- **Yom Kippur (Day of Atonement):** 10 days after; 25-hour fast; repentance; most sacred day

**Pilgrimage Festivals:**
- **Passover (Pesach):** 8 days; commemorates Exodus; seder meal; no leavened bread
- **Shavuot (Pentecost):** 50 days after Passover; giving of Torah
- **Sukkot (Tabernacles):** Temporary huts; harvest festival; remember wilderness

**Other Holidays:**
- **Hanukkah:** 8 days; Maccabean victory; lights, gifts
- **Purim:** Celebrates survival from Haman's plot (Book of Esther); costumes, carnival-like

## Sacred Texts

### Tanakh (Hebrew Bible)

**Torah (Pentateuch):** Genesis, Exodus, Leviticus, Numbers, Deuteronomy (see above)

**Nevi'im (Prophets):**
- Former: Joshua, Judges, Samuel, Kings (historical)
- Latter: Isaiah, Jeremiah, Ezekiel (major); 12 minor prophets

**Ketuvim (Writings):**
- Psalms, Proverbs, Job (wisdom literature)
- Song of Songs, Ruth, Lamentations, Ecclesiastes, Esther (five scrolls)
- Daniel, Ezra-Nehemiah, Chronicles

**Masoretic Text:** Standard Hebrew Bible; vocalization added 7th-10th century

---

### Talmud

**Two Versions:**
- **Babylonian (Bavli):** Authoritative; 2.5 million words; 37 tractates
- **Jerusalem (Yerushalmi):** Shorter; less studied

**Structure:** Page layout with Mishnah center, Gemara surrounding, later commentaries in margins (Rashi, Tosafot)

**Content:** Legal rulings (Halakha) + stories/folklore (Aggadah)

**Study Method:** Chavruta (pairs); debate, question, challenge

---

### Later Works

**Midrash:** Homiletical commentaries on Bible; fill gaps in narrative; moral lessons

**Responsa Literature:** Rabbinic answers to legal questions; ongoing; adapts law to new situations

**Shulchan Aruch (1563):** Joseph Caro; codification of Jewish law; standard for Orthodox

**Zohar:** Kabbalistic text (see above)

## Contemporary Issues

### Israel-Palestine Conflict

- Religious dimension: Temple Mount/Haram al-Sharif (Third Temple vs Al-Aqsa Mosque)
- Settler movement: Religious Zionists cite biblical land promises
- Secular-religious divide in Israel: Draft exemptions for ultra-Orthodox; Sabbath observance laws

### Intermarriage

- US: 58% of non-Orthodox Jews marry non-Jews (Pew 2020)
- Orthodox: <10% intermarriage
- Debate: Is Judaism dying out (assimilation) or evolving (inclusion)?

### Women's Roles

- Orthodox: Traditional gender separation; women don't count in minyan; can't be rabbis (except Open Orthodox)
- Reform/Conservative: Full equality; women rabbis since 1970s
- Bat mitzvah: Introduced 1922 (Reconstructionist); now universal in non-Orthodox

### Conversion

- Orthodox: Strict (study, ritual immersion, circumcision for men, rabbinic court); patrilineal descent rejected
- Reform: Patrilineal descent accepted (1983); easier conversion
- Tensions: Israel recognizes Orthodox conversions only (for citizenship/marriage)

### Antisemitism Resurgence

- Far-right: White nationalism, conspiracy theories (George Soros, "globalists")
- Far-left: Anti-Zionism blurs into antisemitism (Jews blamed for Israel)
- Islamist: Hamas, Hezbollah; antisemitic charters
- Attacks: Pittsburgh synagogue (2018), Poway (2019), Jersey City (2019)

## Key Terms

**Halakha:** Jewish law derived from Torah, Talmud, rabbinic rulings

**Mitzvah:** Commandment; 613 in Torah; good deed

**Shabbat:** Sabbath; Friday sunset to Saturday sunset

**Kashrut:** Dietary laws; kosher = fit/proper

**Tanakh:** Hebrew Bible (Torah + Prophets + Writings)

**Talmud:** Mishnah + Gemara; rabbinic discussions of law

**Rabbi:** Teacher; Jewish scholar; religious leader

**Synagogue:** House of worship, study, community

**Diaspora:** Jewish communities outside Israel; "scattering"

**Zionism:** Movement for Jewish homeland in Palestine/Israel

**Shoah:** Holocaust; Nazi genocide of 6 million Jews

**Aliyah:** Immigration to Israel; "ascent"

## Summary

Judaism: Oldest monotheistic religion; covenantal relationship between God and Jewish people via Torah (613 commandments). Historical trajectory: patriarchs → Exodus → kingdoms → Temple destructions → diaspora → rabbinic Judaism → Holocaust → Israel. Orthopraxy (correct practice) emphasized: Shabbat, kashrut, prayer, lifecycle rituals. Sacred texts: Tanakh (Hebrew Bible) + Talmud (rabbinic debates). Denominations: Orthodox (traditional), Reform (liberal), Conservative (middle), Reconstructionist (evolving civilization), Hasidic (mystical). Contemporary issues: Israel-Palestine, intermarriage, women's equality, antisemitism. Survived 2,000 years without homeland via textual tradition, community cohesion, adaptability. Disproportionate influence on Western civilization (law, ethics, monotheism) despite small numbers (15 million globally).
