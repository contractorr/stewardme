# Glossary

## A

**Abrahamic Religions:** Judaism, Christianity, Islam; trace ancestry to Abraham; monotheistic; prophetic.

**Agnosticism:** Position that existence of God unknowable; neither belief nor disbelief.

**Ahimsa:** Non-violence; central to Jainism, Buddhism, Hinduism (especially Jainism — absolute).

**Allah:** Arabic word for God; used by Muslims (and Arab Christians).

**Anatta:** Buddhist concept of "no-self"; no permanent, unchanging soul.

**Animism:** Belief that spirits inhabit natural objects (trees, rivers, animals); prevalent in indigenous religions.

**Apostasy:** Renunciation of religion; punishable by death in some Islamic countries (13).

**Apostle:** Early Christian missionary; 12 disciples of Jesus; Paul (later apostle).

**Arhat:** Enlightened person in Buddhism (Theravada ideal); achieved nirvana; no longer reincarnates.

**Asceticism:** Severe self-discipline; renunciation of worldly pleasures; fasting, celibacy, poverty.

**Atheism:** Disbelief in existence of God/gods.

**Atman:** Hindu concept of true self/soul; eternal; identical to Brahman ("Thou art That").

**Axial Age:** 800-200 BCE; simultaneous emergence of ethical monotheism, philosophy (Confucius, Buddha, Socrates, Hebrew Prophets).

## B

**Baptism:** Christian initiation rite; water symbolizes purification, rebirth; infant (Catholic) vs adult (Baptist).

**Bhakti:** Devotional love; path in Hinduism; emotional connection to deity.

**Blasphemy:** Insult to God/sacred; punishable (death in Pakistan, Egypt, Iran); Charlie Hebdo attacked for Muhammad cartoons (2015).

**Bodhi:** Enlightenment in Buddhism; awakening; understanding Four Noble Truths.

**Bodhisattva:** Mahayana Buddhism; enlightened being who delays nirvana to save others; compassion emphasized.

**Brahman:** Ultimate reality in Hinduism; impersonal, infinite, unchanging; all is Brahman.

**Buddha:** "Awakened One"; Siddhartha Gautama (563-483 BCE); founder of Buddhism.

## C

**Caliph:** Successor to Muhammad; political-religious leader of Muslims; historical caliphates (Rashidun, Umayyad, Abbasid, Ottoman).

**Canon Law:** Legal system of Catholic Church; governs clergy, sacraments, internal matters; influenced Western law.

**Caste:** Hereditary social hierarchy in Hinduism; four varnas (Brahmin, Kshatriya, Vaishya, Shudra) + Dalits ("untouchables"); banned 1950 but persists.

**Charisma:** Personal magnetism; Weber's authority type; prophets, cult leaders.

**Confucianism:** Chinese philosophy/religion; Confucius (551-479 BCE); ritual (li), humaneness (ren), filial piety (xiao).

**Covenant:** Mutual agreement; God-Israel relationship (Judaism); Old Covenant (Torah) vs New Covenant (Jesus's blood, Christianity).

**Creationism:** Belief that God created universe; Young-Earth (6,000 years) vs Old-Earth; opposes evolution.

**Crusades:** Christian military campaigns (1095-1291); recapture Jerusalem from Muslims; Pope Urban II; brutal; mostly failed.

## D

**Dalai Lama:** Tibetan Buddhist leader; believed to be reincarnation of Avalokiteshvara (bodhisattva of compassion); 14th Dalai Lama (1935-) in exile.

**Darshan:** Hindu practice of seeing/being seen by deity; eye contact with murti (statue).

**Deism:** Belief in God who created universe but doesn't intervene; Enlightenment-era (Jefferson, Franklin).

**Denomination:** Branch within religion; Protestant denominations (Baptist, Lutheran, Methodist, etc.).

**Dharma:** Duty, righteousness, cosmic order; key concept in Hinduism, Buddhism, Jainism, Sikhism.

**Diaspora:** Dispersion from homeland; Jewish diaspora (after 70 CE Temple destruction); now global.

**Dualism:** Cosmic struggle between good and evil forces; Zoroastrianism (Ahura Mazda vs Angra Mainyu).

**Dukkha:** Suffering; First Noble Truth in Buddhism; life entails unsatisfactoriness.

## E

**Ecumenism:** Movement for Christian unity; cooperation/dialogue between denominations.

**Enlightenment:** Awakening; understanding true nature of reality; Buddhist (bodhi), Hindu (moksha).

**Eschatology:** Study of end times; apocalypse, judgment, afterlife.

**Eucharist:** Christian sacrament; bread and wine commemorate Last Supper; transubstantiation (Catholic — becomes body/blood) vs symbolic (Protestant).

**Evangelical:** Protestant movement; born-again experience, Bible inerrant, evangelism; 300M+ globally.

**Excommunication:** Expulsion from Church; loss of sacraments; medieval weapon against heretics, kings.

## F

**Fatwa:** Islamic legal opinion issued by scholar; non-binding (except in Iran); Salman Rushdie fatwa (1989, Khomeini).

**Filioque:** "And the Son"; Western addition to Nicene Creed (Holy Spirit proceeds from Father and Son); East rejected; contributed to 1054 Schism.

**Five Pillars:** Core Islamic practices: Shahada (testimony), Salat (prayer 5×/day), Zakat (charity 2.5%), Sawm (fasting Ramadan), Hajj (pilgrimage Mecca).

**Four Noble Truths:** Buddhist core teaching: 1) Life is suffering, 2) Suffering caused by craving, 3) Suffering can end, 4) Eightfold Path ends suffering.

**Fundamentalism:** Literal interpretation of scripture; reactionary; certainty; term from 1920s US Protestantism; applies broadly (Islamic, Hindu, etc.).

## G

**Gentile:** Non-Jew; Paul opened Christianity to Gentiles (no circumcision, kosher laws required).

**Gnosticism:** Early Christian heresy; salvation via secret knowledge (gnosis); matter evil, spirit good; suppressed by Church.

**Gospel:** "Good news"; first four New Testament books (Matthew, Mark, Luke, John); Jesus's life, teachings, death, resurrection.

**Guru:** Teacher; spiritual guide in Hinduism, Sikhism, Buddhism.

**Gurdwara:** Sikh temple; houses Guru Granth Sahib; langar (free communal meal).

## H

**Hadith:** Reports of Muhammad's sayings, actions; supplement Quran; legal source; graded (sahih = authentic, da'if = weak).

**Hajj:** Islamic pilgrimage to Mecca; Fifth Pillar; once in lifetime if able; 2-3 million annually; Kaaba circumambulation.

**Halakha:** Jewish law; based on Torah, Talmud, rabbinic rulings; governs ritual, civil, criminal matters.

**Halal:** Permissible in Islam; halal food (no pork, alcohol; ritual slaughter); opposite = haram.

**Hanukkah:** Jewish festival; 8 days; commemorates Maccabean victory, Temple rededication (165 BCE); lights, gifts.

**Haram:** Forbidden in Islam; pork, alcohol, gambling, interest, premarital sex.

**Hasidism:** Jewish mystical movement; Eastern Europe, 1700s; charismatic leaders (rebbes); joy, devotion; insular; ultra-Orthodox.

**Heaven:** Afterlife reward; Christianity (eternal life with God), Islam (Jannah — gardens, rivers), varying concepts.

**Hell:** Afterlife punishment; Christianity (eternal separation from God), Islam (Jahannam — fire), Buddhism (temporary realms).

**Heresy:** Belief contrary to orthodoxy; medieval punishment = burning; Arians, Gnostics, Cathars condemned.

**Hijab:** Headscarf worn by Muslim women; modesty; varies culturally; debated (religious obligation vs choice vs oppression).

**Hijra:** Muhammad's emigration from Mecca to Medina (622 CE); Year 1 of Islamic calendar.

**Hinduism:** 1.2 billion; Indian origins; diverse; Brahman-Atman, karma, reincarnation, moksha; Vedas, Upanishads, Bhagavad Gita; caste system.

## I

**Icon:** Religious image; venerated in Orthodox Christianity; windows to heaven; Iconoclasm controversy (8th-9th century).

**Imam:** Sunni = prayer leader; Shia = divinely guided leader (descendant of Muhammad); 12 Imams (Twelver Shia).

**Incarnation:** God becoming human; Jesus in Christianity; contested in Judaism, Islam (prophets not divine).

**Inquisition:** Catholic institution (1184+) to root out heresy; torture, burning; Spanish Inquisition (1478-1834) targeted Jews, Muslims, conversos.

**Islam:** 1.9 billion; founded by Muhammad (570-632 CE); Quran, Hadith; Five Pillars; Sunni 85-90%, Shia 10-15%.

**Islamism:** Political Islam; sharia state; Muslim Brotherhood, Jamaat-e-Islami.

## J

**Jainism:** 5 million; India; Mahavira (599-527 BCE); absolute non-violence (ahimsa); extreme asceticism; no creator God; vegetarian.

**Jehovah's Witnesses:** Christian denomination (8.5M); no Trinity; 144,000 go to heaven; door-to-door evangelism; refuse blood transfusions.

**Jihad:** "Struggle"; greater jihad = internal (against sin); lesser jihad = physical defense of Islam; hijacked by extremists (Al-Qaeda, ISIS).

**Judaism:** 15 million; oldest monotheistic religion; covenantal (God-Israel); Torah, Talmud; 613 commandments; Shabbat, kashrut; Orthodox, Reform, Conservative.

## K

**Kabbalah:** Jewish mysticism; Zohar text; Lurianic Kabbalah (tikkun olam — repair world); popular via celebrities (Madonna).

**Kami:** Spirits/deities in Shinto; in nature, ancestors, objects; venerated, not worshiped.

**Karma:** Action; law of cause-effect; good deeds → good rebirth; bad deeds → bad rebirth (Hinduism, Buddhism, Jainism, Sikhism).

**Khalsa:** Sikh initiated community; founded 1699; Five Ks (kesh, kangha, kara, kirpan, kachera).

**Koran:** See Quran.

**Kosher:** Jewish dietary law (kashrut); permitted foods; ritual slaughter; no pork, shellfish; no milk + meat together.

## L

**Laity:** Non-clergy; ordinary members of religion.

**Liberation Theology:** Latin American Christian movement (1960s-80s); preferential option for poor; structural sin; political activism; Vatican suppressed.

**Liturgy:** Prescribed ritual; worship service; Catholic Mass, Orthodox Divine Liturgy highly ritualized.

## M

**Mahayana:** "Great Vehicle"; Buddhism branch; 360M; China, Japan, Korea; bodhisattva ideal; Pure Land, Zen schools.

**Mahdi:** "Guided One"; future Islamic redeemer; Sunni = coming; Shia = 12th Imam in occultation, will return.

**Mandala:** Circular diagram representing cosmos; Hinduism, Buddhism; meditation aid.

**Mantra:** Sacred sound/phrase; repeated in meditation; Om (Hinduism, Buddhism); Hare Krishna.

**Martyr:** One who dies for faith; early Christians (persecutions), Shia (Husayn at Karbala 680 CE); venerated; inspires others.

**Mass:** Catholic Eucharistic liturgy; bread/wine transubstantiation; central act of worship.

**Mecca:** Holiest city in Islam; Saudi Arabia; birthplace of Muhammad; Kaaba (direction of prayer); hajj destination.

**Messiah:** "Anointed One"; Jews = future redeemer (not yet come); Christians = Jesus; will usher peace, gather exiles, rebuild Temple (Jewish expectation).

**Minyan:** Quorum of 10 Jewish adults (Orthodox = men) required for communal prayer.

**Moksha:** Liberation from rebirth cycle (samsara); goal of Hinduism, Jainism; union with Brahman; via yoga, devotion, knowledge.

**Monastery:** Community of monks/nuns; Christian (Benedictine), Buddhist (Sangha); celibacy, poverty, prayer/meditation; preserved learning.

**Monotheism:** Belief in one God; Judaism, Christianity, Islam, Sikhism, Zoroastrianism.

**Mosque:** Islamic house of worship; prayer hall, minaret (call to prayer); no images; face Mecca.

**Muhammad:** Prophet of Islam (570-632 CE); received Quran from Angel Gabriel; emigrated Mecca to Medina (Hijra 622); conquered Mecca 630; Seal of Prophets.

**Mysticism:** Direct experience of divine; Sufism (Islam), Kabbalah (Judaism), Christian mysticism, Vedanta (Hinduism); ecstasy, union, ineffability.

## N

**Nirvana:** Buddhist liberation; "blowing out" of craving; end of rebirth; not annihilation but indescribable state.

**Noble Eightfold Path:** Buddhist path to end suffering: Right View, Intention, Speech, Action, Livelihood, Effort, Mindfulness, Concentration.

**Nones:** Religiously unaffiliated; atheists, agnostics, "spiritual but not religious"; 26% US (2020), growing.

## O

**Orthodoxy:** "Right belief"; emphasis on correct doctrine (Christianity); vs orthopraxy (right practice).

**Orthopraxy:** "Right practice"; emphasis on correct ritual/action (Judaism, Islam, Hinduism, Confucianism).

## P

**Pacifism:** Opposition to war/violence; Quakers, Mennonites, Jainism (absolute).

**Pagan:** Pre-Christian polytheistic religions (Greek, Roman, Norse); now = Wicca, Neopaganism.

**Pantheism:** God = universe; all is God; Spinoza; some interpretations of Hinduism (Brahman).

**Parable:** Allegorical story; Jesus's teaching method (Good Samaritan, Prodigal Son).

**Passover (Pesach):** Jewish festival; 8 days; commemorates Exodus from Egypt; seder meal; no leavened bread.

**Patriarch:** Founding figure (Abraham, Isaac, Jacob); also leader of Orthodox Church.

**Pentecost:** Christian festival; 50 days after Easter; Holy Spirit descends on apostles; birth of Church.

**Pentecostalism:** Christian movement (280M); Baptism of Holy Spirit; speaking in tongues, healing, prophecy; experiential; fastest-growing.

**Pilgrimage:** Journey to sacred site; hajj (Mecca), Vatican, Jerusalem, Ganges, Bodh Gaya.

**Polytheism:** Belief in multiple gods; ancient Greece, Rome, Egypt, Norse; Hinduism (complex — one Brahman, many manifestations).

**Pope:** Bishop of Rome; Catholic leader; claims universal authority; infallible when teaching ex cathedra on faith/morals (rarely invoked).

**Prayer:** Communication with divine; petition, thanksgiving, praise, confession; individual or communal.

**Prophet:** One who speaks for God; receives revelation; Moses, Isaiah (Judaism), Jesus (Christianity), Muhammad (Islam).

**Protestant:** Christian branch (900M); originated Reformation (1517); sola fide, sola scriptura; diverse denominations.

**Purgatory:** Catholic doctrine; intermediate state after death; purification before heaven; not in Protestant theology.

## Q

**Quran:** Holy book of Islam; revealed to Muhammad 610-632 CE; 114 suras; Arabic; recited, memorized; eternal word of God (Sunni mainstream).

## R

**Rabbi:** Jewish teacher, scholar, religious leader; post-Temple (70 CE) authority via learning, not heredity.

**Ramadan:** Islamic holy month; 9th month; fasting dawn-sunset; commemorates Quran revelation; Eid al-Fitr celebration after.

**Reincarnation:** Rebirth of soul in new body; Hinduism, Buddhism (technically "rebirth" — no permanent soul), Jainism, Sikhism; based on karma.

**Relic:** Physical remains of saint or sacred object; venerated in Catholicism, Orthodox; shrines built.

**Religious Pluralism:** Multiple religions valid paths to truth/salvation; vs exclusivism (one true religion).

**Resurrection:** Rising from dead; Jesus (Christianity); all souls (Judaism, Christianity, Islam — Day of Judgment).

**Ritual:** Prescribed ceremonial act; prayer, sacrifice, pilgrimage, rites of passage; creates sacred time/space.

**Rosary:** Catholic prayer beads; Hail Marys, Our Fathers; meditation on mysteries (events in Jesus/Mary's lives).

## S

**Sacrament:** Outward sign of inward grace; Catholics = 7 (Baptism, Eucharist, Confirmation, Reconciliation, Marriage, Holy Orders, Anointing of Sick); Protestants = 2 (Baptism, Eucharist).

**Saint:** Holy person; venerated in Catholicism, Orthodox; intercessors; canonization process (miracles required).

**Salat:** Islamic prayer; 5 times daily; facing Mecca; ritual ablution, prostrations; Second Pillar.

**Salvation:** Deliverance from sin, death; eternal life; Christianity (faith in Jesus), Islam (submission to Allah + good deeds), Hinduism/Buddhism (liberation from rebirth).

**Samsara:** Cycle of birth-death-rebirth; suffering; goal = escape (moksha, nirvana); Hinduism, Buddhism, Jainism, Sikhism.

**Sangha:** Buddhist monastic community; monks, nuns; preserve teachings; supported by laity.

**Scripture:** Sacred text; authoritative; Torah, Bible, Quran, Vedas, etc.

**Sect:** Subgroup within religion; often dissenting; pejorative vs neutral "denomination."

**Secularization:** Decline of religious authority/practice in society; Europe confirms, Global South/US resist.

**Shahada:** Islamic testimony of faith; "No god but Allah, Muhammad is His messenger"; First Pillar; declaration makes one Muslim.

**Sharia:** Islamic law; based on Quran, Hadith, consensus, analogy; governs worship, transactions, crime; 23 countries official; hudud punishments controversial.

**Shia:** Islam branch (10-15%, 200M); Ali = rightful first caliph; 12 Imams (Twelver majority); Iran, Iraq, Lebanon; Ashura (Husayn martyrdom commemoration).

**Shinto:** Indigenous Japanese religion; kami (spirits in nature, ancestors); purity, harmony; shrines, festivals; coexists with Buddhism; State Shinto (1868-1945) = nationalism.

**Shrine:** Sacred site; saint's tomb, pilgrimage destination; venerated.

**Sikhism:** 30 million; Punjab; Guru Nanak (1469-1539); one God (Waheguru), equality, service; Five Ks; Khalsa; Guru Granth Sahib (scripture = eternal guru).

**Sin:** Transgression of divine law; separation from God; original sin (Christianity — inherited from Adam).

**Soteriology:** Study of salvation/liberation.

**Sufism:** Islamic mysticism; direct experience of God via dhikr, asceticism, love; orders (Qadiriyya, Naqshbandiyya); Rumi; Wahhabis reject as innovation.

**Sunni:** Islam branch (85-90%, 1.6B); elected caliphs; Quran + Hadith + consensus + analogy; four law schools (Hanafi, Maliki, Shafi'i, Hanbali).

**Synagogue:** Jewish house of worship; prayer, study, community; rabbi leads; replaced Temple (destroyed 70 CE).

**Syncretism:** Blending of religious traditions; Vodou (African + Catholic), Santería, Cao Dai.

## T

**Talmud:** Rabbinic Jewish text; Mishnah (oral law) + Gemara (commentary); Babylonian Talmud authoritative; legal discussions, stories; basis of Halakha.

**Tanakh:** Hebrew Bible; Torah (Pentateuch) + Nevi'im (Prophets) + Ketuvim (Writings); Christian Old Testament.

**Tao/Dao:** "The Way"; ultimate reality in Taoism; ineffable ("Tao that can be spoken is not eternal Tao"); source of all.

**Taoism/Daoism:** Chinese religion/philosophy; Laozi, Zhuangzi; Tao, wu wei (non-action), naturalness; yin-yang; philosophical + religious (alchemy, immortality, deities).

**Theodicy:** Problem of evil; why does omnipotent, benevolent God allow suffering?

**Theology:** Systematic study of divine nature, religious doctrines.

**Theravada:** "Way of Elders"; Buddhism branch (150M); Sri Lanka, Southeast Asia; Pali Canon; arhat ideal; conservative.

**Torah:** Jewish Law; Five Books of Moses (Genesis, Exodus, Leviticus, Numbers, Deuteronomy); 613 commandments; read in synagogue; handwritten scroll.

**Transcendence:** God beyond/outside creation; vs immanence (God within creation).

**Transubstantiation:** Catholic doctrine; Eucharistic bread/wine become actual body/blood of Christ (not symbolic).

**Trinity:** Christian doctrine; One God in three persons (Father, Son, Holy Spirit); co-equal, co-eternal; Nicene Creed (325 CE); rejected by Unitarians, Jehovah's Witnesses.

## U

**Ummah:** Global Muslim community; transcends ethnicity, nationality.

**Upanishads:** Hindu philosophical texts (800-200 BCE); Brahman-Atman identity; karma, samsara, moksha; basis of Vedanta.

## V

**Vajrayana:** "Diamond Vehicle"; Buddhism branch (20M); Tibet, Bhutan, Mongolia; tantric; mantras, mandalas; Dalai Lama.

**Vedas:** Oldest Hindu scriptures (1500 BCE+); Rigveda, Samaveda, Yajurveda, Atharvaveda; hymns, rituals; shruti (revealed).

## W

**Wahhabism:** Puritanical Sunni Islam; Saudi Arabia; Muhammad ibn Abd al-Wahhab (1700s); strict Quran/Hadith; reject Sufism, innovation; exported globally.

**Wu Wei:** Taoist concept; "non-action"; effortless action; spontaneity; flow with Tao; like water (soft but powerful).

## Y

**Yin-Yang:** Complementary opposites; Taoism, Confucianism; dark-light, female-male, passive-active; dynamic balance; interdependent.

**Yoga:** Union; Hindu path to divine; multiple types (karma, bhakti, jnana, raja); physical postures (asanas) part of raja yoga.

**Yom Kippur:** Jewish Day of Atonement; holiest day; 25-hour fast; repentance; 10 days after Rosh Hashanah.

## Z

**Zakat:** Islamic charity; 2.5% of wealth annually; Third Pillar; to poor, needy; purifies wealth.

**Zen:** Mahayana Buddhism school (Japan); meditation (zazen); koans (paradoxical riddles); sudden enlightenment; "direct pointing" beyond scripture; influenced arts (tea ceremony, calligraphy, gardens).

**Zionism:** Movement for Jewish homeland in Palestine/Israel; Theodor Herzl (1896); political (secular) + religious (biblical); State of Israel (1948); controversial (settlements, Palestinian displacement).

**Zoroastrianism:** Ancient Persian religion; Zoroaster (c. 1500-1000 BCE disputed); dualism (Ahura Mazda vs Angra Mainyu); influenced Judaism, Christianity, Islam (Satan, angels, heaven/hell, resurrection); ~100,000-200,000 adherents (Parsis in India/Iran).
