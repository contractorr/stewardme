# Contemporary Religious Dynamics

## Overview

Religion in 21st century: Secularization (Europe declining) vs religious vitality (Global South growing, US stable but shifting). Fundamentalism reacts to modernity. New religious movements proliferate. Interfaith dialogue vs sectarian conflict. Science-religion debates (evolution, bioethics). Technology reshapes practice (online sermons, apps). Globalization spreads/homogenizes. Political polarization increasingly religious. Understanding contemporary dynamics essential for predicting social/political trends.

---

## Secularization

### Secularization Thesis

**Claim:** Modernization → inevitable religious decline; science, rationality replace superstition

**Proponents:** Weber, Durkheim, Freud; assumed universal

**Europe:** Confirms thesis; church attendance <10% (UK, France, Netherlands); "empty churches"
- Factors: Welfare state (replaces Church social services), education, prosperity, trust in institutions, WWII disillusionment
- Cultural Christianity: Identify as Christian (tradition, culture) but don't believe/practice

**Reversals:**
- **US:** High religiosity despite modernity; 63% say religion important (Pew); evangelical growth
- **Global South:** Christianity/Islam growing rapidly; Pentecostalism exploding
- **Revised Thesis:** Secularization = pluralism, not decline; religion privatized, not eliminated

---

### Nones (Religiously Unaffiliated)

**Growth:** US = 26% (2020), up from 16% (2007); highest among young (36% Millennials/Gen Z)

**Categories:**
- **Atheist:** No God exists (4% US)
- **Agnostic:** Unknowable (5% US)
- **Nothing in Particular:** Spiritual but not religious; don't affiliate (17% US)

**Drivers:** Distrust of institutions (abuse scandals), association with politics (Christian Right), LGBTQ+ exclusion, science-religion conflict, delayed marriage (religion = family-centric)

**Caveat:** Many Nones believe in God (68%); pray (20%); not purely secular

**Europe:** 25-40% unaffiliated (varies by country); Czech Republic ~75%

**China:** 50%+ unaffiliated (Communist suppression + Confucian tradition)

---

### Post-Secularization

**Thesis:** Secularization reversed; religion returning to public sphere (Habermas, Casanova)

**Evidence:**
- Islamic resurgence (Iran 1979, Arab Spring Islamists)
- US Christian Right (political power since 1980s)
- Pentecostal growth (Global South)
- Religious nationalism (Hindutva, Buddhist, Christian)

**Debate:** Is religion resurgent or just more visible (political mobilization)?

### Projections (2050, Pew)

**Islam:** Fastest growing (demographics + conversion); 2.8 billion (30%); nearly equal to Christianity

**Christianity:** 3 billion (31%); stable share but shifting south (Africa, Latin America); Europe declining

**Hinduism:** 1.4 billion (15%); stable (birth-based, not missionary)

**Buddhism:** 530 million (5%); declining share (low fertility East Asia)

**Unaffiliated:** 1.2 billion (13%); declining share (concentrated in low-fertility regions)

**Judaism:** 16 million (0.2%); stable (low fertility offset by ultra-Orthodox high fertility)

**Factors:** Fertility (Muslims 2.9 vs Buddhists 1.6), age structure (Islam youngest), conversion (Christianity gains in Africa, loses in West; Islam gains converts), switching (Christianity → Nones in West)

---

### Geographic Shifts

**Christianity:** Majority non-Western by 2050
- **Africa:** 1.1 billion (40% of Christians; was 2% in 1900)
- **Latin America:** 680 million
- **Europe:** 450 million (down from 560 million 2010)
- **Implications:** More conservative theology (Africa/Latin America vs liberal West); power shift within denominations

**Islam:** Majority still Middle East/North Africa but growing in Sub-Saharan Africa, Europe (immigration + fertility)
- **Indonesia:** Largest Muslim country (230 million); moderate
- **India:** 300+ million by 2050 (but still minority ~18%)

## Fundamentalism

### Definition

**Characteristics:**
- Literal interpretation of scripture (inerrancy)
- Reaction to modernity (perceived moral decay)
- Certainty; black-white thinking
- Return to "fundamentals" (imagined pure past)
- Often militant, political

**Term Origin:** 1920s US Protestantism; *The Fundamentals* (pamphlets); opposed modernist theology, evolution

---

### Examples Across Religions

**Christian:**
- **US Evangelicals:** Biblical inerrancy; young-earth creationism; opposition to abortion, LGBTQ+, secularism
- **Dominionism:** Christian America; God's law supreme; theocracy

**Islamic:**
- **Wahhabism:** Saudi Arabia; strict Quran/Hadith; reject Sufism, innovation
- **Salafism:** Return to "pious predecessors"; overlaps Wahhabism
- **Islamism:** Political Islam; sharia state; Muslim Brotherhood, Jamaat-e-Islami

**Jewish:**
- **Ultra-Orthodox (Haredi):** Reject modernity; insular communities; strict Halakha
- **Religious Zionism:** Settler movement; biblical land claims; messianic

**Hindu:**
- **Hindutva:** India = Hindu nation; RSS/BJP; anti-Muslim/Christian

**Buddhist:**
- **969 Movement (Myanmar):** Defend Buddhism; anti-Muslim
- **Bodu Bala Sena (Sri Lanka):** "Buddhist Power Force"

---

### Causes

**Modernization Anxiety:** Tradition eroded; identity threat; rapid change → cling to certainty

**Globalization:** Western values imposed; cultural imperialism → resistance

**Secularism:** Religion marginalized in public sphere → backlash

**Humiliation:** Colonial history, Western dominance → reassert pride via religion

**Economic Dislocation:** Inequality, unemployment → seek meaning, community

## New Religious Movements (NRMs)

**Definition:** Religions founded recently (19th-20th century); often syncretic; charismatic leaders

**Estimates:** 10,000+ NRMs globally; mostly small

---

### Examples

**Latter-day Saints (Mormons, 1830):**
- Joseph Smith (founder); Book of Mormon (additional scripture); Jesus visited Americas
- 17 million members; Utah headquarters; missionary-focused
- Controversies: Polygamy (officially ended 1890), racism (Black priesthood ban until 1978), gender roles

**Jehovah's Witnesses (1870s):**
- Charles Taze Russell; no Trinity; 144,000 go to heaven, rest live on paradise Earth
- 8.5 million; door-to-door evangelism; refuse blood transfusions, military service
- Shunning (disfellowshipping); accused of cult-like control

**Scientology (1954):**
- L. Ron Hubbard; alien souls (thetans), trauma (engrams), auditing (counseling)
- ~100,000 members; secretive; celebrity members (Tom Cruise)
- Controversies: Cult allegations, harassment of critics, tax status (religion vs business)

**Baha'i Faith (1863):**
- Baha'u'llah (Persia); progressive revelation (all religions valid stages); unity of humanity
- 5 million members; Iran persecutes (heresy); egalitarian, pacifist
- Haifa, Israel headquarters

**Rastafari (1930s):**
- Jamaica; Haile Selassie (Ethiopia) = divine; Africa repatriation; cannabis sacrament
- <1 million; Bob Marley popularized
- Afrocentric, anti-colonial

**Cao Dai (1926):**
- Vietnam; syncretic (Buddhism, Taoism, Confucianism, Catholicism); Victor Hugo as saint
- 5 million; colorful temples

**Wicca (1950s):**
- Gerald Gardner; modern paganism; witchcraft, nature worship; Goddess + God
- 1 million+; feminist appeal; diverse traditions

---

### Cults vs NRMs

**Cult (Pejorative):** Manipulative, controlling, authoritarian leader; isolation; brainwashing

**NRM (Neutral Academic Term):** Avoids stigma; descriptive

**Characteristics of Destructive Cults:**
- Charismatic leader (living god)
- Isolation from family/society
- Financial exploitation
- Psychological manipulation
- Punishment for leaving

**Examples:** Jonestown (Jim Jones, 1978, 918 dead by cyanide/murder), Heaven's Gate (1997, 39 suicides for UFO), Aum Shinrikyo (1995 sarin attack), Branch Davidians (Waco 1993, 76 dead in siege)

## Interfaith Dialogue

### Goals

**Understanding:** Learn about other religions; reduce ignorance, prejudice

**Cooperation:** Common social action (poverty, environment, peace)

**Theological Exchange:** Compare doctrines; find common ground

---

### Initiatives

**Parliament of World's Religions (1893, Chicago):** First major interfaith gathering; Swami Vivekananda (Hinduism) prominent

**World Council of Churches (1948):** Christian ecumenism; 350+ denominations; Orthodox + Protestant (Catholics observer)

**Vatican II (1962-65):** Catholic opening; ecumenism; "People of God" includes non-Catholics; Muslims worship same God

**Abrahamic Interfaith:** Jews, Christians, Muslims; shared heritage (Abraham); dialogue post-9/11

**United Religions Initiative (2000):** Grassroots; local interfaith cooperation

**Common Word (2007):** 138 Muslim scholars → Christians; common ground (love God, love neighbor)

---

### Challenges

**Theological Exclusivism:** "No salvation outside my religion" vs pluralism

**Proselytizing:** Evangelism vs respect for others' beliefs

**Syncretism Fears:** Diluting distinctiveness

**Political Tensions:** Israel-Palestine, Kashmir, Myanmar → interfaith dialogue difficult

## Science & Religion

### Conflict Thesis (Draper, White, 19th century)

**Claim:** Science and religion inherently opposed; religion declines as science advances

**Examples Cited:** Galileo trial, Darwin opposition, flat-Earth belief

**Critique:** Oversimplified; many scientists religious; Church funded universities, science

---

### Harmony Thesis (NOMA — Gould)

**Claim:** Non-Overlapping Magisteria; science = how (empirical), religion = why (meaning); separate domains

**Support:** Many scientists believe in God (51% in Pew 2009 survey)

**Critique:** Overlap exists (creation, miracles, prayer efficacy); not always separate

---

### Contemporary Debates

**Evolution vs Creationism/Intelligent Design:**
- **Evolution:** Scientific consensus; natural selection, common ancestry
- **Young-Earth Creationism:** 6,000-10,000 year old Earth; literal Genesis; reject evolution
- **Intelligent Design:** Complexity implies designer; "teach the controversy" (rejected by courts as religion, not science)
- **Legal:** *Scopes Trial* (1925), *Edwards v. Aguillard* (1987), *Kitzmiller v. Dover* (2005) — evolution wins

**Bioethics:**
- **Abortion:** Personhood debates; ensoulment; religious conservatives oppose; religious progressives = pro-choice
- **Stem Cell Research:** Embryo = life? (Catholics, evangelicals oppose); others permit
- **Euthanasia:** Sanctity of life vs autonomy; Catholics, Orthodox, most Muslims oppose; some Protestants, Jews permit
- **IVF, Cloning:** Catholic Church opposes; others vary

**Climate Change:**
- **Religious Support:** Pope Francis (*Laudato Si'*, 2015), Dalai Lama, Islamic Declaration on Climate Change; stewardship of creation
- **Religious Opposition:** Some evangelicals skeptical; end-times theology ("God will renew Earth anyway")

## Technology & Religion

**Online Worship:** COVID-19 accelerated; Zoom services, live-streams; permanence debated

**Apps:** Bible apps, prayer times, Quran recitation, meditation (Headspace has Buddhist roots)

**Social Media:** Religious influencers; evangelism; also hate speech, radicalization

**AI Ethics:** Religious input on AI consciousness, moral status

**Transhumanism:** Enhance humans via tech; religious debates (playing God? Or fulfilling divine potential?)

## Gender & Sexuality

### Women's Ordination

**Yes:** Reform/Conservative Jews, Anglicans (1970s+), Lutherans, Methodists, Presbyterians, some Buddhists

**No:** Catholic, Orthodox (Christian), Orthodox Jews, most Muslims, some evangelicals

**Debate:** Tradition ("Jesus chose male apostles") vs equality

---

### LGBTQ+ Inclusion

**Affirming:**
- **Judaism:** Reform, Reconstructionist, Conservative (debated) ordain LGBTQ+; same-sex marriage
- **Christianity:** Episcopalians, ELCA Lutherans, Presbyterians (PCUSA), United Church of Christ, Quakers
- **Buddhism, Hinduism:** Varied; some teachers affirming; Dalai Lama = mixed

**Non-Affirming:**
- **Christianity:** Catholic, Orthodox, Southern Baptists, evangelicals; "love sinner, hate sin"; conversion therapy (discredited)
- **Islam:** Homosexuality = sin in mainstream interpretation; death penalty in 10 countries
- **Judaism:** Orthodox = prohibited (Leviticus)

**Schisms:** Anglican Communion splitting; Methodist split (2019); Presbyterian battles

## Religious Switching & Conversion

**US Trends:**
- Christianity → None: Largest flow; 12% raised Christian now unaffiliated
- Mainline Protestant → Evangelical: Some switching to conservative churches
- Low conversion to Islam, Judaism (ethnic religions; barriers to conversion)
- Pentecostalism: Fastest-growing Christian movement globally; experiential, charismatic

**Motivations:** Marriage (interfaith conversion), spiritual seeking, dissatisfaction, immigration (assimilation)

---

## Religious Persecution

**Scale:** 360 million Christians face persecution (Open Doors); similar for minorities in Muslim countries, Muslims in Myanmar/China

**Examples:**
- **China:** Uyghur Muslims (1M+ in camps, "re-education"), Tibetan Buddhists, Falun Gong, underground Christians
- **North Korea:** Christian = death; worship of Kim family mandatory
- **Pakistan:** Blasphemy laws target Christians, Ahmadis; Asia Bibi case
- **Myanmar:** Rohingya Muslims (genocide)
- **India:** Rising attacks on Muslims, Christians (mob lynchings, church burnings)
- **Saudi Arabia:** No public non-Muslim worship; atheism = terrorism

**International Response:** US International Religious Freedom Act (1998); sanctions; advocacy (limited impact)

## Key Terms

**Secularization:** Decline of religious authority/practice in society; modernity-driven

**Nones:** Religiously unaffiliated; atheists, agnostics, "spiritual but not religious"

**Fundamentalism:** Literal scripture; reactionary; certain; political

**NRM (New Religious Movement):** Recently founded religions; often syncretic

**Interfaith Dialogue:** Communication between religions; understanding, cooperation

**NOMA:** Non-Overlapping Magisteria; science and religion = separate domains (Gould)

**Religious Switching:** Changing affiliation; conversion or disaffiliation

**Religious Persecution:** Discrimination, violence against religious groups

## Summary

Contemporary religion: **Secularization** (Europe churches empty, US Nones growing 26%) vs **vitality** (Global South Christianity/Islam booming, Pentecostalism exploding). **Projections:** Islam nearly equals Christianity by 2050 (demographics); Christianity shifts south (Africa 40% by 2050). **Fundamentalism** reacts to modernity: Christian (creationism, theocracy), Islamic (Wahhabism, Islamism), Jewish (Haredi), Hindu (Hindutva), Buddhist (969); driven by modernization anxiety, globalization backlash. **NRMs** (Mormons 17M, Jehovah's Witnesses 8.5M, Scientology, Baha'i, Wicca) proliferate; cults (Jonestown, Heaven's Gate) vs benign. **Interfaith dialogue** (Parliament of World Religions, Common Word) seeks understanding but challenged by exclusivism, proselytizing, political conflicts. **Science-religion:** Evolution-creationism battles (courts side with evolution), bioethics (abortion, stem cells, euthanasia), climate (religious leaders mostly support action). **Technology** reshapes practice (online worship, apps, social media). **Gender/sexuality:** Women's ordination debated (Reform Jews/Anglicans yes, Catholic/Orthodox no); LGBTQ+ inclusion splits denominations (Anglicans, Methodists schisming). **Switching:** Christianity → Nones largest flow; Pentecostalism fastest-growing. **Persecution:** 360M Christians, Uyghurs (China 1M+ camps), Rohingya (Myanmar genocide), blasphemy laws (Pakistan). Religion remains powerful force shaping politics, identity, conflict, meaning in 21st century.
