# Speciation

## Overview

Speciation is the process by which new species arise. Understanding how one lineage splits into two is central to explaining biodiversity. This involves defining what species are, how reproductive isolation develops, and the geographic contexts in which speciation occurs.

---

## What is a Species?

### Species Concepts

| Concept | Definition | Limitations |
|---------|------------|-------------|
| **Biological (BSC)** | Groups that can interbreed and produce fertile offspring | Doesn't apply to asexuals, fossils |
| **Phylogenetic (PSC)** | Smallest diagnosable cluster with shared ancestry | May split too finely |
| **Morphological** | Distinguishable by physical features | Cryptic species, polymorphism |
| **Ecological** | Distinct ecological niche | Hard to measure |

**Most widely used:** Biological Species Concept (Ernst Mayr, 1942)

### The Species Problem

- No single definition works for all cases
- Hybridization blurs boundaries
- Species are often "fuzzy" categories
- Speciation is a process, not an event

---

## Reproductive Isolation

Barriers preventing gene flow between populations.

### Prezygotic Barriers (Before Fertilization)

| Barrier | Mechanism | Example |
|---------|-----------|---------|
| **Habitat isolation** | Different habitats | Ground vs. tree-dwelling snakes |
| **Temporal isolation** | Different breeding times | Spring vs. fall flowering plants |
| **Behavioral isolation** | Different courtship/signals | Firefly flash patterns |
| **Mechanical isolation** | Incompatible genitalia | Lock-and-key in insects |
| **Gametic isolation** | Sperm/egg incompatibility | Sea urchin species |

### Postzygotic Barriers (After Fertilization)

| Barrier | Mechanism | Example |
|---------|-----------|---------|
| **Hybrid inviability** | Hybrid embryos fail | Sheep-goat hybrids |
| **Hybrid sterility** | Hybrids can't reproduce | Mule (horse × donkey) |
| **Hybrid breakdown** | F2 or later generations fail | Some plant hybrids |

### Evolution of Isolation

- Isolation can evolve as **byproduct** of divergence
- Or be **reinforced** by selection against hybrids
- Multiple barriers often act together

## Geographic Modes of Speciation

### Allopatric Speciation

**Definition:** Speciation due to geographic separation

**Process:**
1. Population divided by barrier (mountain, river, ocean)
2. Gene flow interrupted
3. Populations diverge by selection and/or drift
4. Reproductive isolation evolves
5. If reunited, don't interbreed → two species

**Evidence:**
- Most common mode of speciation
- Island radiations (Galápagos finches, Hawaiian Drosophila)
- Species pairs across geographic barriers

**Vicariance vs. Dispersal:**
- **Vicariance:** Barrier forms, splitting existing range
- **Dispersal:** Colonization of new area (founder event)

### Peripatric Speciation

- Variant of allopatric
- Small peripheral population isolated
- Genetic drift + founder effects important
- May lead to rapid divergence

### Sympatric Speciation

**Definition:** Speciation without geographic separation

**Mechanisms:**
- Disruptive selection
- Assortative mating (like mates with like)
- Polyploidy (instant reproductive isolation)
- Host shifts in parasites/herbivores

**Examples:**
- Cichlid fishes in African lakes
- Apple maggot fly host shift
- Many plant species via polyploidy

**Controversy:**
- Theoretically difficult (gene flow should prevent)
- Requires strong disruptive selection + assortative mating
- Some "sympatric" cases may be microallopatric

### Parapatric Speciation

**Definition:** Speciation with limited geographic contact

- Populations adjacent but partially separated
- Hybrid zone may form at contact
- Divergence despite some gene flow

## Speciation Genetics

### How Many Genes?

- Sometimes few genes with large effect
- Sometimes many genes with small effects
- "Speciation genes" affect reproductive isolation

### Dobzhansky-Muller Incompatibilities

**How hybrid incompatibility evolves:**

1. Ancestral population: genotype AABB
2. Population 1 evolves: AaBB → aaBB
3. Population 2 evolves: AABb → AAbb
4. Hybrid aabb: a and b never tested together → incompatible

**Key insight:** Neither population passes through unfit intermediate

### Chromosomal Speciation

- Chromosome rearrangements can cause hybrid sterility
- Inversions, translocations, fusions
- Create recombination barriers
- May accumulate incompatibilities

## Polyploidy and Instant Speciation

### Types of Polyploidy

**Autopolyploidy:**
- Genome duplication within species
- AAAA from AA
- Instant reproductive isolation from diploids

**Allopolyploidy:**
- Hybridization + genome duplication
- AABB from AA × BB hybrid
- Combines two species' genomes

### Significance

- Common in plants (30-70% of flowering plants)
- Rare in animals
- Instant speciation event
- Often associated with invasiveness, wider ranges

## Hybrid Zones

### Definition

Geographic area where divergent populations meet and hybridize.

### Outcomes

| Outcome | Description |
|---------|-------------|
| **Reinforcement** | Selection strengthens isolation; hybrids less fit |
| **Fusion** | Hybridization erodes differences; one species |
| **Stability** | Hybrid zone persists; balance of selection and gene flow |

### Examples

- European crows (hooded × carrion)
- Fire-bellied toads
- Hybrid sunflower species

---

## Speciation Rate

### Factors Affecting Rate

**Faster speciation:**
- Sexual selection (divergent mate preferences)
- Ecological opportunity (empty niches)
- Small population size (drift)
- Habitat fragmentation

**Slower speciation:**
- High gene flow
- Stabilizing selection
- Large, continuous populations

### Adaptive Radiation

Rapid speciation filling diverse ecological niches:
- Darwin's finches (14 species, varied diets)
- Hawaiian honeycreepers (~50 species from one ancestor)
- Cichlids (hundreds of species in African lakes)

## Key Terms

| Term | Definition |
|------|------------|
| **Speciation** | Formation of new species |
| **Reproductive isolation** | Barriers preventing interbreeding |
| **Allopatric** | Speciation with geographic separation |
| **Sympatric** | Speciation without geographic separation |
| **Polyploidy** | Having more than two chromosome sets |
| **Hybrid zone** | Area where divergent populations interbreed |
| **Reinforcement** | Selection strengthening isolation |
| **Dobzhansky-Muller** | Model for hybrid incompatibility evolution |

---

## Summary

- Species defined primarily by reproductive isolation (BSC)
- Reproductive barriers can be prezygotic or postzygotic
- Allopatric speciation (geographic isolation) is most common
- Sympatric speciation is possible but requires special conditions
- Polyploidy enables instant speciation, especially in plants
- Hybrid zones reveal speciation in action
- Speciation rate varies with sexual selection, ecology, population structure
