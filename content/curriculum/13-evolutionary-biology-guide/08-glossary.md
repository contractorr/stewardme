# Glossary of Evolutionary Biology

Quick reference for key terms organized alphabetically.

---

## A

**Adaptation** - Trait that increases an organism's fitness in its environment; also the process of becoming adapted.

**Adaptive radiation** - Rapid diversification of a lineage into many species filling diverse ecological niches.

**Allopatric speciation** - Speciation occurring due to geographic separation of populations.

**Allele** - A variant form of a gene at a particular locus.

**Analogous structures** - Similar features in unrelated organisms due to convergent evolution (not common ancestry).

**Artificial selection** - Human-directed selection for desired traits in domesticated species.

---

## B

**Biogeography** - Study of geographic distribution of species and its evolutionary causes.

**Biological Species Concept (BSC)** - Species defined as groups that can interbreed and produce viable, fertile offspring.

**Bipedalism** - Walking upright on two legs; defining feature of hominins.

**Bottleneck** - Drastic reduction in population size causing loss of genetic variation.

---

## C

**Clade** - A monophyletic group consisting of an ancestor and all its descendants.

**Coevolution** - Reciprocal evolutionary change between interacting species.

**Convergent evolution** - Independent evolution of similar traits in unrelated lineages.

---

## D

**Directional selection** - Selection favoring one extreme phenotype, shifting population mean.

**Disruptive selection** - Selection favoring both extreme phenotypes over intermediate.

**Dobzhansky-Muller incompatibility** - Hybrid unfitness arising from interactions between genes that evolved separately in diverging populations.

**Drift** - See Genetic drift.

---

## E

**Encephalization** - Evolutionary increase in brain size relative to body size.

**Endosymbiosis** - Theory that mitochondria and chloroplasts originated as engulfed bacteria.

**Evolution** - Change in allele frequencies in a population over generations; descent with modification.

**Evolutionary rescue** - Population survival through rapid adaptive evolution in response to environmental change.

---

## F

**Fitness** - Relative reproductive success of a genotype; contribution to next generation.

**Founder effect** - Reduced genetic diversity when new population established by small number of individuals.

**Fossil** - Preserved remains or traces of organisms from the past.

---

## G

**Gene flow** - Movement of alleles between populations through migration and interbreeding.

**Genetic drift** - Random changes in allele frequencies due to chance sampling; stronger in small populations.

**Genotype** - The genetic constitution of an individual (e.g., AA, Aa, aa).

---

## H

**Hardy-Weinberg equilibrium** - Theoretical state where allele frequencies remain constant in absence of evolution.

**Heritability (h²)** - Proportion of phenotypic variation due to genetic variation.

**Hominin** - Humans and all ancestors since divergence from chimps (~6-7 MYA).

**Homologous structures** - Similar structures due to shared ancestry (e.g., vertebrate limbs).

**Homology** - Similarity due to common descent.

**Horizontal gene transfer** - Gene transfer between organisms other than parent to offspring.

**Hybrid zone** - Geographic area where divergent populations meet and interbreed.

---

## K

**Key innovation** - Novel trait that enables exploitation of new resources or habitats, often triggering adaptive radiation.

---

## L

**LUCA** - Last Universal Common Ancestor; hypothetical organism ancestral to all life.

---

## M

**Macroevolution** - Evolutionary patterns and processes at or above the species level.

**Mass extinction** - Rapid, global loss of a large proportion of species.

**Microevolution** - Evolution within populations; changes in allele frequencies.

**Molecular clock** - Use of molecular divergence to estimate time since common ancestor.

**Monophyletic** - A group containing an ancestor and all of its descendants (= clade).

**Mutation** - Heritable change in DNA sequence; ultimate source of genetic variation.

---

## N

**Natural selection** - Differential survival and reproduction based on heritable traits; mechanism of adaptation.

**Neutral theory** - Theory that most molecular evolution is due to drift of neutral mutations.

---

## O

**Out of Africa** - Theory that modern humans evolved in Africa and dispersed globally ~70-50 kya.

---

## P

**Paraphyletic** - A group containing an ancestor but not all descendants (e.g., "reptiles" excluding birds).

**Phenotype** - Observable characteristics of an organism.

**Phylogeny** - Evolutionary relationships among organisms; the tree of life.

**Polyploidy** - Having more than two complete sets of chromosomes.

**Population genetics** - Study of genetic variation within populations and its change over time.

**Postzygotic isolation** - Reproductive barriers acting after fertilization (hybrid inviability, sterility).

**Prezygotic isolation** - Reproductive barriers acting before fertilization (behavioral, temporal, mechanical).

**Punctuated equilibrium** - Pattern of evolutionary stasis interrupted by rapid change.

---

## R

**Red Queen hypothesis** - Idea that species must constantly evolve to maintain fitness relative to coevolving species.

**Reinforcement** - Evolution of stronger prezygotic isolation when populations with postzygotic isolation come into contact.

**Reproductive isolation** - Barriers preventing gene flow between populations; essential for speciation.

---

## S

**Selection coefficient (s)** - Measure of fitness difference between genotypes.

**Sexual selection** - Selection based on mating success (mate choice, competition for mates).

**Speciation** - The process by which new species arise.

**Species** - Fundamental unit of biological classification; various definitions exist (see BSC).

**Stabilizing selection** - Selection favoring intermediate phenotype; reduces variation.

**Sympatric speciation** - Speciation occurring without geographic separation.

**Synapomorphy** - Shared derived character used to define clades.

---

## T

**Transitional fossil** - Fossil showing intermediate features between ancestral and descendant groups.

---

## V

**Vestigial structure** - Reduced or functionless structure that was functional in ancestors.

**Vicariance** - Splitting of a species' range by formation of a geographic barrier.

---

## Quick Formulas

| Formula | Meaning |
|---------|---------|
| p + q = 1 | Allele frequencies sum to 1 |
| p² + 2pq + q² = 1 | Hardy-Weinberg genotype frequencies |
| R = h² × S | Response to selection |
| dN/dS | Ratio detecting selection (< 1 purifying, > 1 positive) |
| Ne | Effective population size |
