# Human Evolution

## Overview

Human evolution is the study of our lineage from the common ancestor we share with other great apes. Over 6-7 million years, hominins evolved bipedalism, large brains, tool use, and complex culture, eventually giving rise to Homo sapiens.

## Key Hominin Traits

### What Makes a Hominin?

**Defining features:**
1. **Bipedalism** - walking upright on two legs
2. **Reduced canine teeth**
3. **Increased brain size** (over time)

### Bipedalism: The First Major Change

**Skeletal adaptations:**
- Foramen magnum positioned underneath skull
- S-curved spine
- Bowl-shaped pelvis
- Angled femur (knock-kneed)
- Arched foot, non-grasping big toe

**Why evolve bipedalism?**
- Free hands for carrying food/tools
- Energy efficiency for long-distance travel
- Thermoregulation (less sun exposure)
- Seeing over tall grass
- Likely multiple factors

## Fossil Record Overview

### Major Hominin Groups

| Group | Time (MYA) | Key Features |
|-------|------------|--------------|
| Early hominins | 7-4 | Bipedal, small brain, ape-like |
| Australopiths | 4-2 | Fully bipedal, small brain |
| Early Homo | 2.5-1.5 | Larger brain, stone tools |
| Later Homo | 1.5-0.3 | Large brain, complex tools |
| Modern humans | 0.3-present | Globular skull, symbolic thought |

### Important Fossil Species

#### Earliest Hominins

**Sahelanthropus tchadensis** (7 MYA)
- "Toumaï" - Chad
- Possibly bipedal (foramen magnum position)
- Oldest potential hominin

**Ardipithecus ramidus** (4.4 MYA)
- "Ardi" - Ethiopia
- Bipedal but still arboreal
- Grasping big toe

#### Australopiths

**Australopithecus afarensis** (3.9-2.9 MYA)
- "Lucy" - Ethiopia (1974)
- Fully bipedal, small brain (~430 cc)
- Laetoli footprints (3.6 MYA)

**Australopithecus africanus** (3-2 MYA)
- "Taung Child" - South Africa
- Slightly larger brain

**Paranthropus (robust australopiths)** (2.7-1.2 MYA)
- Large jaws, sagittal crest
- Specialized for hard foods
- Evolutionary dead end

#### Genus Homo

**Homo habilis** (2.4-1.4 MYA)
- "Handy man"
- First stone tools (Oldowan)
- Brain ~600-700 cc

**Homo erectus** (1.9-0.1 MYA)
- First hominin to leave Africa
- Brain ~900-1100 cc
- Acheulean hand axes
- Used fire

**Homo heidelbergensis** (0.7-0.3 MYA)
- Common ancestor of Neanderthals and H. sapiens?
- Large brain (~1200 cc)
- Hunted large game

**Homo neanderthalensis** (0.4-0.04 MYA)
- Europe and western Asia
- Large brain (~1500 cc)
- Cold-adapted body
- Buried dead, made art
- Interbred with H. sapiens

**Homo sapiens** (0.3 MYA-present)
- Globular skull, chin
- Brain ~1400 cc
- Complex language, symbolic thought

## Brain Evolution

### Encephalization

| Species | Brain size (cc) | EQ |
|---------|-----------------|-----|
| Chimp | 400 | 2.5 |
| Australopithecus | 450 | 2.5 |
| H. habilis | 650 | 3.1 |
| H. erectus | 950 | 3.3 |
| H. sapiens | 1400 | 5.8 |

**EQ (Encephalization Quotient):** brain size relative to body size

### Why Did Brains Get Bigger?

Hypotheses:
- **Social brain:** complex social groups require intelligence
- **Ecological:** extracting food, tool use
- **Runaway selection:** intelligence sexually selected
- **Cooking:** allowed energy extraction for brain growth

**Costs of large brains:**
- 20% of energy at rest
- Long developmental period
- Difficult childbirth

## Out of Africa

### Multiple Dispersals

| Dispersal | Species | When (MYA) | Where |
|-----------|---------|------------|-------|
| First | H. erectus | ~1.8 | Asia, Europe |
| Second | H. heidelbergensis | ~0.6 | Europe, Asia |
| Third | H. sapiens | ~0.07-0.05 | Global |

### Modern Human Origins

**Out of Africa model (supported by genetics):**
- Modern humans evolved in Africa ~300,000 years ago
- Left Africa ~70,000-50,000 years ago
- Replaced/absorbed other hominins
- All non-Africans descend from this migration

**Genetic evidence:**
- Greatest genetic diversity in Africa
- All non-African populations are subset of African diversity
- Mitochondrial Eve (~200 kya)
- Y-chromosomal Adam (~200-300 kya)

### Interbreeding

Modern human DNA contains:
- **1-4% Neanderthal** (in non-Africans)
- **3-6% Denisovan** (in Melanesians, some Asians)
- Possible archaic African admixture

## Tool Technologies

| Industry | Time (MYA) | Species | Characteristics |
|----------|------------|---------|-----------------|
| Oldowan | 2.6-1.7 | H. habilis | Simple flakes, choppers |
| Acheulean | 1.76-0.13 | H. erectus | Hand axes, symmetry |
| Mousterian | 0.3-0.03 | Neanderthals | Prepared cores, hafting |
| Upper Paleolithic | 0.05-0.01 | H. sapiens | Blades, bone tools, art |

### Behavioral Modernity

Appeared ~50,000-70,000 years ago:
- Symbolic art (cave paintings, figurines)
- Personal ornaments
- Long-distance trade
- Complex burials
- Sophisticated tools
- Possible language development

## Recent Human Evolution

### Ongoing Selection

- Lactase persistence (dairy farming)
- Malaria resistance (sickle cell, G6PD deficiency)
- High-altitude adaptation (Tibet, Andes)
- Skin pigmentation (UV/vitamin D balance)
- Amylase gene copies (starch digestion)

### Human Genetic Diversity

- All humans share 99.9% of DNA
- Genetic differences between "races" < differences within
- Race is not a biological category but social construct
- Most variation is clinal (gradual geographic change)

## Summary

- Humans share common ancestor with chimps ~6-7 MYA
- Bipedalism evolved first; large brains came later
- Australopiths (4-2 MYA) were bipedal but small-brained
- Genus Homo emerged ~2.5 MYA with larger brains and tools
- H. erectus first left Africa ~1.8 MYA
- Modern H. sapiens evolved in Africa ~300,000 years ago
- Global dispersal ~70,000-50,000 years ago with some interbreeding
- Human evolution continues today in response to diet, disease, environment
