# Introduction to Evolution

## Overview

Evolution is the change in heritable characteristics of biological populations over successive generations. It explains the diversity of life on Earth and unifies all of biology.

---

## What is Evolution?

**Biological evolution** = descent with modification. Populations change over time as genetic variants that improve survival and reproduction become more common.

Two key observations:
1. Organisms produce more offspring than can survive
2. Offspring vary in their traits

**Result:** Those with advantageous traits survive and reproduce more → traits become more common over generations.

### Levels of Evolution

| Level | Timescale | Example |
|-------|-----------|---------|
| **Microevolution** | Generations | Pesticide resistance in insects |
| **Macroevolution** | Millions of years | Origin of mammals |

## Historical Development

### Pre-Darwin Ideas

- **Lamarck (1809)** - Proposed inheritance of acquired characteristics (wrong mechanism, but recognized change over time)
- **Cuvier** - Recognized extinction through fossil record
- **Lyell** - Uniformitarianism: Earth shaped by gradual processes over vast time

### Darwin and Wallace

**Charles Darwin (1809-1882)**
- HMS Beagle voyage (1831-1836)
- Observed variation in finches, tortoises across Galápagos Islands
- "On the Origin of Species" (1859)

**Alfred Russel Wallace (1823-1913)**
- Independently conceived natural selection
- Biogeography work in Malay Archipelago
- Joint presentation with Darwin (1858)

**Key insight:** Natural selection as mechanism for evolution

## Evidence for Evolution

### 1. Fossil Record

- **Transitional fossils** show intermediate forms
  - *Tiktaalik*: fish-to-tetrapod transition
  - *Archaeopteryx*: dinosaur-to-bird transition
- **Stratigraphic succession**: older fossils in deeper layers
- Fossils appear in order predicted by evolution

### 2. Comparative Anatomy

**Homologous structures** - Same underlying structure, different functions
- Human arm, whale flipper, bat wing, dog leg all share bone pattern
- Evidence of common ancestor

**Vestigial structures** - Reduced, functionless remnants
- Human appendix, whale pelvis, flightless bird wings

**Analogous structures** - Similar function, different origin (convergent evolution)
- Bird wings vs. insect wings

### 3. Molecular Evidence

- **Universal genetic code** - All life uses same DNA/RNA system
- **Molecular homology** - Shared genes across species
- **Molecular clocks** - DNA differences correlate with divergence time
- **Pseudogenes** - Broken genes shared between related species

### 4. Biogeography

- Island species resemble nearby mainland species
- Continental drift explains distribution patterns
- Endemic species on isolated islands (Madagascar lemurs, Australian marsupials)

### 5. Direct Observation

- Bacteria evolving antibiotic resistance
- Darwin's finches beak changes during droughts
- Peppered moth industrial melanism
- Laboratory evolution experiments (E. coli long-term evolution)

## Common Misconceptions

| Misconception | Reality |
|---------------|---------|
| "Evolution is just a theory" | Scientific theory = well-supported explanation; evolution is fact |
| "Humans evolved from chimps" | Humans and chimps share common ancestor (~6 MYA) |
| "Evolution is random" | Mutation is random; selection is non-random |
| "Evolution means progress" | Evolution = adaptation to environment, not improvement |
| "Individuals evolve" | Populations evolve; individuals are selected |

---

## Key Terms

| Term | Definition |
|------|------------|
| **Evolution** | Change in allele frequencies in a population over generations |
| **Natural selection** | Differential survival/reproduction based on heritable traits |
| **Adaptation** | Trait that increases fitness in an environment |
| **Fitness** | Reproductive success relative to others in population |
| **Homology** | Similarity due to shared ancestry |
| **Convergent evolution** | Similar traits evolving independently |

---

## Summary

- Evolution is descent with modification via natural selection
- Darwin and Wallace independently proposed natural selection (1858-1859)
- Evidence comes from fossils, anatomy, molecules, biogeography, and direct observation
- Evolution explains both unity (common ancestry) and diversity (adaptation) of life
