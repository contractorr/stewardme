# Introduction to Government & Politics

## Overview

Government is the system through which a society makes and enforces collective decisions. Politics is the process of determining who gets what, when, and how. Understanding government matters because it shapes every aspect of daily life—from taxes and education to healthcare and security.

## Key Concepts

### What is Government?

Government is an institution that holds the **legitimate monopoly on the use of force** within a territory (Max Weber's definition). It creates laws, enforces them, and resolves disputes.

**Core Functions:**
- Maintain order and security
- Provide public goods (roads, defense, courts)
- Manage the economy
- Protect rights and liberties
- Redistribute resources

### What is Politics?

Politics encompasses all activities related to governance and power. Harold Lasswell famously defined it as "who gets what, when, and how."

**Key aspects:**
- **Power** - ability to influence others' behavior
- **Authority** - legitimate/accepted power
- **Legitimacy** - public belief that government has right to rule

### The State vs. Government

| Term | Definition |
|------|------------|
| **State** | Political entity with defined territory, population, sovereignty |
| **Government** | Institutions and people who make/enforce decisions for the state |
| **Nation** | Group sharing common identity, culture, language |
| **Nation-state** | State where borders align with national identity |

### Why Government Exists

**Social Contract Theory:** People give up some freedoms in exchange for order and protection.

- **Hobbes** - Without government, life is "nasty, brutish, and short"; strong authority needed
- **Locke** - Government protects natural rights (life, liberty, property); people can overthrow tyranny
- **Rousseau** - Legitimate government based on "general will" of the people

## Real-World Examples

- **Failed states** (Somalia 1990s) show chaos without functioning government
- **COVID-19 response** demonstrated government's role in public health crises
- **Tax policy** debates illustrate different views on government's economic role

| Term | Definition |
|------|------------|
| **Sovereignty** | Supreme authority within a territory |
| **Legitimacy** | Accepted right to rule |
| **Social contract** | Implicit agreement between people and government |
| **Public goods** | Services benefiting all, provided by government |
| **Monopoly on force** | Only government can legally use coercion |

---

## Summary

Government is the institutionalized system for making collective decisions within a society. It derives authority from legitimacy—the belief that it has the right to rule. Political philosophers have long debated why government exists and what its proper scope should be. Understanding these foundations is essential for analyzing any political system.
