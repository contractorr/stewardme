# Glossary of Key Terms

Quick reference for essential government and politics terminology.

---

## A

**Agenda setting** - Media influence on which issues the public considers important.

**Authoritarianism** - Political system with limited pluralism, no guiding ideology demanding mass mobilization; power concentrated in leader or small group.

**Authority** - Legitimate, accepted power to make decisions and command obedience.

## B

**Bicameralism** - Legislature with two chambers (e.g., Senate and House).

**Bureaucracy** - Administrative agencies staffed by appointed officials who implement policy.

## C

**Checks and balances** - System where each government branch can limit the powers of others.

**Civil liberties** - Individual freedoms protected from government interference (speech, religion, assembly).

**Civil society** - Organizations and associations independent of government (NGOs, clubs, religious groups).

**Coalition government** - Government formed by multiple parties sharing power.

**Cohabitation** - In semi-presidential systems, when president and prime minister are from different parties.

**Common law** - Legal system based on judicial precedent and case decisions.

**Communism** - Ideology advocating classless society, collective ownership, abolition of private property.

**Confederation** - Loose alliance of sovereign states with weak central authority.

**Conservatism** - Ideology emphasizing tradition, gradual change, established institutions.

**Constitution** - Fundamental law establishing government structure and limits.

**Constitutional review** - See *Judicial review*.

## D

**Democracy** - Government in which power derives from the people, typically through elections.

**Devolution** - Central government transfers powers to regional/local governments.

**Dictatorship** - Government by single ruler or small group with absolute power.

**Direct democracy** - Citizens vote directly on policies rather than electing representatives.

**Duverger's Law** - Theory that plurality voting systems tend to produce two-party systems.

## E

**Electoral authoritarianism** - Regime holding manipulated elections while maintaining autocratic control.

**Executive** - Branch of government that implements and enforces laws.

## F

**Fascism** - Far-right ideology emphasizing ultra-nationalism, authoritarianism, and rejection of democracy.

**Federalism** - System with constitutionally divided powers between national and regional governments.

**First-past-the-post (FPTP)** - Electoral system where candidate with most votes wins (plurality).

**Fusion of powers** - Executive branch depends on and emerges from legislature (parliamentary systems).

## G

**Gerrymandering** - Manipulating electoral district boundaries for political advantage.

## H

**Head of government** - Leader responsible for running government and making policy (e.g., Prime Minister).

**Head of state** - Official representing the nation, often ceremonial (e.g., monarch, president in parliamentary system).

**Hybrid regime** - Government combining democratic and authoritarian characteristics.

## I

**Ideology** - Coherent system of political beliefs about how society should be organized.

**Interest group** - Organization seeking to influence policy without running for office.

## J

**Judicial review** - Court power to declare laws or actions unconstitutional.

**Judiciary** - Branch of government that interprets laws and resolves disputes.

**Junta** - Military ruling council that has seized power.

## L

**Left-wing** - Political orientation favoring equality, change, and government intervention in economy.

**Legislature** - Branch of government that makes laws.

**Legitimacy** - Belief that government has the right to rule.

**Liberalism** - Ideology emphasizing individual rights, liberty, and (in classical form) limited government.

**Libertarianism** - Ideology advocating minimal government and maximum individual freedom.

**Lobbying** - Attempting to influence government officials on behalf of an interest.

## M

**Majoritarian** - Electoral systems requiring majority (or plurality) to win.

**Mixed-member proportional (MMP)** - Electoral system combining district voting with proportional party lists.

**Monarchy** - Government headed by hereditary ruler (king, queen, emperor).

## N

**Nation** - Group of people sharing common identity, culture, language, or history.

**Nation-state** - State whose borders align with a distinct national group.

**Nationalism** - Ideology prioritizing national identity and interests.

## O

**Oligarchy** - Government by a small, elite group.

## P

**Parliamentary system** - Executive (Prime Minister) depends on legislative confidence; fusion of powers.

**Party identification** - Psychological attachment to a political party.

**Pluralism** - Theory that power is dispersed among many competing groups in society.

**Plurality** - Most votes (not necessarily majority); winner in FPTP system.

**Political culture** - Shared attitudes, values, and beliefs about politics in a society.

**Political party** - Organization that seeks to win elections and control government.

**Political socialization** - Process of developing political attitudes and beliefs.

**Populism** - Political approach appealing to "ordinary people" against "corrupt elites."

**Precedent** - Prior court decision that guides future rulings (common law).

**Presidential system** - Separately elected executive with fixed term; separation of powers.

**Proportional representation (PR)** - Electoral system allocating seats based on party vote shares.

**Public goods** - Services benefiting everyone, typically provided by government (defense, roads).

## R

**Referendum** - Direct popular vote on a specific policy or constitutional question.

**Representative democracy** - Citizens elect officials to make decisions on their behalf.

**Republic** - Government without monarch; power held by people and elected representatives.

**Right-wing** - Political orientation favoring tradition, hierarchy, and free markets.

**Rule of law** - Principle that all are subject to law, applied equally and fairly.

## S

**Semi-presidential system** - System with both directly elected president and prime minister responsible to legislature.

**Separation of powers** - Division of government into independent executive, legislative, and judicial branches.

**Social contract** - Theory that government legitimacy comes from implicit agreement with the governed.

**Social movement** - Sustained collective action seeking social or political change.

**Socialism** - Ideology advocating collective ownership and economic equality.

**Sovereignty** - Supreme authority within a territory; independence from external control.

**State** - Political entity with defined territory, population, government, and sovereignty.

**Suffrage** - The right to vote.

## T

**Theocracy** - Government by religious leaders according to divine law.

**Totalitarianism** - Extreme authoritarianism seeking total control over society through ideology and terror.

**Turnout** - Percentage of eligible voters who cast ballots.

## U

**Unicameral** - Legislature with single chamber.

**Unitary system** - Central government holds supreme authority; subnational units exist at its discretion.

## V

**Vote of no confidence** - Legislative vote removing government from power (parliamentary systems).

## W

**Welfare state** - Government providing social safety net (healthcare, unemployment, pensions).

## Quick Reference Tables

### Government Systems at a Glance

| System | Executive | Legislature Relationship | Example |
|--------|-----------|-------------------------|---------|
| Presidential | Directly elected | Separate, independent | USA |
| Parliamentary | From legislature | Fused, depends on confidence | UK |
| Semi-presidential | Both elected president and PM | Shared power | France |

### Electoral Systems at a Glance

| System | How It Works | Typical Result |
|--------|--------------|----------------|
| FPTP | Plurality wins district | Two-party system |
| Party-list PR | Seats match vote shares | Multi-party |
| MMP | District + proportional seats | Multi-party |
| Ranked choice | Eliminate lowest, redistribute | Moderate winners |
