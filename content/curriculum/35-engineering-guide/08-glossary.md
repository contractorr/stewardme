# Engineering Glossary

## A

**Absorption**: Process where gas dissolves into liquid (e.g., CO₂ removal from flue gas)

**AC (Alternating Current)**: Electric current that periodically reverses direction; standard for power grids

**Accelerated Life Testing**: Subjecting components to higher stress to induce failures faster

**Actuator**: Device that converts control signal into physical action (valve, motor)

**Adiabatic**: Process with no heat transfer to/from surroundings

**Aerodynamics**: Study of air flow around objects; critical for aircraft, vehicles

**Airfoil**: Wing-shaped surface designed to generate lift

**Alloy**: Mixture of metals (or metal + non-metal) with enhanced properties

**AM (Amplitude Modulation)**: Encoding information by varying carrier wave amplitude

**Amplifier**: Electronic circuit increasing signal amplitude (voltage, current, or power)

**Analog**: Continuous signal (contrast with digital/discrete)

**Angle of Attack**: Angle between airfoil chord and oncoming flow

**Annealing**: Heat treatment to reduce hardness, increase ductility

**Anode**: Positive electrode; electrons flow toward it

**API**: Active Pharmaceutical Ingredient (drug substance)

**Aquifer**: Underground water-bearing rock layer

**Arch**: Curved structure transferring loads primarily through compression

**Availability**: Fraction of time system is operational; MTBF/(MTBF+MTTR)

## B

**Bandwidth**: Range of frequencies in signal or system capacity

**Base Isolation**: Decoupling building from ground motion using bearings (earthquake protection)

**Batch Process**: Producing discrete quantities (contrast with continuous)

**Bearing**: Component supporting rotating shaft while allowing motion

**Bearing Capacity**: Maximum load soil can support without failure

**Bernoulli's Equation**: Energy conservation in fluid flow; p + ½ρv² + ρgh = constant

**Biofuel**: Fuel from biological sources (ethanol from corn, biodiesel from vegetable oil)

**Bipolar Junction Transistor (BJT)**: Three-layer semiconductor amplifier/switch

**Boiler**: Heat exchanger generating steam from water

**Boundary Condition**: Constraint at edge of domain (physics or math)

**Brayton Cycle**: Thermodynamic cycle for gas turbines (compression, combustion, expansion)

**Brittle**: Material fracturing with little plastic deformation (glass, concrete)

**Buckling**: Sudden sideways collapse of slender member under compression

**Buoyancy**: Upward force on object in fluid; equals weight of displaced fluid

**Bypass Ratio**: Air through fan vs. core in turbofan engine

## C

**CAD (Computer-Aided Design)**: Software for creating engineering drawings and 3D models

**Caisson**: Watertight structure for working underwater; also type of deep foundation

**Capacitor**: Electronic component storing energy in electric field

**Carnot Efficiency**: Maximum theoretical heat engine efficiency; 1 - T_cold/T_hot

**Catalyst**: Substance speeding reaction without being consumed

**Cathode**: Negative electrode; electrons flow from it

**Cellular Network**: Wireless network dividing area into cells with base stations

**Centrifugal Pump**: Pump using rotating impeller to increase fluid pressure

**Ceramic**: Inorganic non-metallic solid (hard, brittle, heat-resistant)

**CFD (Computational Fluid Dynamics)**: Numerical solution of fluid flow equations

**Characteristic Equation**: det(A - λI) = 0; used to find eigenvalues

**Chemical Reactor**: Vessel where chemical reactions occur (batch, CSTR, PFR, etc.)

**Circuit**: Closed path for electric current flow

**Circular Economy**: Economic system where materials perpetually circulate (vs. linear take-make-dispose)

**Closed-Loop Control**: Control system using output feedback

**CMOS**: Complementary Metal-Oxide-Semiconductor; dominant IC technology

**Coefficient of Performance (COP)**: Heat moved per work input (refrigeration, heat pumps)

**Coking**: Process converting heavy oil to lighter products + solid carbon (coke)

**Column**: Vertical structural member carrying compression loads

**Combined Cycle**: Power plant using gas turbine + steam turbine (highest fossil efficiency ~60%)

**Composite**: Material combining two or more constituents (fiberglass, carbon fiber-epoxy)

**Compressive Stress**: Force per area pushing surfaces together

**Condenser**: Heat exchanger condensing vapor to liquid (releases latent heat)

**Conduction**: Heat transfer through material via molecular collisions

**Continuous Stirred-Tank Reactor (CSTR)**: Well-mixed reactor with continuous flow

**Convection**: Heat transfer via fluid motion

**Conversion**: Fraction of reactant converted to products in chemical reactor

**Corrosion**: Chemical degradation of materials (rust, oxidation)

**Cracking**: Breaking large hydrocarbon molecules into smaller (thermal or catalytic)

**Creep**: Slow permanent deformation under constant stress (especially at high temperature)

**Critical Path**: Longest sequence of dependent activities; determines project duration

**Crystallization**: Precipitation of pure solid from solution

**CSTR**: See Continuous Stirred-Tank Reactor

**Curvature**: Measure of how curved a surface or line is

**Cycle**: Thermodynamic process returning to initial state

## D

**DAC (Digital-to-Analog Converter)**: Converts digital signal to analog voltage

**Dam**: Barrier impounding water (gravity, arch, embankment types)

**Damper**: Device dissipating energy (vibration control)

**Darcy-Weisbach Equation**: Relates pressure drop to pipe friction

**DC (Direct Current)**: Electric current flowing in one direction with constant voltage

**DCS (Distributed Control System)**: Computer system controlling industrial plant

**Dead Load**: Permanent structural weight (self-weight, fixed equipment)

**Derating**: Operating component below rated capacity (improves reliability)

**Desalination**: Removing salt from seawater (reverse osmosis, distillation)

**Design for Manufacturing (DFM)**: Designing products for ease of production

**Diesel Cycle**: Thermodynamic cycle for diesel engines (high compression, constant-pressure combustion)

**Digital Twin**: Virtual replica of physical system updated with real-time data

**Diode**: Semiconductor allowing current flow in one direction only

**Distillation**: Separation based on boiling point differences

**DMAIC**: Define-Measure-Analyze-Improve-Control (Six Sigma methodology)

**Dopant**: Impurity added to semiconductor to control conductivity

**Drag**: Force opposing motion through fluid

**Drag Coefficient**: Dimensionless measure of drag for given shape

**Ductile**: Material undergoing significant plastic deformation before fracture (opposite of brittle)

**Dynamic Load**: Time-varying force (wind gusts, earthquakes, vehicle impact)

## E

**Earthquake Engineering**: Designing structures to resist seismic forces

**Eigenvalue**: Scalar λ where A**v** = λ**v** for matrix A and vector **v**

**Elastic Deformation**: Reversible deformation (returns to original shape when load removed)

**Electric Motor**: Converts electrical energy to mechanical rotation

**Electrolysis**: Using electricity to drive chemical reaction (e.g., water → H₂ + O₂)

**Electromagnetism**: Physics of electric and magnetic fields and their interactions

**Embedded System**: Computer system integrated into physical device (not general-purpose)

**Emissivity**: Measure of surface's thermal radiation efficiency

**Energy Balance**: Energy in = Energy out + Accumulation

**Enthalpy**: Heat content; H = U + PV

**Entropy**: Measure of disorder; increases in isolated system (2nd Law of Thermodynamics)

**Equilibrium**: State where net force and moment are zero (statics)

**Erosion**: Wearing away of material by fluid flow or particles

**Euler Buckling**: Sudden lateral collapse of column under compression

**Evaporator**: Heat exchanger vaporizing liquid (absorbs latent heat)

**Exothermic**: Chemical reaction releasing heat

**Extraction**: Separation by transferring component between immiscible liquid phases

## F

**Factor of Safety**: Ratio of capacity to expected load (overdesign margin)

**Failure Mode**: How component/system can fail (yielding, fracture, fatigue, buckling, etc.)

**Fatigue**: Failure from repeated cyclic loading below ultimate strength

**FCC (Fluid Catalytic Cracking)**: Process breaking heavy oil into gasoline using catalyst

**FEA (Finite Element Analysis)**: Numerical method for solving engineering problems by discretization

**Feedback Control**: Control using output measurements to adjust inputs

**Feedforward Control**: Control adjusting for disturbances before they affect output

**Fermentation**: Microbial conversion (e.g., sugar → ethanol; used in brewing, biofuels, antibiotics)

**FET (Field-Effect Transistor)**: Voltage-controlled semiconductor switch; includes MOSFET

**Fiber Optics**: Light transmission through glass fibers (high bandwidth, low loss)

**Fick's Law**: Diffusion rate proportional to concentration gradient

**Filter**: Circuit/device allowing certain frequencies, blocking others (low-pass, high-pass, band-pass)

**Finite Element Method**: See FEA

**First Law of Thermodynamics**: Energy conservation; ΔU = Q - W

**Fluidized Bed**: Solid particles suspended by upward fluid flow (excellent mixing, heat transfer)

**FM (Frequency Modulation)**: Encoding information by varying carrier frequency

**FOS**: See Factor of Safety

**Fouling**: Accumulation of deposits on heat transfer surfaces (reduces performance)

**Foundation**: Structural element transferring loads to ground (shallow or deep)

**Fourier's Law**: Heat conduction rate proportional to temperature gradient

**Fracture**: Material breaking into pieces

**Free Body Diagram (FBD)**: Isolated object showing all forces acting on it

**Frequency**: Number of cycles per second (Hertz, Hz)

**Friction**: Force opposing sliding between surfaces

## G

**Galvanic Corrosion**: Accelerated corrosion when dissimilar metals in contact

**Gas Turbine**: Engine using Brayton cycle (compressor, combustor, turbine)

**Gate**: Control terminal in FET; voltage controls current flow

**Gaussian Elimination**: Algorithm solving systems of linear equations

**Gear**: Toothed wheel transmitting rotational motion and torque

**Generator**: Converts mechanical rotation to electrical power

**Geotechnical Engineering**: Engineering dealing with soil, rock, groundwater

**GPS (Global Positioning System)**: Satellite navigation system

**Gradient**: Vector of partial derivatives; points in direction of steepest increase

**Green Chemistry**: Designing chemical products/processes to reduce hazards and environmental impact

**Grid**: Electrical network distributing power from generators to consumers

**Grout**: Fluid mixture (cement, sand, water) filling gaps, stabilizing structures

## H

**Haber-Bosch Process**: Industrial ammonia synthesis (N₂ + H₂ → NH₃); feeds ~50% of humanity

**Half-life**: Time for quantity to reduce to half (radioactive decay, chemical reactions)

**HALT (Highly Accelerated Life Testing)**: Find design weaknesses by stressing to failure

**Hardware-in-the-Loop (HIL)**: Testing with real hardware + simulated components

**Harmonic**: Integer multiple of fundamental frequency

**Hazard**: Potential source of harm

**HAZOP (Hazard and Operability Study)**: Systematic safety analysis technique

**Heat Capacity**: Energy required to raise temperature by 1°C

**Heat Engine**: Device converting thermal energy to work (follows thermodynamic cycle)

**Heat Exchanger**: Device transferring heat between fluids

**Heat Pump**: Device moving heat from cold to hot (requires work input)

**Heat Treatment**: Controlled heating/cooling to alter material properties

**Hooke's Law**: Stress proportional to strain in elastic region (σ = Eε)

**HVAC**: Heating, Ventilation, and Air Conditioning

**Hydraulic**: Using pressurized liquid to transmit power

**Hydrocarbon**: Compound of hydrogen and carbon (oil, natural gas)

**Hysteresis**: Path-dependent behavior; different loading vs. unloading curves

## I

**I-beam**: Beam with I-shaped cross-section (efficient for bending)

**IC (Integrated Circuit)**: Multiple electronic components on single chip

**ICD (Interface Control Document)**: Specifies how components connect/interact

**Impedance**: Opposition to AC current flow (generalizes resistance to include reactance)

**Impulse Turbine**: Turbine driven by high-velocity jet striking buckets

**Inductor**: Electronic component storing energy in magnetic field

**Induction Motor**: AC motor where rotating magnetic field induces rotor current

**Inertia**: Resistance to acceleration (mass for linear, moment of inertia for rotational)

**Injection Molding**: Manufacturing process forcing molten plastic into mold

**Instrumentation**: Sensors and measurement devices

**Insulation**: Material reducing heat transfer (or electrical conduction)

**Integrated Circuit**: See IC

**Interface**: Boundary where systems/components connect and interact

**Inverter**: Converts DC to AC (used in motor drives, solar, electric vehicles)

**Ionic**: Type of chemical bond (electrostatic attraction); ceramics typically ionic

**Irreversible**: Process that cannot be reversed without external work (entropy increases)

**ISO**: International Organization for Standardization; publishes technical standards

**Isothermal**: Constant temperature process

## J

**JIT (Just-In-Time)**: Manufacturing philosophy producing only what's needed when needed (reduces inventory)

**Joule**: Unit of energy; 1 J = 1 N·m = 1 W·s

**Junction**: (1) Connection point in circuit (2) Interface in semiconductor (PN junction)

## K

**Kaizen**: Continuous improvement philosophy (small incremental changes)

**Kinematics**: Study of motion without considering forces

**Kinetics**: (1) Study of motion considering forces (2) Rates of chemical reactions

**kW (Kilowatt)**: 1000 Watts; unit of power

**kWh (Kilowatt-hour)**: Unit of energy; 1 kWh = 3.6 MJ

## L

**Lagrange Multiplier**: Method for constrained optimization

**Laminar Flow**: Smooth, layered fluid flow (low Reynolds number)

**Latent Heat**: Energy absorbed/released during phase change (without temperature change)

**LCA (Lifecycle Assessment)**: Analysis of environmental impacts across product's full life

**Lean Manufacturing**: Toyota-developed system eliminating waste (muda)

**LED (Light-Emitting Diode)**: Semiconductor emitting light when current flows

**Lift**: Force perpendicular to fluid flow direction (enables flight)

**Linear Programming**: Optimization of linear objective with linear constraints

**Liquefaction**: (1) Converting gas to liquid (2) Soil losing strength, behaving like liquid (earthquake hazard)

**Live Load**: Temporary, variable structural loads (occupants, vehicles, snow)

**Load**: Force or system of forces acting on structure

**Load Factor**: Ratio of average to peak load

**Logic Gate**: Digital circuit implementing Boolean function (AND, OR, NOT, etc.)

**LQR (Linear Quadratic Regulator)**: Optimal control method minimizing quadratic cost

## M

**Machining**: Removing material to create part (turning, milling, drilling)

**Mass Balance**: Mass in = Mass out + Accumulation

**Mass Transfer**: Movement of chemical species (diffusion, convection)

**Material Science**: Study of material structure-property relationships

**MBSE (Model-Based Systems Engineering)**: Using central system model vs. text documents

**Mean Time Between Failures (MTBF)**: Average time until next failure; 1/(failure rate)

**Mean Time To Repair (MTTR)**: Average time to fix failure

**Membrane**: Selective barrier allowing certain species to pass

**Metallurgy**: Science of metals and their properties

**Microprocessor**: Computer CPU on single integrated circuit

**Modulation**: Encoding information onto carrier wave (AM, FM, phase, etc.)

**Modulus of Elasticity**: See Young's Modulus

**Moisture Content**: Water content in material (affects properties)

**Molding**: Shaping material in mold (casting, injection molding, compression molding)

**Moment**: Rotational effect of force; torque; τ = F × d

**Moment of Inertia**: (1) Resistance to angular acceleration (2) Geometric property for beam bending

**Momentum**: Mass times velocity (conserved quantity)

**Moore's Law**: Transistor count doubles every ~2 years (held 1965-2020s)

**MOSFET**: Metal-Oxide-Semiconductor FET; dominant transistor in digital circuits

**Motor**: Device converting electrical to mechanical energy

## N

**Nanoparticle**: Particle 1-100 nm in size (unique properties at nanoscale)

**Nanotechnology**: Engineering at molecular scale (1-100 nm)

**Natural Frequency**: Frequency at which system oscillates when disturbed

**Navier-Stokes Equations**: Fundamental equations governing fluid motion

**Neural Network**: Computing system inspired by biological neurons (machine learning)

**Neutron**: Electrically neutral subatomic particle in nucleus

**Newton (N)**: Unit of force; 1 N = 1 kg·m/s²

**Newton's Laws**: Three laws of motion (inertia, F=ma, action-reaction)

**Nominal**: Intended or specified value (vs. actual)

**Non-Destructive Testing (NDT)**: Inspecting without damaging (ultrasound, X-ray, etc.)

**Nonlinear**: Not proportional; superposition doesn't apply

**NP-Complete**: Hardest problems verifiable in polynomial time

**N-type Semiconductor**: Doped with extra electrons (negative charge carriers)

**Nuclear Reactor**: Device initiating and controlling nuclear fission chain reaction

**Numerical Method**: Algorithm approximating solution to mathematical problem

## O

**Ohm (Ω)**: Unit of electrical resistance

**Ohm's Law**: V = IR (voltage = current × resistance)

**Open-Loop Control**: Control without feedback

**Operational Amplifier (Op-Amp)**: High-gain differential amplifier (versatile analog building block)

**Optimization**: Finding best solution from feasible set (maximize/minimize objective)

**Oscillation**: Repetitive back-and-forth variation

**Otto Cycle**: Thermodynamic cycle for gasoline engines (compression, constant-volume combustion)

**Overdesign**: Designing with more capacity than required (provides safety margin)

**Oxidation**: Loss of electrons; chemical reaction with oxygen

## P

**Parallel Circuit**: Components connected across same voltage (currents add)

**Particle Accelerator**: Device accelerating charged particles to high speeds (research, medical)

**Pascal (Pa)**: Unit of pressure; 1 Pa = 1 N/m²

**Passive**: Not requiring external power (passive filter uses only R, L, C)

**Permeability**: (1) Soil: ability to transmit water (2) Magnetic: ability to support magnetic field

**Phase**: (1) State of matter (solid, liquid, gas) (2) Angle in AC signal

**Phase Diagram**: Shows equilibrium phases vs. temperature/pressure/composition

**PID Controller**: Proportional-Integral-Derivative controller (ubiquitous in control)

**Pile**: Deep foundation element (driven or drilled) transferring load to depth

**Piping**: System of pipes, valves, fittings transporting fluids

**Piston**: Cylindrical component moving in cylinder (engines, pumps, compressors)

**Plastic Deformation**: Permanent (irreversible) deformation

**PLC (Programmable Logic Controller)**: Industrial computer for automation

**Plug Flow Reactor (PFR)**: Tubular reactor with composition gradient along length

**PN Junction**: Interface between P-type and N-type semiconductor (forms diode)

**Poisson's Ratio**: Lateral strain / axial strain (material property)

**Polymerization**: Joining monomers into polymer chains

**Porosity**: Fraction of material volume that is void space

**Power**: Rate of energy transfer; P = dW/dt = dE/dt

**Power Electronics**: Electronics for converting/controlling electrical power (inverters, converters)

**Power Factor**: Real power / apparent power (measures AC power efficiency)

**Prestressed Concrete**: Concrete with internal compression from tensioned tendons

**Pressure**: Force per unit area (Pa, psi)

**Pressure Drop**: Reduction in pressure along flow path (due to friction, fittings)

**Prime Mover**: Engine or turbine providing mechanical power

**Process Control**: Maintaining process variables at desired values

**PSM (Process Safety Management)**: Framework for preventing chemical process accidents

**P-type Semiconductor**: Doped with electron deficiencies/"holes" (positive charge carriers)

**Pump**: Device increasing fluid pressure

**Pyrolysis**: Thermal decomposition in absence of oxygen

## Q

**QAM (Quadrature Amplitude Modulation)**: Digital modulation varying both amplitude and phase

**Quality Control**: Ensuring products meet specifications

**Quality Factor (Q)**: (1) Resonance sharpness (2) Energy stored / energy dissipated per cycle

**Quantization**: Converting continuous signal to discrete levels (ADC)

**Quantum Computing**: Computing using quantum superposition and entanglement

**Quenching**: Rapid cooling (heat treatment) to achieve desired properties

## R

**Radiation**: (1) Heat transfer via electromagnetic waves (2) Emission of particles/energy from radioactive decay

**Rankine Cycle**: Thermodynamic cycle for steam power plants

**Rated**: Specified maximum (rated power, rated voltage, etc.)

**Reactant**: Starting material in chemical reaction

**Reaction Turbine**: Turbine driven by pressure change across blades

**Reactor**: See Chemical Reactor

**Rebar (Reinforcing Bar)**: Steel bars in reinforced concrete providing tensile strength

**Rectifier**: Converts AC to DC (uses diodes)

**Redundancy**: Backup components for reliability

**Refrigerant**: Fluid undergoing phase changes in refrigeration cycle

**Refrigeration Cycle**: Thermodynamic cycle moving heat from cold to hot

**Regenerative Braking**: Converting vehicle kinetic energy to electrical (charges battery)

**Regression Analysis**: Statistical method modeling relationships between variables

**Reinforced Concrete**: Concrete with embedded steel bars providing tensile strength

**Reliability**: Probability system functions correctly for specified time

**Renewable Energy**: Energy from replenishing sources (solar, wind, hydro, biomass)

**Requirements**: Specifications of what system must do and how well

**Reservoir**: (1) Large water storage (2) Thermal reservoir in thermodynamics

**Resilience**: Ability to absorb energy elastically (area under stress-strain curve)

**Resistance**: Opposition to current flow (Ohms)

**Resistor**: Component dissipating electrical energy as heat

**Resonance**: Large amplitude response when driving frequency matches natural frequency

**Retaining Wall**: Structure holding back soil

**Reverse Osmosis**: Forcing water through membrane to remove dissolved solids (desalination)

**Reynolds Number**: ρvD/μ; dimensionless ratio determining laminar/turbulent flow

**Rigid Body**: Idealization where object doesn't deform

**RMS (Root Mean Square)**: Effective value; V_rms = V_peak/√2 for sinusoidal AC

**Robot**: Programmable mechanical manipulator

**Root Cause Analysis**: Identifying underlying reason for failure (vs. symptoms)

**Rotational Dynamics**: Study of rotating motion (torque, angular momentum, moment of inertia)

**RSA**: Public-key cryptography algorithm (widely used)

## S

**Safety Factor**: See Factor of Safety

**Sampling**: Measuring signal at discrete time intervals (ADC)

**Saturation**: (1) Maximum output of nonlinear device (2) Soil voids completely filled with water

**Scale-up**: Increasing process from lab to industrial scale (major chemical engineering challenge)

**Second Law of Thermodynamics**: Entropy of isolated system increases; heat engines limited efficiency

**Seismic**: Related to earthquakes

**Selectivity**: Desired product / byproducts in chemical reactor

**Semiconductor**: Material with controllable conductivity (silicon, gallium arsenide)

**Sensor**: Device measuring physical quantity (temperature, pressure, position, etc.)

**Series Circuit**: Components connected end-to-end (same current, voltages add)

**Settlement**: Vertical compression of soil/foundation

**Shear**: Force parallel to surface; causes sliding or tearing

**Shear Stress**: Shear force per unit area

**Shear Wall**: Structural wall resisting lateral loads

**Shell-and-Tube Heat Exchanger**: One fluid through tubes, other around tubes (most common type)

**Short Circuit**: Unintended low-resistance path (high current, dangerous)

**SI Units**: International System of Units (meter, kilogram, second, etc.)

**Signal**: Time-varying quantity carrying information

**Signal Processing**: Analyzing, manipulating, interpreting signals

**Simplex Algorithm**: Method solving linear programming problems

**Simulation**: Using computational model to study system behavior

**Six Sigma**: Quality methodology targeting 3.4 defects per million

**Skyscraper**: Very tall building (lateral loads dominate design)

**Smart Grid**: Electrical grid with two-way communication, automation, distributed generation

**Soil Mechanics**: Study of soil behavior under load

**Solar Cell**: Semiconductor converting light to electricity (photovoltaic)

**Solenoid**: Coil generating magnetic field when current flows (used in valves, actuators)

**Solidification**: Phase change from liquid to solid

**Span**: Distance between structural supports

**Specific Heat**: Energy to raise 1 kg by 1°C

**Spectral Analysis**: Decomposing signal into frequency components (Fourier analysis)

**Spillway**: Channel releasing excess water from dam

**Stability**: (1) Structure: resist overturning/buckling (2) Control: system returns to equilibrium after disturbance (3) Chemical: resist decomposition

**Stall**: Sudden loss of lift when airfoil angle too steep (flow separation)

**Standard**: Established specification or test method (ASTM, ISO, ASME, IEEE)

**Static**: Not moving or changing with time

**Steady State**: System properties not changing with time (dX/dt = 0)

**Steam Cracking**: Heating hydrocarbons to 800-900°C to produce ethylene, propylene

**Steel**: Iron alloy with carbon <2% (strong, ductile, versatile)

**Stefan-Boltzmann Law**: Radiation heat transfer proportional to T⁴

**Stoichiometry**: Quantitative relationships in chemical reactions

**Strain**: Deformation per unit length; ε = ΔL/L

**Strength**: Maximum stress material can withstand before failure

**Stress**: Force per unit area; σ = F/A

**Stress-Strain Curve**: Graph of stress vs. strain (characterizes material behavior)

**Structural Engineering**: Design of load-bearing structures

**Subsidence**: Sinking of ground surface

**Substrate**: (1) Reactant in enzymatic reaction (2) Base material (e.g., silicon wafer)

**Superconductor**: Material with zero electrical resistance below critical temperature

**Superposition**: Principle that total response equals sum of individual responses (linear systems)

**Surge**: Sudden pressure/flow increase (piping systems)

**Suspension Bridge**: Bridge with deck hanging from cables

**Sustainability**: Meeting present needs without compromising future generations' ability

**Synchronous Generator**: AC generator where rotor speed locked to frequency

**System**: Set of interacting components forming complex whole

**Systems Engineering**: Interdisciplinary approach to complex system development

## T

**Tensile Strength**: Maximum tensile stress before fracture

**Tensile Stress**: Force per area pulling surfaces apart

**Thermal Conductivity**: Material property; heat flow per unit area per unit temperature gradient

**Thermal Expansion**: Change in size with temperature

**Thermodynamics**: Science of heat, work, and energy transformations

**Thermoelectric**: Converting temperature difference to electricity (or vice versa)

**Thermoset**: Polymer that hardens permanently when cured (cannot be remelted)

**Thermoplastic**: Polymer that softens when heated (can be remelted and reformed)

**Three-Phase Power**: Three AC voltages 120° apart (efficient transmission, simpler motors)

**Torque**: Rotational moment; τ = F × r

**Toughness**: Energy absorbed before fracture (area under stress-strain curve)

**Transformer**: Device changing AC voltage via electromagnetic induction

**Transient**: Temporary behavior during transition (not steady state)

**Transistor**: Semiconductor device; electrically-controlled switch/amplifier

**Transmission Line**: High-voltage power line

**Truss**: Framework of members forming triangles (efficient structure)

**Turbine**: Device extracting energy from fluid flow (steam, gas, hydro)

**Turbulence**: Chaotic fluid flow with eddies and mixing (high Reynolds number)

**Turbulent Flow**: See Turbulence

## U

**Ultimate Strength**: Maximum stress before material breaks

**Ultrasonic**: Sound waves above human hearing (>20 kHz); used in testing, cleaning, medical imaging

**Uncertainty**: Lack of exact knowledge; quantified in measurements and predictions

**Unit Operation**: Standard processing step (distillation, filtration, etc.)

**UPS (Uninterruptible Power Supply)**: Battery backup providing temporary power during outage

**Utilization**: Actual use / capacity (higher = more efficient resource use)

## V

**Vacuum**: Pressure below atmospheric

**Validation**: Confirming system meets stakeholder needs (vs. verification: meets requirements)

**Valve**: Device controlling fluid flow (on/off, throttling, check, relief, etc.)

**Vapor Pressure**: Pressure exerted by vapor in equilibrium with liquid

**Variable-Frequency Drive (VFD)**: Controls AC motor speed by varying frequency

**VEE Model**: V-shaped systems engineering process (requirements → design → test)

**Velocity**: Rate of position change; vector with magnitude and direction

**Verification**: Confirming system meets requirements through test/analysis

**Vibration**: Oscillating mechanical motion

**Viscosity**: Fluid's resistance to flow (internal friction)

**VLSI (Very Large-Scale Integration)**: IC with 100,000+ transistors

**Volt (V)**: Unit of voltage (electrical potential difference)

**Voltage**: Electrical potential difference; energy per unit charge

**Voltage Drop**: Decrease in voltage due to resistance (V = IR)

**Volume**: Three-dimensional space occupied

## W

**Wastewater**: Used water requiring treatment before environmental release

**Watt (W)**: Unit of power; 1 W = 1 J/s

**Wave**: Disturbance propagating through medium or space

**Wear**: Gradual material loss from surfaces due to friction, erosion, corrosion

**Weld**: Joining metals by melting and fusing

**Wireless**: Communication without physical connection (radio, cellular, WiFi, Bluetooth)

**Work**: Force times distance; W = F·d; energy transfer

**Work Breakdown Structure (WBS)**: Hierarchical task decomposition for project management

**Working Fluid**: Fluid undergoing thermodynamic cycle (steam, air, refrigerant)

## X

**X-ray**: High-energy electromagnetic radiation; used in imaging, inspection

## Y

**Yield**: (1) Product obtained from reaction (2) Yield strength: stress where permanent deformation begins

**Yield Strength**: Stress at which material begins permanent deformation

**Young's Modulus (E)**: Material stiffness; stress/strain in elastic region

## Z

**Zero Defects**: Quality goal of no defects (theoretical; Six Sigma targets 3.4 per million)

**Zeroth Law of Thermodynamics**: If A and B in equilibrium with C, then A and B in equilibrium (defines temperature)
