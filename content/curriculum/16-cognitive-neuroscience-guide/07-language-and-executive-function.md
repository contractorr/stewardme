# Language and Executive Function

## Overview

Language is the human capacity for acquiring and using complex communication systems. Executive functions are the cognitive control processes that regulate thought and action. Both are hallmarks of human cognition and depend heavily on frontal and temporal cortical regions. This chapter covers the neural basis of language processing and the prefrontal cortex's role in cognitive control.

## Key Concepts

### Language: Core Components

| Component | Definition | Example |
|-----------|------------|---------|
| **Phonology** | Sound system | /k/, /æ/, /t/ → "cat" |
| **Morphology** | Word structure | un- + break + -able |
| **Syntax** | Sentence structure | Word order, grammar |
| **Semantics** | Meaning | "Cat" means furry feline |
| **Pragmatics** | Language in context | Sarcasm, indirect requests |

### Classical Language Model

**Wernicke-Geschwind Model:**

```
Heard speech → Auditory cortex → Wernicke's area (comprehension)
                                        ↓
                              Arcuate fasciculus
                                        ↓
                              Broca's area (production)
                                        ↓
                              Motor cortex → Speech
```

**Broca's Area (Left inferior frontal gyrus):**
- Speech production
- Syntax processing
- **Broca's aphasia:** Non-fluent, effortful speech; comprehension relatively spared
  - "Want... coffee... no... sugar"

**Wernicke's Area (Left posterior superior temporal):**
- Speech comprehension
- Word retrieval
- **Wernicke's aphasia:** Fluent but meaningless speech; poor comprehension
  - "The slithy toves did gyre and gimble"

**Arcuate Fasciculus:**
- White matter tract connecting Broca's and Wernicke's
- **Conduction aphasia:** Good comprehension and fluent speech, but poor repetition

### Modern Language Networks

The classical model is oversimplified. Modern view emphasizes:

**Dual-Stream Model (Hickok & Poeppel):**

| Stream | Pathway | Function |
|--------|---------|----------|
| **Ventral** | Temporal lobe | Sound-to-meaning mapping |
| **Dorsal** | Parietal/frontal | Sound-to-articulation mapping |

**Extended Language Network:**
- **Angular gyrus** - Semantic integration, reading
- **Supramarginal gyrus** - Phonological processing
- **Middle temporal gyrus** - Semantic processing
- **Inferior frontal gyrus** - Production, selection, syntax
- **Anterior temporal lobe** - Semantic hub

### Language Processing

#### Speech Perception

**Challenges:**
- No clear word boundaries in continuous speech
- High variability in acoustic signal
- Context-dependent (same sound different depending on surrounding sounds)

**Categorical perception:**
- Perceive phonemes as discrete categories
- Sharp boundary between /b/ and /p/

**Motor theory:**
- Understand speech partly through motor simulation
- Mirror neurons may play a role

#### Word Recognition

**Mental lexicon:**
- ~50,000-100,000 words stored
- Organized by meaning, sound, frequency

**Models:**
- **Cohort model** - Activate candidates matching initial sounds, narrow down
- **TRACE** - Interactive activation; features, phonemes, words

**Frequency effect:**
- Common words recognized faster
- Word frequency stored in mental lexicon

#### Sentence Processing

**Parsing:**
- Assigning syntactic structure
- Incremental (word-by-word)

**Garden-path sentences:**
- Initial parse leads to error
- "The horse raced past the barn fell"
- Requires reanalysis

**Working memory demands:**
- Long-distance dependencies tax WM
- Left inferior frontal regions active

### Reading

**Dual-Route Model:**

| Route | Process | Used For |
|-------|---------|----------|
| **Lexical** | Whole-word recognition | Familiar words |
| **Sublexical** | Grapheme-to-phoneme conversion | Novel words, nonwords |

**Visual Word Form Area (VWFA):**
- Left fusiform gyrus
- Specialized for written word recognition
- Develops with reading experience

**Dyslexia:**
- Difficulty with accurate/fluent word recognition
- Often phonological processing deficit
- Reduced activation in left temporoparietal regions
- Treatable with phonics-based intervention

### Language and the Brain: Key Principles

**Left hemisphere dominance:**
- ~95% right-handers, ~70% left-handers
- But right hemisphere contributes (prosody, metaphor, discourse)

**Plasticity:**
- Early damage can shift language to right hemisphere
- Critical period for native-like acquisition

**Bilingualism:**
- Similar neural substrates for both languages
- Prefrontal control for language switching
- Cognitive benefits (executive function)

### Executive Functions

**Definition:** Top-down control processes that regulate cognition and behavior

**Core Components (Miyake et al.):**

| Function | Description | Test |
|----------|-------------|------|
| **Inhibition** | Suppress prepotent responses | Stroop task, Go/No-Go |
| **Working memory** | Maintain and manipulate info | N-back, digit span |
| **Cognitive flexibility** | Switch between tasks/rules | Wisconsin Card Sort, task switching |

**Higher-order functions (depend on core):**
- Planning
- Reasoning
- Problem-solving
- Decision-making

### Prefrontal Cortex (PFC)

**Location:** Anterior to motor cortex; last to develop, first to decline

**Subdivisions:**

| Region | Functions |
|--------|-----------|
| **Dorsolateral PFC** | Working memory, planning, cognitive flexibility |
| **Ventrolateral PFC** | Response inhibition, retrieval |
| **Orbitofrontal** | Reward, emotion, social cognition |
| **Anterior cingulate** | Conflict monitoring, error detection |
| **Frontopolar** | Metacognition, multitasking |

**PFC Damage Effects:**
- Difficulty planning, organizing
- Impulsivity, disinhibition
- Perseveration (can't shift strategies)
- Poor judgment, decision-making
- Personality changes (Phineas Gage)

### Cognitive Control Mechanisms

**Goal maintenance:**
- PFC maintains task goals in working memory
- Biases processing in posterior regions

**Conflict monitoring:**
- Anterior cingulate detects response conflict
- Signals need for increased control
- Error-related negativity (ERN) in ERP

**Response inhibition:**
- Stop-signal task: Inhibit initiated response
- Right inferior frontal gyrus critical

**Task switching:**
- Switch cost: Slower/more errors after switch
- Requires updating task set
- Lateral PFC, parietal cortex involved

### The Stroop Effect

**Task:** Name the ink color of color words

| Condition | Example | Response |
|-----------|---------|----------|
| Congruent | RED (in red) | Easy |
| Incongruent | RED (in blue) | Difficult |

**Explanation:**
- Reading is automatic, color naming requires control
- Conflict between word meaning and ink color
- Anterior cingulate detects conflict
- Dorsolateral PFC implements control

### Decision-Making

**Neural Systems:**

| System | Characteristics | Regions |
|--------|----------------|---------|
| **Model-based** | Deliberative, flexible | Prefrontal cortex |
| **Model-free** | Habitual, efficient | Basal ganglia |

**Reward processing:**
- Orbitofrontal cortex: Value representation
- Ventral striatum: Reward prediction
- Dopamine signals prediction errors

**Iowa Gambling Task:**
- Orbitofrontal damage → poor decisions despite knowing rules
- Somatic marker hypothesis (Damasio): Emotions guide decisions

### Development of Executive Function

**Protracted development:**
- PFC last region to mature (into 20s)
- Myelination continues through adolescence
- Executive functions improve throughout childhood

**Implications:**
- Adolescent risk-taking (reward system mature, control not)
- Educational approaches should scaffold executive function
- Early adversity can impair development

## Real-World Examples

- **Aphasia rehabilitation:** Constraint-induced language therapy forces use of impaired function
- **Bilingual advantages:** Enhanced executive control from constant language switching
- **ADHD:** Executive function deficits; treated with stimulants affecting PFC dopamine
- **Frontal lobe injuries:** TBI often affects frontal lobes → executive dysfunction
- **Aging:** Executive functions decline early; training may help maintain

## Summary

Language processing involves distributed networks including Broca's area (production), Wernicke's area (comprehension), and many other regions. Modern models emphasize dual streams (ventral for meaning, dorsal for articulation) rather than simple localization. Executive functions—inhibition, working memory, cognitive flexibility—depend on prefrontal cortex and allow flexible, goal-directed behavior. These control processes develop slowly, are vulnerable to damage, and underlie much of what makes human cognition unique.
