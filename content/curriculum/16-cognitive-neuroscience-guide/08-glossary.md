# Glossary of Key Terms

Quick reference for essential cognitive neuroscience terminology.

---

## A

**Action potential** - All-or-none electrical signal propagating down a neuron's axon; the basic unit of neural communication.

**Afferent** - Carrying information toward the central nervous system (sensory neurons).

**Agnosia** - Inability to recognize objects, faces, or sounds despite intact sensory function.

**Amygdala** - Almond-shaped limbic structure critical for emotional processing, especially fear.

**Anterograde amnesia** - Inability to form new memories after brain injury.

**Aphasia** - Language impairment caused by brain damage.

**Association cortex** - Cortical areas that integrate information from multiple sources; support higher cognition.

**Attention** - Cognitive process of selectively concentrating on relevant information.

**Axon** - Long projection from neuron cell body that conducts action potentials to other neurons.

## B

**Basal ganglia** - Subcortical nuclei involved in motor control, habit learning, and reward processing.

**Binocular rivalry** - Phenomenon where different images to each eye result in alternating perception; used to study consciousness.

**Blood-brain barrier** - Selective barrier between blood and brain that protects neural tissue.

**Broca's area** - Left inferior frontal region critical for speech production.

**Brodmann areas** - Numbered regions of cerebral cortex based on cytoarchitecture.

## C

**Central executive** - Working memory component that controls attention and coordinates other systems.

**Cerebellum** - "Little brain" at back of head; coordinates movement, timing, and some cognitive functions.

**Cerebral cortex** - Outer layer of gray matter covering cerebral hemispheres; seat of higher cognition.

**Cognitive control** - See *Executive functions*.

**Consolidation** - Process by which memories become stable over time.

**Contralateral** - On the opposite side; brain often represents contralateral body/space.

**Corpus callosum** - Largest white matter tract connecting left and right hemispheres.

## D

**Declarative memory** - Conscious memory for facts (semantic) and events (episodic).

**Dendrite** - Branching projection from neuron that receives input from other neurons.

**Dopamine** - Neurotransmitter involved in reward, motivation, motor control, and cognition.

**Dorsal stream** - "Where/how" visual pathway from occipital to parietal cortex.

**Double dissociation** - Evidence that two functions are independent when each can be impaired without the other.

## E

**Efferent** - Carrying information away from the central nervous system (motor neurons).

**Electroencephalography (EEG)** - Recording electrical activity from the scalp; excellent temporal resolution.

**Encoding** - Process of transforming information into memory representation.

**Episodic memory** - Memory for personal events and experiences, including context.

**Event-related potential (ERP)** - EEG averaged to specific events; reveals timing of cognitive processes.

**Executive functions** - Top-down control processes including inhibition, working memory, and cognitive flexibility.

## F

**fMRI (functional MRI)** - Neuroimaging technique measuring blood oxygen changes; indicates neural activity.

**Frontal lobe** - Anterior brain lobe; motor control, planning, decision-making, personality.

**Fusiform face area (FFA)** - Temporal-occipital region specialized for face processing.

## G

**GABA (gamma-aminobutyric acid)** - Main inhibitory neurotransmitter in the brain.

**Glial cells** - Non-neuronal brain cells providing support, insulation, and immune functions.

**Global workspace theory** - Theory that consciousness involves information being broadcast globally across brain networks.

**Glutamate** - Main excitatory neurotransmitter in the brain.

**Gray matter** - Brain tissue containing neuron cell bodies and dendrites.

## H

**Hebbian learning** - "Neurons that fire together wire together"; basis of synaptic plasticity.

**Hemispatial neglect** - Failure to attend to contralesional space after parietal damage.

**Hippocampus** - Medial temporal lobe structure critical for forming new declarative memories.

**Homunculus** - "Little man"; distorted body map in motor and sensory cortex.

## I

**Implicit memory** - Memory without conscious awareness; includes procedural, priming, conditioning.

**Inattentional blindness** - Failure to notice unexpected objects when attention is engaged elsewhere.

**Inferior** - Lower/toward bottom (in anatomical terms).

**Inhibition** - Executive function; suppressing prepotent or inappropriate responses.

**Ipsilateral** - On the same side.

## L

**Lateral** - Away from the midline.

**Lesion** - Area of brain damage; lesion studies relate damage to functional deficits.

**Limbic system** - Collection of structures (hippocampus, amygdala, etc.) involved in emotion and memory.

**Long-term potentiation (LTP)** - Persistent strengthening of synaptic connections; cellular basis of learning.

## M

**Medial** - Toward the midline.

**Medial temporal lobe (MTL)** - Region including hippocampus, critical for declarative memory.

**MEG (magnetoencephalography)** - Measures magnetic fields from neural activity; good spatial and temporal resolution.

**Mirror neurons** - Neurons that fire both when performing and observing an action.

**Motor cortex** - Precentral gyrus; generates voluntary movements.

**Myelin** - Fatty insulation around axons that speeds signal conduction.

## N

**Neural correlate of consciousness (NCC)** - Brain activity associated with conscious experience.

**Neuroimaging** - Techniques for visualizing brain structure or function (MRI, fMRI, PET, etc.).

**Neuron** - Basic functional unit of nervous system; transmits information via electrical and chemical signals.

**Neurotransmitter** - Chemical messenger released at synapses to communicate between neurons.

**NMDA receptor** - Glutamate receptor critical for synaptic plasticity and memory.

## O

**Occipital lobe** - Posterior brain lobe; visual processing.

**Orbitofrontal cortex** - Ventral prefrontal region; reward processing, decision-making.

## P

**Parietal lobe** - Superior brain lobe; sensory integration, spatial processing, attention.

**PET (positron emission tomography)** - Imaging technique using radioactive tracers to measure brain activity.

**Phonological loop** - Working memory component for verbal/acoustic information.

**Plasticity** - Brain's ability to change structure and function with experience.

**Prefrontal cortex (PFC)** - Anterior frontal regions supporting executive functions.

**Priming** - Facilitated processing due to prior exposure; implicit memory.

**Procedural memory** - Memory for skills and habits; implicit, depends on basal ganglia.

**Proprioception** - Sense of body position and movement.

## R

**Receptive field** - Region of sensory space to which a neuron responds.

**Receptor** - Protein that binds neurotransmitter or other signaling molecule.

**REM sleep** - Sleep stage with rapid eye movements, vivid dreams, and muscle paralysis.

**Resting potential** - Electrical charge across neuron membrane at rest (~-70mV).

**Retrieval** - Process of accessing stored memories.

**Retrograde amnesia** - Loss of memories from before brain injury.

**Retinotopy** - Orderly mapping of visual field onto visual cortex.

## S

**Semantic memory** - Memory for facts and general knowledge; context-independent.

**Somatosensory cortex** - Postcentral gyrus; processes touch, pressure, pain, temperature.

**Somatotopy** - Orderly mapping of body surface onto cortex.

**Superior** - Upper/toward top (in anatomical terms).

**Synapse** - Junction between neurons where chemical communication occurs.

**Synaptic plasticity** - Changes in synaptic strength that underlie learning and memory.

## T

**Temporal lobe** - Lateral brain lobe; auditory processing, memory, language.

**Thalamus** - Central relay station; processes and transmits sensory information to cortex.

**TMS (transcranial magnetic stimulation)** - Non-invasive brain stimulation using magnetic pulses.

**Top-down processing** - Cognitive processing guided by knowledge, expectations, goals.

**Tonotopy** - Orderly mapping of sound frequency onto auditory cortex.

## V

**Ventral stream** - "What" visual pathway from occipital to temporal cortex.

**Visuospatial sketchpad** - Working memory component for visual and spatial information.

## W

**Wernicke's area** - Left posterior temporal region critical for language comprehension.

**White matter** - Brain tissue containing myelinated axons connecting regions.

**Working memory** - System for temporarily holding and manipulating information.

## Quick Reference Tables

### Major Brain Lobes

| Lobe | Location | Key Functions |
|------|----------|---------------|
| Frontal | Front | Executive function, motor, speech |
| Parietal | Top | Sensory, spatial, attention |
| Temporal | Side | Hearing, memory, language |
| Occipital | Back | Vision |

### Key Neurotransmitters

| NT | Type | Functions | Related Disorders |
|----|------|-----------|-------------------|
| Glutamate | Excitatory | Learning, memory | Epilepsy, neurodegeneration |
| GABA | Inhibitory | Calming | Anxiety, epilepsy |
| Dopamine | Modulatory | Reward, motor | Parkinson's, addiction |
| Serotonin | Modulatory | Mood, sleep | Depression, anxiety |
| Acetylcholine | Varies | Attention, memory | Alzheimer's |

### Memory Systems

| System | Conscious? | Brain Regions | Example |
|--------|------------|---------------|---------|
| Episodic | Yes | Hippocampus, PFC | Your last birthday |
| Semantic | Yes | Temporal cortex | Paris is in France |
| Procedural | No | Basal ganglia, cerebellum | Riding a bike |
| Priming | No | Sensory cortex | Easier word completion |

### Research Methods Comparison

| Method | Measures | Spatial Res. | Temporal Res. |
|--------|----------|--------------|---------------|
| fMRI | Blood flow | ~1-3mm | ~1-2 sec |
| EEG | Electrical | ~1cm | ~1ms |
| MEG | Magnetic | ~5mm | ~1ms |
| PET | Metabolism | ~5mm | ~30 sec |
| Single-unit | Neuron firing | Single neuron | ~1ms |
