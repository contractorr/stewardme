## 1. Energy Fundamentals

### Core Concept: Powering Society
Energy industry = generation, transmission, distribution, and retail of electricity, oil, gas, and renewable sources. Essential infrastructure with heavy regulation, capital intensity, and long asset lives.

**The value chain:** Generation → Transmission → Distribution → Retail/Consumption

### Major Sectors

| Sector | Description | Key characteristics |
|--------|-------------|---------------------|
| **Electric utilities** | Power generation and delivery | Regulated monopolies, capital intensive |
| **Oil & Gas (upstream)** | Exploration and production | Commodity exposure, capital cycles |
| **Oil & Gas (midstream)** | Pipelines, storage, processing | Fee-based, infrastructure |
| **Oil & Gas (downstream)** | Refining, marketing | Margin-based, commodity spreads |
| **Renewables** | Wind, solar, hydro, storage | Growing fast, PPA-driven |
| **Natural gas utilities** | Gas distribution | Regulated, heating demand |
| **Water utilities** | Water and wastewater | Municipal focus, regulated |

## 3. Business Model Deep Dive

### Regulated Utility Economics

**Cost-of-service regulation:**
```
Revenue Requirement = Operating Costs + Depreciation + Taxes + (Rate Base × ROE)
```

**Rate base:** Capital invested in utility assets
**ROE:** Allowed return on equity (typically 9-11%)

**Key metrics:**

| Metric | Definition | Typical |
|--------|------------|---------|
| **Rate base growth** | Capital investment pace | 5-8% annual |
| **ROE (earned)** | Actual return on equity | 9-11% |
| **FFO/Debt** | Cash flow to leverage | 14-17% |
| **Regulatory lag** | Time to recover costs | 6-18 months |

### Deregulated/Merchant Power

**Revenue:** Wholesale power price × generation volume

**Power price drivers:**
- Fuel costs (gas, coal)
- Demand (weather, economy)
- Capacity prices
- Renewable credits (RECs)

**Key metrics:**
- Spark spread (power price - gas cost)
- Dark spread (power price - coal cost)
- Capacity factor
- Heat rate (efficiency)

### Oil & Gas Economics

**Upstream:**
```
Revenue = Production volume × Commodity price
- Lifting costs
- DD&A (depletion, depreciation, amortization)
- Finding costs
= Operating profit
```

**Key metrics:**
- Reserves (proved, probable)
- Reserve replacement ratio
- Finding and development costs (F&D)
- Breakeven price per barrel

**Midstream:**
- Fee-based: Throughput × tariff
- Less commodity exposure
- Long-term contracts (take-or-pay)

**Downstream:**
- Crack spread: Product price - crude cost
- Refining margin, utilization rate

### Renewables Economics

**PPA (Power Purchase Agreement):**
- Long-term contract (10-25 years)
- Fixed price per MWh
- Offtaker: utility, corporate, or merchant

**Project finance:**
```
Revenue (PPA price × output)
- Operating costs
- Debt service
= Equity returns (IRR target: 8-12%)
```

**LCOE** (Levelized Cost of Energy): All-in cost per MWh

## 6. Emerging Models & Trends

### Decarbonization

**Grid transition:**
- Coal → gas → renewables
- 2035 clean grid targets
- Transmission buildout needed

**Electrification:**
- EVs (transportation)
- Heat pumps (buildings)
- Industrial processes

**Emerging tech:**
- Green hydrogen
- Long-duration storage
- Small modular reactors (SMRs)
- Carbon capture (CCUS)

### Distributed Energy Resources (DERs)

**Types:**
- Rooftop solar
- Behind-the-meter storage
- EVs (vehicle-to-grid)
- Smart thermostats, water heaters

**Grid impact:**
- Duck curve (solar oversupply midday)
- Need for flexibility
- Distribution grid stress
- Rate design challenges

### Grid Modernization

**Smart grid:**
- AMI (smart meters)

- Grid edge intelligence

**Challenges:**

- Cybersecurity threats
- Interconnection queues
- Workforce transition

### AI/ML Applications

- Predictive maintenance
- Outage prediction

**Customer:**
- Usage analytics
- Demand response optimization
- Rate optimization

**Trading:**

## 7. Market Dynamics

### Market Size

- US electric utility industry: ~$500B revenue
- Global energy: ~$7T
- US oil & gas: ~$400B revenue
- Renewables investment: $500B+ global annually
- US solar installations: $50B+

### Industry Structure

**Electric utilities:**
- ~3,000 utilities in US
- IOUs serve ~70% of customers
- Top 10 IOUs: ~35% of electricity sales
- Regulated monopoly structure in most areas

**Oil & gas:**
- Highly consolidated (majors)
- Fragmented services sector
- Cyclical investment patterns

**Renewables:**
- Consolidating (NextEra dominant)
- Private equity active
- Corporate PPA market growing

### Regulatory Environment

**Utility regulation:**
- State PUCs (Public Utility Commissions)
- FERC (Federal - wholesale, transmission)
- Rate cases for cost recovery
- Integrated Resource Planning (IRP)

**Environmental:**
- Clean Air Act
- EPA regulations
- State RPS (Renewable Portfolio Standards)
- Carbon pricing (regional - RGGI, CA cap-and-trade)

**Key policy:**
- IRA (Inflation Reduction Act) - massive clean energy incentives
- ITC/PTC (Investment/Production Tax Credits)
- PURPA (small generator access)

### Key Challenges

**Utilities:**
- Decarbonization costs
- Wildfire liability (CA)
- Rate affordability
- Grid reliability with renewables

**Oil & gas:**
- Energy transition uncertainty
- Permitting challenges
- ESG investor pressure
- Commodity volatility

**Renewables:**
- Interconnection delays
- Supply chain constraints
- Permitting and siting
- Grid integration

## Quick Reference: Electricity Flow

```
┌─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┐
│                    GENERATION                                │
│  Nuclear │ Gas │ Coal │ Hydro │ Wind │ Solar │ Storage      │
└─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┘
                              ↓ High voltage (115-765kV)
┌─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┐
│                   TRANSMISSION                               │
│  Bulk power │ RTO/ISO markets │ Interconnections            │
└─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┘
                              ↓ Substations (step down)
┌─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┐
│                   DISTRIBUTION                               │
│  Local delivery │ Feeders │ Transformers │ DERs             │
└─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┘
                              ↓ Service voltage
┌─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┐
│                    CUSTOMERS                                 │
│  Residential │ Commercial │ Industrial │ EVs                │
└─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────┘
```

**Market structure:**
```
Regulated (vertically integrated): Generation → T&D → Retail (all one utility)
Deregulated: Generation (IPPs) → T&D (utility) → Retail (competitive)
```
