# Physics Glossary

## Quick Reference: Key Terms A-Z

---

## A

**Absolute Zero**
- Lowest possible temperature: 0 K = -273.15°C
- Particles have minimum quantum mechanical energy
- Unattainable according to third law of thermodynamics

**Acceleration**
- Rate of change of velocity
- Vector quantity: a = Δv/Δt
- Unit: m/s²

**Adiabatic Process**
- Thermodynamic process with no heat transfer (Q = 0)
- Temperature changes due to compression/expansion
- ΔU = -W

**Ampere (A)**
- SI unit of electric current
- 1 A = 1 coulomb per second

**Angular Momentum**
- Rotational analog of linear momentum
- L = Iω (for rotating rigid body)
- L = r × p (for point particle)
- Conserved in absence of external torques

**Antimatter**
- Particles with opposite charge from normal matter
- Positron (e⁺), antiproton, etc.
- Matter-antimatter annihilation produces energy

**Atom**
- Basic unit of chemical element
- Nucleus (protons + neutrons) + electron cloud
- Size: ~10⁻¹⁰ m

---

## B

**Blackbody**
- Idealized object that absorbs/emits all radiation
- Spectrum depends only on temperature
- Led to Planck's quantum hypothesis

**Bohr Model**
- Early quantum model of atom (1913)
- Electrons in quantized circular orbits
- Successfully predicted hydrogen spectrum

**Boltzmann Constant (kB)**
- Links temperature to energy
- kB = 1.381 × 10⁻²³ J/K
- Average kinetic energy: ½kBT per degree of freedom

**Boson**
- Particle with integer spin (0, 1, 2, ...)
- Obey Bose-Einstein statistics
- Multiple bosons can occupy same quantum state
- Examples: Photon, gluon, W/Z bosons, Higgs boson

---

## C

**Capacitance**
- Ability to store electric charge
- C = Q/V
- Unit: Farad (F)

**Carnot Cycle**
- Idealized thermodynamic cycle
- Maximum theoretical efficiency between two temperatures
- η = 1 - TC/TH

**Centripetal Acceleration**
- Acceleration toward center of circular path
- a = v²/r
- Required to maintain circular motion

**Charge (Electric)**
- Fundamental property of matter
- Two types: positive and negative
- Quantized in units of elementary charge e = 1.602 × 10⁻¹⁹ C
- Conserved

**Classical Mechanics**
- Physics of macroscopic objects at non-relativistic speeds
- Based on Newton's laws
- Valid when v << c and scales >> atomic

**Conductor**
- Material allowing free charge flow
- Metals, plasma, saltwater
- Free electrons or ions

**Conservation Laws**
- Fundamental principles: quantity remains constant
- Energy, momentum, angular momentum, charge, mass-energy

**Coulomb (C)**
- SI unit of electric charge
- Charge of ~6.24 × 10¹⁸ electrons

**Coulomb's Law**
- Electric force between point charges
- F = k(q₁q₂)/r²
- k = 8.99 × 10⁹ N⋅m²/C²

---

## D

**de Broglie Wavelength**
- Matter wave wavelength
- λ = h/p
- All particles have associated wave

**Diffraction**
- Bending of waves around obstacles or through apertures
- Evidence of wave nature
- Limits resolution of optical instruments

**Doppler Effect**
- Frequency shift due to relative motion
- Source approaching: higher frequency (blueshift)
- Source receding: lower frequency (redshift)

---

## E

**Electric Field (E)**
- Force per unit charge
- E = F/q
- Unit: N/C or V/m
- Created by charges

**Electric Potential (Voltage)**
- Potential energy per unit charge
- V = U/q
- Unit: Volt (V) = J/C

**Electromagnetic Radiation**
- Self-propagating oscillating electric and magnetic fields
- Includes radio, microwave, infrared, visible, UV, X-rays, gamma rays
- All travel at speed of light in vacuum

**Electromagnetic Spectrum**
- Range of all electromagnetic radiation
- Characterized by wavelength or frequency
- c = λf

**Electron**
- Negatively charged elementary particle
- Charge: -e = -1.602 × 10⁻¹⁹ C
- Mass: 9.109 × 10⁻³¹ kg
- Fermion with spin ½

**Electron Volt (eV)**
- Energy unit convenient for atomic physics
- 1 eV = 1.602 × 10⁻¹⁹ J
- Energy gained by electron through 1 V potential

**Energy**
- Capacity to do work
- Many forms: kinetic, potential, thermal, chemical, nuclear
- Conserved (first law of thermodynamics)
- Unit: Joule (J)

**Entanglement**
- Quantum correlation between particles
- Measuring one instantly affects the other
- Non-local phenomenon
- No faster-than-light communication possible

**Entropy (S)**
- Measure of disorder or number of microstates
- S = kB ln Ω (Boltzmann)
- Increases in isolated systems (second law)
- Unit: J/K

**Equipotential Surface**
- Surface of constant electric potential
- No work required to move charge along surface
- Field lines perpendicular to equipotentials

---

## F

**Faraday's Law**
- Changing magnetic flux induces EMF
- ε = -dΦB/dt
- Basis for generators and transformers

**Fermion**
- Particle with half-integer spin (½, 3/2, ...)
- Obey Fermi-Dirac statistics and Pauli exclusion principle
- Cannot occupy same quantum state
- Examples: Electron, proton, neutron, quarks

**Force**
- Push or pull on object
- Vector quantity
- F = ma (Newton's second law)
- Unit: Newton (N) = kg⋅m/s²

**Frequency**
- Number of oscillations per unit time
- f = 1/T
- Unit: Hertz (Hz) = 1/s

**Friction**
- Force opposing relative motion
- Static friction: fs ≤ μsN (prevents motion)
- Kinetic friction: fk = μkN (during motion)

---

## G

**Gamma Ray**
- Highest energy electromagnetic radiation
- Wavelength < 0.01 nm
- Produced by nuclear reactions
- Highly penetrating

**Gravitational Constant (G)**
- Fundamental constant in Newton's law of gravitation
- G = 6.674 × 10⁻¹¹ N⋅m²/kg²

**Ground State**
- Lowest energy state of quantum system
- Has zero-point energy (not zero)
- Most stable configuration

---

## H

**Heat**
- Energy transferred due to temperature difference
- Flows spontaneously from hot to cold
- Unit: Joule (J) or calorie (cal)

**Heat Capacity**
- Energy required to raise temperature by 1 K
- C = Q/ΔT
- Extensive property (depends on amount)

**Hertz (Hz)**
- SI unit of frequency
- 1 Hz = 1 cycle per second

**Hooke's Law**
- Spring force proportional to displacement
- F = -kx
- k = spring constant

---

## I

**Impulse**
- Change in momentum
- J = Δp = FΔt
- Unit: N⋅s = kg⋅m/s

**Inductance**
- Property opposing current change
- ε = -L(dI/dt)
- Unit: Henry (H)

**Inertia**
- Resistance to changes in motion
- Quantified by mass
- Newton's first law

**Inertial Reference Frame**
- Non-accelerating reference frame
- Newton's laws hold
- Special relativity applies

**Intensity**
- Power per unit area
- I = P/A
- For waves: I ∝ A² (amplitude squared)
- Unit: W/m²

**Interference**
- Superposition of waves
- Constructive: Amplitudes add (in phase)
- Destructive: Amplitudes subtract (out of phase)

**Ion**
- Atom or molecule with net electric charge
- Cation: Positive (lost electrons)
- Anion: Negative (gained electrons)

**Isothermal Process**
- Thermodynamic process at constant temperature
- ΔU = 0 for ideal gas
- Q = W

**Isotope**
- Atoms of same element with different neutron numbers
- Same proton number (same element)
- Different mass numbers

---

## J

**Joule (J)**
- SI unit of energy and work
- 1 J = 1 N⋅m = 1 kg⋅m²/s²
- 1 J = 1 W⋅s

---

## K

**Kelvin (K)**
- SI unit of temperature
- Absolute scale (0 K = absolute zero)
- K = °C + 273.15

**Kinetic Energy**
- Energy of motion
- KE = ½mv² (non-relativistic)
- KE = (γ - 1)mc² (relativistic)

**Kirchhoff's Laws**
- Circuit analysis principles
- Current Law (KCL): ΣIin = ΣIout (conservation of charge)
- Voltage Law (KVL): ΣV = 0 around loop (conservation of energy)

---

## L

**Laser**
- Light Amplification by Stimulated Emission of Radiation
- Coherent, monochromatic, collimated light
- Applications: Surgery, communication, measurement

**Length Contraction**
- Relativistic effect: Moving objects appear shorter
- L = L₀/γ = L₀√(1 - v²/c²)
- Only along direction of motion

**Lenz's Law**
- Induced current opposes change in flux
- Consequence of energy conservation
- Determines direction of induced EMF

**Light**
- Visible electromagnetic radiation (400-700 nm)
- Dual nature: Wave and particle (photon)
- Speed in vacuum: c = 299,792,458 m/s (exact)

**Longitudinal Wave**
- Oscillation parallel to propagation
- Examples: Sound, pressure waves

**Lorentz Factor (γ)**
- γ = 1/√(1 - v²/c²)
- Quantifies relativistic effects
- γ = 1 at rest, γ → ∞ as v → c

---

## M

**Magnetic Field (B)**
- Field exerting force on moving charges
- Unit: Tesla (T) = N/(A⋅m)
- Created by moving charges (currents) or magnets

**Magnetic Flux**
- Measure of magnetic field through surface
- ΦB = ∫B⋅dA
- Unit: Weber (Wb) = T⋅m²

**Mass**
- Measure of matter; resistance to acceleration
- Inertial mass: F = ma
- Gravitational mass: F = GMm/r²
- Equivalent by Einstein's equivalence principle

**Mass-Energy Equivalence**
- E = mc²
- Mass and energy are interconvertible
- Basis of nuclear energy

**Maxwell's Equations**
- Four fundamental equations unifying electromagnetism
- Predict electromagnetic waves traveling at c
- Foundation of classical electromagnetism

**Momentum**
- Product of mass and velocity
- p = mv (classical)
- p = γmv (relativistic)
- Conserved in isolated systems

---

## N

**Neutron**
- Electrically neutral nuclear particle
- Mass: 1.675 × 10⁻²⁷ kg (slightly heavier than proton)
- Composed of quarks (udd)
- Free neutron decays (~10 min half-life)

**Newton (N)**
- SI unit of force
- 1 N = 1 kg⋅m/s²

**Newton's Laws**
- **First**: Object at rest/constant motion unless acted on by net force (inertia)
- **Second**: F = ma (quantifies force-acceleration relationship)
- **Third**: Action-reaction pairs (F₁₂ = -F₂₁)

**Nuclear Fission**
- Heavy nucleus splits into lighter nuclei
- Releases energy (E = mc²)
- Chain reaction possible
- Power plants, nuclear weapons

**Nuclear Fusion**
- Light nuclei combine into heavier nucleus
- Releases energy (powers stars)
- Requires extreme temperature/pressure
- Future energy source

**Nucleus**
- Central core of atom
- Contains protons and neutrons
- Size: ~10⁻¹⁵ m (femtometer)
- Contains >99.9% of atom's mass

---

## O

**Ohm (Ω)**
- SI unit of electrical resistance
- 1 Ω = 1 V/A

**Ohm's Law**
- V = IR
- Current proportional to voltage (for constant R)
- Applies to ohmic materials

**Optics**
- Study of light and its interactions
- Geometric optics: Ray model (lenses, mirrors)
- Physical optics: Wave nature (interference, diffraction)

---

## P

**Pauli Exclusion Principle**
- No two fermions can occupy same quantum state
- Explains atomic structure and periodic table
- Applies to electrons in atoms

**Period**
- Time for one complete oscillation
- T = 1/f
- Unit: Second (s)

**Photoelectric Effect**
- Light ejects electrons from metal
- Evidence for photons
- KEmax = hf - φ
- Einstein's explanation won Nobel Prize

**Photon**
- Quantum of light (particle of EM radiation)
- Energy: E = hf
- Momentum: p = h/λ
- Zero rest mass, travels at c

**Planck's Constant (h)**
- Fundamental quantum constant
- h = 6.626 × 10⁻³⁴ J⋅s
- Relates energy to frequency: E = hf
- ℏ = h/(2π) = 1.055 × 10⁻³⁴ J⋅s

**Polarization**
- Restriction of transverse wave oscillation to specific plane
- Unpolarized light: All directions
- Polarized light: One direction
- Methods: Filters, reflection, scattering

**Positron**
- Antiparticle of electron
- Charge: +e
- Same mass as electron
- Discovered 1932 (Anderson)

**Potential Energy**
- Stored energy due to position or configuration
- Gravitational: PEg = mgh
- Elastic: PEs = ½kx²
- Electric: U = kq₁q₂/r

**Power**
- Rate of energy transfer or work
- P = E/t = W/t
- Unit: Watt (W) = J/s

**Proton**
- Positively charged nuclear particle
- Charge: +e = 1.602 × 10⁻¹⁹ C
- Mass: 1.673 × 10⁻²⁷ kg
- Composed of quarks (uud)

---

## Q

**Quantum**
- Discrete, indivisible unit
- Energy quantized: E = nhf
- Angular momentum quantized: L = nℏ

**Quantum Mechanics**
- Physics of atomic and subatomic scales
- Probabilistic, wave-particle duality
- Described by wave function (ψ)

**Quantum Number**
- Integer or half-integer specifying quantum state
- Principal (n), angular momentum (l), magnetic (ml), spin (ms)

**Quark**
- Elementary particle, constituent of protons/neutrons
- Six types: up, down, charm, strange, top, bottom
- Fractional electric charge
- Never observed isolated

---

## R

**Radiation**
- Energy emission/transmission
- Electromagnetic: EM waves
- Nuclear: Alpha, beta, gamma particles
- Thermal: Heat transfer via EM waves

**Radioactivity**
- Spontaneous nuclear decay
- Alpha (helium nucleus), beta (electron/positron), gamma (photon)
- Exponential decay: N(t) = N₀e^(-λt)

**Reflection**
- Wave bouncing off surface
- Angle of incidence = angle of reflection
- Phase change possible at boundary

**Refraction**
- Bending of wave at boundary due to speed change
- Snell's law: n₁ sin θ₁ = n₂ sin θ₂
- Refractive index: n = c/v

**Refractive Index**
- Ratio of light speed in vacuum to speed in medium
- n = c/v (n ≥ 1)
- Determines how much light bends

**Relativity**
- Einstein's theories revolutionizing space and time
- Special (1905): High speeds, flat spacetime
- General (1915): Gravity as spacetime curvature

**Resistance**
- Opposition to current flow
- R = V/I (Ohm's law)
- R = ρL/A (resistivity formula)
- Unit: Ohm (Ω)

**Rest Energy**
- Energy of particle at rest
- E₀ = mc²
- Minimum energy of particle

---

## S

**Schrödinger Equation**
- Fundamental equation of quantum mechanics
- Describes evolution of wave function
- Time-independent: Hψ = Eψ

**Semiconductor**
- Material with intermediate conductivity
- Silicon, germanium
- Conductivity controlled by doping
- Basis of electronics (transistors, diodes)

**Simultaneity**
- Being at same time
- Relative in special relativity
- Events simultaneous in one frame may not be in another

**Snell's Law**
- Refraction equation
- n₁ sin θ₁ = n₂ sin θ₂
- Relates angles and refractive indices

**Sound**
- Longitudinal pressure wave in medium
- Speed in air (~343 m/s), water (~1480 m/s)
- Frequency determines pitch
- Human hearing: 20 Hz - 20 kHz

**Spacetime**
- Unified 4D continuum of space and time
- (ct, x, y, z) coordinates
- Warped by mass-energy (general relativity)

**Specific Heat**
- Heat capacity per unit mass
- c = Q/(mΔT)
- Unit: J/(kg⋅K)

**Spectrum**
- Distribution of intensity vs. wavelength or frequency
- Continuous: All wavelengths (white light, blackbody)
- Line: Discrete wavelengths (atomic emission/absorption)

**Speed of Light (c)**
- 299,792,458 m/s (exact by definition)
- Maximum speed in universe
- Same for all observers (special relativity postulate)

**Spin**
- Intrinsic angular momentum of particles
- Quantum property (not classical rotation)
- Fermions: Half-integer (½, 3/2, ...)
- Bosons: Integer (0, 1, 2, ...)

**Standing Wave**
- Interference pattern of oppositely traveling waves
- Nodes (zero amplitude) and antinodes (maximum amplitude)
- Appears stationary
- Musical instruments, resonance

**Superposition**
- Waves: Sum of individual displacements (ytotal = y₁ + y₂)
- Quantum: Simultaneous existence in multiple states

---

## T

**Temperature**
- Measure of average kinetic energy of particles
- Scales: Kelvin, Celsius, Fahrenheit
- Absolute zero: 0 K = -273.15°C

**Tesla (T)**
- SI unit of magnetic field
- 1 T = 1 N/(A⋅m) = 1 Wb/m²
- 1 T = 10,000 Gauss

**Thermodynamics**
- Study of heat, energy, work
- Four laws: Zeroth (temperature), First (conservation), Second (entropy), Third (absolute zero)

**Time Dilation**
- Relativistic effect: Moving clocks run slow
- Δt = γΔt₀
- Confirmed by experiments (muons, atomic clocks)

**Torque**
- Rotational force
- τ = r × F = rF sin θ
- Causes angular acceleration

**Total Internal Reflection**
- Complete reflection at boundary (no refraction)
- Occurs when angle > critical angle
- Fiber optics, prisms

**Transverse Wave**
- Oscillation perpendicular to propagation
- Examples: Light, string waves, water surface

**Tunneling**
- Quantum penetration through classically forbidden barrier
- Probability: T ∝ e^(-2κL)
- Applications: Nuclear fusion, STM, electronics

---

## U

**Uncertainty Principle**
- Heisenberg's principle: ΔxΔp ≥ ℏ/2
- Fundamental limit on simultaneous knowledge
- Not measurement limitation, but nature's property
- Also: ΔEΔt ≥ ℏ/2

**Uniform Circular Motion**
- Constant speed in circular path
- Centripetal acceleration: a = v²/r
- Centripetal force: F = mv²/r

---

## V

**Velocity**
- Rate of change of position (vector)
- v = Δx/Δt
- Unit: m/s

**Virtual Particle**
- Particle existing briefly due to energy-time uncertainty
- Can violate energy conservation for Δt ≈ ℏ/(2ΔE)
- Measurable effects (Casimir, Lamb shift)

**Volt (V)**
- SI unit of electric potential
- 1 V = 1 J/C = 1 W/A

**Voltage**
- See Electric Potential
- Potential difference between two points

---

## W

**Watt (W)**
- SI unit of power
- 1 W = 1 J/s

**Wave**
- Disturbance propagating through space/time
- Transfers energy without transferring matter
- Characterized by λ, f, A, v

**Wave Function (ψ)**
- Mathematical description of quantum state
- Complex-valued
- |ψ|² = probability density

**Wave-Particle Duality**
- Fundamental quantum concept
- Particles exhibit wave properties (interference, diffraction)
- Waves exhibit particle properties (photons, quantization)

**Wavelength (λ)**
- Distance between consecutive wave peaks
- Unit: Meter (m)
- Relation: v = λf

**Weight**
- Gravitational force on object
- W = mg
- Differs from mass (gravitational vs. inertial)

**Work**
- Energy transferred by force over distance
- W = Fd cos θ
- W = ∫F·dl (general)
- Unit: Joule (J)

**Work Function (φ)**
- Minimum energy to remove electron from material
- Determines threshold frequency in photoelectric effect
- Varies by material

---

## X

**X-ray**
- High-energy electromagnetic radiation
- Wavelength: 0.01 - 10 nm
- Penetrates matter (medical imaging, crystallography)
- Produced by electron transitions or Bremsstrahlung

---

## Z

**Zero-Point Energy**
- Minimum energy of quantum system
- Nonzero (E ≠ 0 in ground state)
- Consequence of uncertainty principle
- Example: Helium remains liquid at 0 K

**Zeroth Law of Thermodynamics**
- If A and C in equilibrium, and B and C in equilibrium, then A and B in equilibrium
- Establishes temperature as consistent property
- Allows thermometers to work

## Formula Quick Reference

### Classical Mechanics
- **Newton's second law**: F = ma
- **Kinematic equations**: v = v₀ + at; x = x₀ + v₀t + ½at²; v² = v₀² + 2a(x - x₀)
- **Momentum**: p = mv
- **Kinetic energy**: KE = ½mv²
- **Gravitational PE**: PE = mgh
- **Work**: W = Fd cos θ
- **Power**: P = W/t = Fv
- **Circular motion**: ac = v²/r; F = mv²/r

### Thermodynamics
- **First law**: ΔU = Q - W
- **Ideal gas**: PV = nRT
- **Heat transfer**: Q = mcΔT
- **Efficiency**: η = W/QH = 1 - QC/QH
- **Carnot efficiency**: η = 1 - TC/TH
- **Entropy change**: ΔS = Q/T (reversible)

### Electromagnetism
- **Coulomb's law**: F = kq₁q₂/r²
- **Electric field**: E = F/q = kQ/r²
- **Electric potential**: V = kQ/r
- **Capacitance**: C = Q/V
- **Ohm's law**: V = IR
- **Power**: P = IV = I²R = V²/R
- **Magnetic force**: F = qvB sin θ; F = BIL
- **Faraday's law**: ε = -dΦB/dt

### Waves and Optics
- **Wave equation**: v = λf
- **Snell's law**: n₁ sin θ₁ = n₂ sin θ₂
- **Lens/mirror equation**: 1/f = 1/do + 1/di
- **Magnification**: m = -di/do = hi/ho
- **Double slit**: d sin θ = mλ
- **Single slit**: a sin θ = mλ

### Special Relativity
- **Time dilation**: Δt = γΔt₀
- **Length contraction**: L = L₀/γ
- **Lorentz factor**: γ = 1/√(1 - v²/c²)
- **Energy**: E = γmc²
- **Rest energy**: E₀ = mc²
- **Energy-momentum**: E² = (pc)² + (mc²)²

### Quantum Mechanics
- **Planck**: E = hf
- **Photoelectric**: KEmax = hf - φ
- **de Broglie**: λ = h/p
- **Uncertainty**: ΔxΔp ≥ ℏ/2; ΔEΔt ≥ ℏ/2
- **Hydrogen energy**: En = -13.6 eV/n²
