# Glossary

## Archipelago

A chain or group of islands. Indonesia is the clearest example in this guide.

## Capital

The main political city of a country. It is often, but not always, the largest or richest city.

## Constitutional monarchy

A system where a monarch remains head of state, but elected institutions govern day-to-day politics.

## Federation

A state divided into regions or provinces that keep some powers while sharing a national government.

## Landlocked

A country with no direct access to the open sea.

## Peninsula

A piece of land mostly surrounded by water but connected to a larger landmass.

## Parliamentary system

A political system where the government depends on the support of a legislature or parliament.

## Presidential system

A political system where the president is a central executive authority elected separately from the legislature.

## Republic

A state without a hereditary monarch as head of state.

## Region

A major internal area of a country or a larger part of the world with shared characteristics.

## Strait

A narrow body of water connecting larger seas or oceans. These can become major trade chokepoints.

## Summary

Use these terms as a quick orientation layer when you revisit the guide. Geography becomes easier when the recurring political and spatial vocabulary feels familiar.
