# Glossary

## A

**Abyssal Zone:** Ocean depth 4,000-6,000m; cold (2°C), dark, high pressure; sparse life; covers abyssal plains.

**Acidification (Ocean):** Decrease in ocean pH from dissolved CO₂; pH 8.2→8.1 since 1750 (30% more acidic); threatens shell-forming organisms.

**Albedo:** Reflectivity of surface; ice/snow ~0.5-0.7 (high), ocean ~0.06 (low); affects climate via radiation balance.

**AMOC (Atlantic Meridional Overturning Circulation):** Atlantic branch of thermohaline circulation; transports ~1.3 petawatts heat northward; weakening 15-20% since 1950.

**Aphotic Zone:** Lightless ocean (>200m depth); no photosynthesis; 98% of ocean volume.

**Aquaculture:** Fish/shellfish/seaweed farming; 130M tonnes/year; surpassed wild capture 2013; challenges: pollution, disease, escapes, feed.

**Argo Floats:** 4,000+ autonomous profiling floats measuring ocean temp, salinity, currents; critical for climate monitoring.

**AUV (Autonomous Underwater Vehicle):** Untethered underwater robot; pre-programmed missions; used for mapping, surveys; no real-time control.

## B

**Ballast Water:** Water carried in ship for stability; transports organisms port-to-port; major vector for invasive species.

**Baltic Dry Index (BDI):** Shipping rate index for dry bulk (iron ore, coal, grain); leading indicator of global economic activity.

**Bathyal Zone:** Ocean depth 200-4,000m; continental slope; cold-water corals, deep-sea fish; twilight to dark.

**Bathymetry:** Underwater topography; depth mapping; critical for navigation, resource management, habitat mapping.

**BBNJ Treaty:** Agreement on Biodiversity Beyond National Jurisdiction; adopted 2023; enables high-seas MPAs, environmental impact assessments.

**Benthic:** Relating to seafloor; benthic organisms live on/in sediment (vs pelagic organisms in water column).

**Bioluminescence:** Biological light production; 90% of deep-sea organisms; used for communication, camouflage, hunting.

**Blue Carbon:** Carbon stored in coastal/marine ecosystems (mangroves, seagrass, salt marshes); store 3-5× more carbon per area than terrestrial forests.

**Blue Economy:** Sustainable ocean-based economic activity; includes fisheries, shipping, tourism, offshore wind; projected $3T/year by 2030.

**Bycatch:** Non-target species caught incidentally in fisheries; 10-20% of catch discarded; includes dolphins, turtles, seabirds.

## C

**Capesize:** Bulk carrier >100,000 DWT; too large for Panama/Suez canals; must round Cape of Good Hope/Horn.

**Carbonate System:** Ocean's chemical buffering; absorbs ~25% of anthropogenic CO₂; decreasing pH (acidification).

**Catch Share:** Percentage-based allocation of fishing quota; can be traded; aligns incentives for long-term stewardship.

**CCAMLR:** Commission for Conservation of Antarctic Marine Living Resources; manages Southern Ocean; ecosystem-based; strong enforcement.

**Chemosynthesis:** Energy production from chemicals (not sunlight); hydrothermal vent bacteria convert H₂S→energy; supports unique ecosystems.

**Chokepoint (Maritime):** Narrow strait concentrating shipping; Malacca (25% of trade), Suez (12%), Hormuz (21% of oil); blockage disrupts global economy.

**Clarion-Clipperton Zone (CCZ):** Pacific abyssal plain; 21M km²; richest polymetallic nodule field; 21 billion tonnes nodules.

**Continental Shelf:** Shallow seafloor (0-200m) extending from coast; contains 90%+ of fisheries; part of EEZ.

**Coral Bleaching:** Expulsion of zooxanthellae from stressed coral; turns white; caused by 1-2°C warming; prolonged = death.

**Coriolis Effect:** Deflection of moving objects (air, water) due to Earth's rotation; rightward in Northern Hemisphere, leftward in Southern.

**CTD:** Conductivity-Temperature-Depth sensor; measures salinity, temperature, pressure; standard oceanographic instrument.

## D

**Dead Zone:** Hypoxic area (O₂ <2 mg/L) where most marine life cannot survive; 400+ globally; caused by nutrient pollution, warming.

**Demersal:** Bottom-dwelling fish (cod, flatfish); vs pelagic (open-water); slower-growing; more vulnerable to overfishing.

**Density:** Mass per unit volume; seawater 1020-1030 kg/m³; controlled by temperature (decreases density), salinity (increases), pressure (increases).

**Deoxygenation:** Ocean oxygen decline; 2% loss since 1960 (0-1000m); caused by warming (reduces solubility), stratification (reduces mixing).

**DWT (Deadweight Tonnage):** Ship's carrying capacity (cargo + fuel + crew + provisions); measures how much weight vessel can carry.

## E

**EEZ (Exclusive Economic Zone):** 0-200 nautical miles from coast; coastal state has rights to resources (fish, oil, minerals); 38% of ocean; 90%+ of fisheries.

**Ekman Transport:** Net water movement perpendicular to wind direction (due to Coriolis); drives coastal upwelling when wind blows parallel to coast.

**ENSO (El Niño Southern Oscillation):** Quasi-periodic climate pattern; oscillates every 2-7 years; major global weather driver; El Niño = warm eastern Pacific, suppressed upwelling.

**Epipelagic Zone:** Ocean surface 0-200m; sunlight; warm; where photosynthesis occurs; contains 90%+ of marine life.

**Eutrophication:** Excess nutrients (nitrogen, phosphorus) → algal blooms → decomposition consumes oxygen → dead zones.

## F

**Flag of Convenience:** Ship registered in country other than owner's for regulatory arbitrage (lower taxes, weaker labor/safety standards); Panama, Liberia largest registries.

**FPSO (Floating Production Storage Offloading):** Ship-shaped offshore oil platform; processes, stores oil; offloads to tankers; used in deep water (500-3,000m).

## G

**Gyre:** Large circular ocean current system; 5 major subtropical gyres; clockwise in Northern Hemisphere, counterclockwise in Southern; centers accumulate plastic.

## H

**Hadal Zone:** Ocean trenches 6,000-11,000m; deepest ocean; extreme pressure (1,100 atm); Mariana Trench; amphipods, snailfish, extremophiles.

**Halocline:** Layer of rapid salinity change with depth; creates density gradient; inhibits mixing.

**High Seas:** Ocean beyond EEZs (>200 nm from coast); no state sovereignty; 62% of ocean; governance weak; UNCLOS grants freedom of navigation, fishing, research.

**Hydrothermal Vents:** Seafloor hot springs; 400°C water; rich in minerals, sulfur; support chemosynthetic ecosystems (tube worms, clams); discovered 1977.

**Hypoxia:** Low oxygen (<2 mg/L); creates "dead zones"; fish/shrimp flee or die; 400+ coastal areas affected.

## I

**IMO (International Maritime Organization):** UN agency regulating international shipping; sets safety, environmental standards; 175 member states.

**ISA (International Seabed Authority):** UN body regulating deep-sea mining in "the Area" (seafloor beyond national jurisdiction); 168 members; headquartered in Jamaica.

**ITQ (Individual Transferable Quota):** Tradable fishing quota; fisher owns percentage of Total Allowable Catch; aligns incentives; controversial (concentrates wealth).

**IUU Fishing (Illegal, Unreported, Unregulated):** 20-30% of global catch; $10-23 billion/year; undermines management, depletes stocks.

## K

**Kelp Forest:** Temperate/cold-water ecosystem; giant kelp grows 45m tall, 60 cm/day; supports 1,000+ species; threatened by sea urchins, marine heatwaves.

**Keystone Species:** Species with disproportionate impact on ecosystem; sea otters control urchins → protect kelp; sharks control prey; removal → trophic cascades.

## M

**Manganese Nodules:** See Polymetallic Nodules.

**Mangrove:** Salt-tolerant coastal trees; tropical/subtropical; provide nursery habitat, coastal protection, carbon storage (3-5× terrestrial forests).

**Mesopelagic Zone:** Ocean depth 200-1,000m; twilight; temperature drops sharply (thermocline); oxygen minimum zone; lanternfish, squid.

**Methane Hydrates:** Ice-like solid; methane trapped in water molecules; found in continental slopes, permafrost; enormous energy potential but climate risk.

**MPA (Marine Protected Area):** Ocean zone with restricted human activity to conserve ecosystems; 8% of ocean in MPAs; <3% fully protected (no-take).

**MSY (Maximum Sustainable Yield):** Largest catch removable year-after-year without depleting stock; typically 30-50% of virgin biomass; theoretical ideal.

## O

**Ocean Acidification:** See Acidification (Ocean).

**Offshore Wind:** Wind turbines in ocean; 50+ GW globally; growing 20-30%/year; fixed-bottom (shallow) or floating (deep); stronger, steadier winds than onshore.

**OMZ (Oxygen Minimum Zone):** Mid-depth layer (200-1,000m) with low oxygen; formed by decomposition + poor ventilation; expanding with warming.

**OTEC (Ocean Thermal Energy Conversion):** Exploits temperature difference between warm surface, cold deep water to generate electricity; tropics only; pilot projects.

**Overfished:** Stock biomass below sustainable threshold; needs rebuilding; 34% of global stocks overfished.

**Overfishing:** Fishing mortality above sustainable level; biomass declining; leads to overfished status.

## P

**Panamax:** Ship size limited by old Panama Canal dimensions; ~5,000-6,000 TEU for container ships; 60,000-80,000 DWT for bulk carriers.

**Pelagic:** Relating to open water (not seafloor); pelagic organisms live in water column (fish, plankton); vs benthic.

**pH:** Measure of acidity; ocean currently 8.1 (slightly alkaline); was 8.2 pre-industrial; logarithmic scale (0.1 decrease = 30% more acidic).

**Photic Zone:** Surface layer where sunlight enables photosynthesis; 0-200m (euphotic); also "epipelagic"; 90%+ of marine life here.

**Phytoplankton:** Microscopic photosynthetic organisms (diatoms, dinoflagellates, cyanobacteria); base of marine food web; produce 50-80% of atmospheric oxygen.

**Polymetallic Nodules:** Potato-sized rocks on abyssal plains (4,000-6,000m); contain manganese, nickel, cobalt, copper; formed over millions of years; 21 billion tonnes in CCZ.

**Pycnocline:** Layer of rapid density change with depth; created by temperature/salinity gradients; inhibits mixing between surface and deep.

## R

**RFMO (Regional Fisheries Management Organization):** Manages fish stocks in high seas or straddling stocks; examples: ICCAT (tuna), CCAMLR (Antarctic); effectiveness varies.

**ROV (Remotely Operated Vehicle):** Tethered underwater robot; real-time control from surface; used for oil/gas, salvage, science; works to 6,000m depth.

## S

**Salinity:** Concentration of dissolved salts; average ~35 PSU (practical salinity units); affects density, freezing point, organism physiology.

**Seagrass Meadow:** Underwater flowering plants (not algae); coastal, shallow water; nursery habitat; carbon storage ("blue carbon"); 300,000 km² globally.

**Sea Level Rise:** Currently 3.4 mm/year (2006-2015), accelerating; 40% from thermal expansion, 60% from ice melt; 30-110 cm projected by 2100.

**Seafloor Massive Sulfides (SMS):** Mineral deposits at hydrothermal vents; high-grade copper, zinc, gold, silver; extraction threatens unique vent ecosystems.

**SOFAR Channel:** Sound Fixing and Ranging channel; depth ~1,000m where sound speed minimized; sound waves trapped, travel 1,000+ km; used by whales, submarines.

**Stratification:** Layering of water by density; warm/fresh surface, cold/salty deep; inhibits mixing; increasing with warming → nutrient depletion.

**Sverdrup (Sv):** Unit of ocean current transport; 1 Sv = 1 million m³/s; Antarctic Circumpolar Current = 130 Sv (100× all rivers combined).

## T

**TAC (Total Allowable Catch):** Annual catch limit for fish stock; set based on stock assessment; allocated via quotas to fishers/countries.

**TEU (Twenty-foot Equivalent Unit):** Standard shipping container size; 20 feet long; largest container ships carry 24,000 TEU.

**Thermohaline Circulation:** Global deep ocean circulation driven by density differences (temperature + salinity); ~1,000-year cycle; transports heat, carbon, nutrients.

**Thermocline:** Layer of rapid temperature decrease with depth; separates warm surface from cold deep; acts as barrier to mixing; typically 50-1,000m.

**Trophic Cascade:** Domino effect through food web; removing apex predator → prey explodes → overgrazes → ecosystem collapse.

## U

**UNCLOS (UN Convention on Law of the Sea):** "Constitution for oceans"; adopted 1982; established maritime zones (EEZ, high seas), dispute resolution; 168 parties (US not ratified).

**Upwelling:** Vertical movement of deep, cold, nutrient-rich water to surface; driven by wind or topography; supports 50% of global fish catch despite <1% of ocean area.

## V

**VLCC (Very Large Crude Carrier):** Tanker 200,000-320,000 DWT; carries crude oil; larger than Suezmax but smaller than ULCC.

## W

**Western Boundary Current:** Fast, narrow current on western side of ocean basin; Gulf Stream, Kuroshio; transport heat poleward; 2-5× faster than eastern boundary currents.

## Z

**Zooplankton:** Drifting animals (copepods, krill, jellyfish, larval fish); graze on phytoplankton; food for fish, whales; 90% energy loss per trophic level.

**Zooxanthellae:** Symbiotic algae living in coral tissue; provide energy via photosynthesis; expelled when coral stressed (bleaching).

## Units & Abbreviations

**atm:** Atmosphere (pressure unit); 1 atm = sea level pressure; increases ~1 atm per 10m depth

**°C:** Degrees Celsius

**cm:** Centimeter

**dB:** Decibel (sound intensity); whales ~180 dB, ship engines ~180-190 dB, seismic airguns ~250 dB

**DWT:** Deadweight tonnage (ship capacity)

**EJ:** Exajoule = 10¹⁸ joules

**GW:** Gigawatt = 10⁹ watts (power)

**J:** Joule (energy unit)

**kg:** Kilogram

**km:** Kilometer

**km²:** Square kilometer

**m:** Meter

**m³:** Cubic meter

**mg/L:** Milligrams per liter (concentration)

**mm:** Millimeter

**nm:** Nautical mile = 1.852 km

**PSU:** Practical salinity unit (dimensionless; ~g/kg)

**ppm:** Parts per million (concentration); CO₂ currently 420 ppm

**Sv:** Sverdrup (ocean current volume); 1 Sv = 10⁶ m³/s

**TEU:** Twenty-foot equivalent unit (container)

**PW:** Petawatt = 10¹⁵ watts (power)
