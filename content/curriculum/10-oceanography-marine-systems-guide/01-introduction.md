# Introduction: Oceanography & Marine Systems

## Overview

Oceans cover 71% of Earth's surface, contain 97% of its water, regulate global climate, produce half of atmospheric oxygen, feed 3+ billion people, and carry 80% of global trade by volume. Yet they remain less mapped than Mars. The "hydraulic operating system" of planetary habitability and human civilization.

**Core thesis:** The ocean isn't just blue space between continents — it's Earth's primary climate regulator, protein source for billions, transportation superhighway, and next resource frontier.

---

## Why This Matters

- **Climate Control:** Oceans absorb 90% of excess heat from global warming; drive weather patterns via evaporation, currents, heat redistribution
- **Food Security:** 3.3 billion rely on seafood for 20%+ of protein; wild capture fisheries ~90M tonnes/year; aquaculture ~130M tonnes/year
- **Trade:** 11 billion tonnes cargo shipped annually; 90% of global trade by volume; chokepoints (Suez, Malacca, Panama) = economic jugulars
- **Oxygen:** Phytoplankton produce 50-80% of atmospheric O₂; marine photosynthesis rivals terrestrial
- **Carbon Sink:** Oceans absorb ~25% of anthropogenic CO₂; slowing rate of atmospheric warming but causing acidification
- **Resources:** Deep-sea polymetallic nodules contain cobalt, nickel, copper, rare earths; offshore oil/gas = 30% of global production
- **Frontiers:** 80% of ocean unmapped at high resolution; more people have been to space than Mariana Trench

## Key Frameworks

### 1. Ocean Layers (By Light & Temperature)

| Zone | Depth | % Volume | Characteristics |
|---|---|---|---|
| Epipelagic (Sunlight) | 0-200m | 2% | Photosynthesis; warm; where most life concentrates |
| Mesopelagic (Twilight) | 200-1000m | 6% | Faint light; sharp temp drop (thermocline); oxygen minimum zone |
| Bathypelagic (Midnight) | 1000-4000m | 75% | No light; cold (~4°C); sparse life; anglerfish territory |
| Abyssopelagic (Abyss) | 4000-6000m | 16% | Near-freezing; immense pressure; slow-moving currents |
| Hadopelagic (Trenches) | 6000-11,000m | <1% | Deepest ocean; extreme pressure (1000+ atm); chemosynthetic ecosystems |

**Key insight:** 98% of ocean volume is dark, cold, and under extreme pressure. Habitable surface layer is thin skin.

### 2. Thermohaline Circulation ("Global Conveyor Belt")

- **Mechanism:** Dense, cold, salty water sinks at poles → drives deep ocean currents → resurfaces in tropics via upwelling → completes loop
- **Timescale:** ~1000 years for water parcel to complete circuit
- **Climate Impact:** Transports heat from tropics to poles; regulates regional climates; AMOC slowdown could crash European temps
- **Carbon Pump:** Sinking water carries dissolved CO₂ to deep ocean (centuries-millennia storage)
- **Nutrient Cycling:** Upwelling brings nutrients from deep → fuels surface productivity → supports fisheries

### 3. Ocean-Atmosphere Coupling

| Phenomenon | Mechanism | Impact |
|---|---|---|
| El Niño Southern Oscillation (ENSO) | Weakened trade winds → warm water sloshes east across Pacific | Global weather disruption; droughts, floods, coral bleaching |
| Hurricanes | Warm ocean (>26.5°C) evaporates → moisture fuels storm intensification | Coastal destruction; intensifying with warming |
| Sea Ice Albedo Feedback | Ice reflects sunlight (albedo ~0.5-0.7); open water absorbs (albedo ~0.06) | Arctic amplification; accelerated regional warming |

### 4. Exclusive Economic Zones (EEZ)

- **Definition:** 200 nautical miles from coastline; coastal state has rights to resources
- **Coverage:** EEZs = 38% of ocean surface; contains 90%+ of fisheries, offshore oil/gas
- **Largest EEZs:** USA (11.4M km²), France (10M km²), Australia (8.1M km²) — small island territories matter
- **High Seas:** 62% of ocean beyond EEZs; governed by UN Convention on Law of the Sea (UNCLOS)
- **Geopolitics:** Overlapping claims (South China Sea); ice retreat opens Arctic shipping/resources

### 5. Marine Trophic Levels

```
Sunlight → Phytoplankton (primary producers)
       → Zooplankton (primary consumers)
       → Small fish (secondary consumers)
       → Large fish (tertiary consumers)
       → Apex predators
```

**Energy loss:** ~90% lost per trophic level → only 10% passed up
**Efficiency:** Eating lower on chain (anchovies vs tuna) more efficient
**Overfishing:** Removing apex predators destabilizes ecosystems (trophic cascades)

## Key Technologies

**Observation:**
- **Argo floats:** 4,000+ autonomous profiling floats; measure temp, salinity, currents
- **Satellite altimetry:** Sea surface height → ocean currents, heat content, sea level rise
- **AUVs/ROVs:** Autonomous/remotely operated vehicles; mapping, sampling, inspection
- **Seismic surveys:** Sound waves map subsurface geology → oil/gas, tectonic structure

**Resource Extraction:**
- **Offshore platforms:** Fixed (shallow), jack-up (mobile), semi-submersible (deep), FPSO (floating production)
- **Subsea mining:** Polymetallic nodules, seafloor massive sulfides, cobalt crusts — in development
- **Fishing:** Bottom trawling (destructive), purse seining (efficient for schools), longlines (bycatch issues)

**Transport:**
- **Container ships:** 400m long, 24,000 TEU capacity; economies of scale drive gigantism
- **Bulk carriers:** Iron ore, coal, grain; Capesize vessels (>100,000 DWT) too large for Panama/Suez
- **Tankers:** Crude oil, LNG, chemicals; VLCC = 300,000+ DWT

## Connections to Other Domains

**Climate:** Ocean heat capacity >> atmosphere; 90% of warming energy absorbed by oceans. ENSO, monsoons, hurricanes all ocean-driven. Slowing AMOC = Europe climate risk. Methane hydrates in seabed = potential feedback loop.

**Geopolitics:** Chokepoints = strategic vulnerabilities (Hormuz = 21% global oil, Malacca = 25% trade). South China Sea disputes over resources/transit. Arctic opening with ice retreat (Northwest Passage, resource access). China's "string of pearls" port strategy. Naval power projection depends on ocean control.

**Economics:** Maritime trade underpins globalization. Shipping = 3% of GHG emissions. Fisheries = livelihood for 60M people. Offshore oil/gas critical to energy transition timing. Coastal real estate highly vulnerable to sea level rise. Tourism economies depend on coral reefs (dying).

**Ecology:** Ocean primary productivity supports terrestrial life via atmospheric O₂. Nutrient upwelling = richest fishing grounds. Coral reefs = 25% of marine species but <0.1% of ocean area. Ocean acidification threatens shellfish, corals (carbonate skeleton formation). Warming → coral bleaching, range shifts, invasive species.
